# Outkast — Aquemini

**Release Date:** 1998-09-29  
**Label:** LaFace Records / Arista Records  
**Producers:** Outkast, Organized Noize  
**Recorded:** Bosstown Recording Studios and Doppler Studios, Atlanta, Georgia  
**Wikipedia:** [Aquemini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquemini)

## Overview

Aquemini is the third studio album by the American hip hop duo Outkast, released on September 29, 1998. The title is a portmanteau of the two performers' Zodiac signs: Aquarius (Big Boi) and Gemini (André 3000), indicative of the album's recurring theme of the differing personalities of the two members.

Released as the follow-up to the duo's commercially successful 1996 album *ATLiens*, Aquemini expands on the previous record's outer space-inspired compositions by incorporating live instrumentation and drawing on 1970s funk, southern soul, gospel, country, psychedelic rock, and other influences. Featured guest artists include Raekwon, George Clinton, and Erykah Badu. Lyrically, Aquemini explores individuality, human nature, addiction, self-inflicted struggles, technology, and interpersonal relationships.

Aquemini received widespread critical acclaim. It peaked at No. 2 on both the Billboard 200 and the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart. The album was certified platinum in November 1998, only two months after its release, and was certified double platinum on July 2, 1999. Since its initial release, Aquemini has gone on to be considered one of the greatest hip hop albums ever made. In a 2020 updated list, Rolling Stone ranked it number 49 on its list of the 500 Greatest Albums of All Time.
