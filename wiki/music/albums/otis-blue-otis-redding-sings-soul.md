# Otis Redding — Otis Blue/Otis Redding Sings Soul

**Release Date:** 1965-09-15  
**Wikipedia:** [Otis Blue/Otis Redding Sings Soul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otis_Blue/Otis_Redding_Sings_Soul)

## Overview

Otis Blue/Otis Redding Sings Soul (often referred to simply as Otis Blue) is the third studio album by the American soul singer and songwriter Otis Redding. It was first released on September 15, 1965, as an LP record through the Stax Records subsidiary label Volt.
Otis Blue is composed mainly of cover versions of contemporary R&B hits, exploring themes from the blues and love ballads, among others. Three of the LP's eleven songs were written by Redding, and three others were written by fellow soul singer Sam Cooke, who had died several months before the album was made. Except for one track, Otis Blue was recorded in less than 24 hours (22 studio hours in two sessions which spanned 28 hours from July 9 to 10, 1965), at the Stax recording studio in Memphis, Tennessee.
