# Public Enemy — Fear of a Black Planet

**Release Date:** 1990-04-10  
**Label:** Def Jam Recordings / Columbia Records  
**Producers:** The Bomb Squad  
**Recorded:** 1989–1990  
**Wikipedia:** [Fear of a Black Planet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fear_of_a_Black_Planet)

## Overview

Fear of a Black Planet is the third studio album by American hip hop group Public Enemy. It was released on April 10, 1990, by Def Jam Recordings and Columbia Records, and produced by the group's production team The Bomb Squad. Having fulfilled their initial creative ambitions with *It Takes a Nation of Millions to Hold Us Back* (1988), the group aspired to create what lead rapper Chuck D called "a deep, complex album".

Reflecting its confrontational tone, Fear of a Black Planet features elaborate sound collages that incorporate varying rhythms, numerous samples, media sound bites, and eccentric loops. Thematically, it explores organization and empowerment within the black community, social issues affecting African Americans, and race relations.

A commercial and critical hit, Fear of a Black Planet sold two million copies in the United States. Its success contributed significantly to the popularity of Afrocentric and political subject matter in hip hop. In 2004, the Library of Congress added it to the National Recording Registry. In 2020, it was ranked number 176 on Rolling Stone's list of the 500 greatest albums of all time.
