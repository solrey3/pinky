## Wisdom / Themes

# SUMMARY
Kanye West presents his sixth studio album Yeezus, a 2013 experimental hip-hop record exploring fame, power, and minimalism through aggressive production.

# IDEAS
- Yeezus marks Kanye's shift toward abrasive industrial sounds.
- The album rejects commercial polish for raw artistic expression.
- Minimalist production highlights sparse beats and heavy sampling.
- Themes critique celebrity culture and racial dynamics boldly.
- Kanye challenges listeners with unconventional song structures throughout.
- Release strategy involved surprise drops and limited promotion tactics.
- Artwork features simple red text on white background design.
- Collaborations include Daft Punk, Chief Keef, and King Louie.
- Tracks like "Black Skinhead" deliver intense political commentary.
- Yeezus influenced later trap and experimental rap movements significantly.
- Kanye uses fashion references to expand artistic identity.
- The album's brevity emphasizes impact over filler material.
- Controversy surrounded lyrics and public persona during rollout.
- Production drew from industrial, punk, and electronic genres freely.
- Yeezus positioned Kanye as boundary-pushing innovator in hip-hop.
