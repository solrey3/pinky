# Metafiction

Fiction that self-consciously addresses the devices of fiction, exposing its own artificiality. It collapses the boundary between the story world and the "real" world, often by having characters acknowledge they are fictional or by drawing attention to the act of writing/reading.

## Definition

Metafiction operates by making the medium visible. Instead of immersing the reader in a seamless narrative world, it repeatedly jolts awareness back to the constructed nature of the text. Cervantes used a fictional Moorish historian (Cide Hamete Benengeli) as the supposed author of *Don Quixote*; Part Two then has characters who have read Part One, creating a recursive textual loop.
