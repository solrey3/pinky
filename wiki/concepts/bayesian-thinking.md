# Bayesian Thinking

A framework for reasoning under uncertainty that treats beliefs as probabilities and updates them incrementally as new evidence arrives. Named after Thomas Bayes, the approach contrasts with frequentist statistics by incorporating prior knowledge. The core formula: new belief = (prior belief × likelihood of evidence) / total probability of evidence.

## Notes

Bayesian thinking is the connective tissue between forecasting, poker, and sports analytics. All three domains require the same discipline: establish a baseline belief, observe new evidence, update the belief proportionally, and resist the temptation to overreact to single data points (results-oriented thinking). The poker player's "read" on an opponent is a Bayesian update; the baseball GM's evaluation of a prospect is a Bayesian update; the weather forecaster's revised prediction is a Bayesian update. What unifies them is humility about uncertainty and systematicity about revision.
