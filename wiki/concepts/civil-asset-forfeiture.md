# Civil Asset Forfeiture

Civil asset forfeiture is the government practice of seizing property allegedly connected to wrongdoing, often before or without a completed criminal conviction. The core governance problem is proportionality and due process: the state can turn a minor allegation into a major property deprivation if seizure, storage, litigation cost, or delay become punishment by process.

## Notes

Track this separately from ordinary takings doctrine. Takings usually asks what compensation follows a public-use seizure; civil forfeiture asks whether law-enforcement process itself can deprive owners of property disproportionate to the alleged offense.
