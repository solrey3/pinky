# Chivalry and Satire

The use of chivalric romance tropes as a vehicle for social and literary satire. Cervantes targeted the exaggerated, formulaic knightly romances popular in 16th-century Spain, but his satire transcended its target to become a broader meditation on human aspiration and delusion.

## Definition

Chivalric romances were a medieval and early modern literary genre featuring knights, quests, damsels in distress, and supernatural adventures. By Cervantes' time, the genre had become formulaic and clichéd. *Don Quixote* was explicitly written to satirize these works — yet the affection for the form is palpable beneath the mockery. Mark Twain observed that the book ultimately "swept the world's admiration for the mediaeval chivalry-silliness out of existence," but others argue it dignified the impulse even as it mocked the execution.
