# Quantum Error Correction

The set of techniques used to protect fragile quantum information from noise, decoherence, and operational errors. Useful quantum error correction is a prerequisite for moving quantum computing from laboratory demonstrations toward reliable, scalable computation.

- [2026-06-17: Evening Brief — Wednesday, June 17, 2026](../sources/newsletter-2026-06-17-evening.md) — Microsoft, Atom Computing, and EeroQ are reported as promising useful quantum error correction by 2028, giving the wiki a concrete timeline claim to track.

## Notes

The key phrase is “useful,” not merely “demonstrated.” Quantum computing has long had impressive lab milestones that did not translate into reliable production workloads. A 2028 error-correction promise should be tracked as an infrastructure claim with a date, because the value of the claim depends on whether it produces usable, repeatable, fault-tolerant computation rather than another narrow benchmark.
