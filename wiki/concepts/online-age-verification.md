# Online Age Verification

Online age verification is the policy and technical problem of determining whether a user is old enough to access an internet service or content category. It sits between child-safety policy, privacy, anonymity, platform liability, and identity infrastructure.

## Notes

The operational concern is tradeoff design. Age checks can be promoted as child-protection infrastructure, but they also risk normalizing identity disclosure, reducing anonymous access, creating sensitive data stores, and making platform access depend on third-party verification systems. Privacy-preserving proofs, data minimization, retention limits, auditability, and clear appeal paths matter as much as the policy goal.
