# Score Effects

The tendency for NBA teams to play less efficiently when holding large leads and more efficiently when trailing by significant margins. In a tied game, a team behind by 20 points is expected to score ~6 points per 100 possessions more than it would otherwise — equivalent to replacing an average player with LeBron James.

Because good teams spend more time with large leads, raw stats underrate them and overrate bad teams. [[RAPTOR]] adjusts for score effects by evaluating the margin in an average possession (not final score) and recognizes that effects grow larger in later quarters and are smaller in the playoffs.
