# Idealism vs Realism

The fundamental dialectical tension between seeing the world as it *could be* (idealism) versus seeing the world as it *is* (realism). This opposition is one of the oldest in Western thought and finds perhaps its most vivid literary embodiment in the characters of Don Quixote and Sancho Panza.

## Definition

**Idealism** is the conviction that reality is mentally constructed or shaped by beliefs, values, and aspirations. **Realism** is the commitment to grounding action in observable, material facts. Neither position is sufficient alone: pure idealism risks delusion and harm, while pure realism risks cynicism and passivity. Wisdom, per the *Don Quixote* reading, resides in synthesizing the two.
