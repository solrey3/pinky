# Permanent Daylight Saving Time

Permanent daylight saving time is the policy proposal to keep clocks on daylight saving time year-round rather than switching seasonally between standard time and daylight time. In the wiki, it belongs to the public-policy graph as a small but concrete example of federal rules changing daily infrastructure: schedules, commutes, broadcast calendars, school mornings, health routines, and interstate coordination.

## Notes

The governance lesson is procedural more than ideological: clock policy is mundane infrastructure. A successful change depends less on the headline vote than on federal-state coordination, implementation calendars, and clear communication to institutions that rely on time standards.
