# Advantage Play

The practice of using skill, analysis, or information asymmetry to gain a mathematical edge over the house or other players in games of chance. Advantage play includes card counting, GTO poker, sports betting with superior models, and any scenario where disciplined analysis overcomes randomness. It requires emotional control, bankroll management, and the discipline to treat results as noise and process as signal.

## Notes

Advantage play is the practical application of Bayesian thinking in adversarial environments. All three sources share the same structure: identify a mathematical edge, manage variance through bankroll discipline, and execute without emotional interference. The MIT team's downfall — ego and greed — is the same failure mode that destroys poker players who abandon GTO for hero plays. This connects to [[Moneyball]]: both are forms of information arbitrage, exploiting institutional blindness to mathematical reality.
