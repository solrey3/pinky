# Statistical Privacy

Statistical privacy is the practice of publishing useful aggregate data while reducing the risk that individuals or small groups can be re-identified from those releases. Techniques such as noise infusion, suppression, aggregation, and differential privacy protect confidentiality, but they also change the public data that downstream users analyze.

## Notes

The June 2026 newsletter thread matters because statistical privacy sits between two legitimate needs. The public needs accurate, comparable statistics for policy and analysis; individuals need protection from disclosure risks created by increasingly powerful data linkage. The governance problem is not simply whether to add noise, but how transparent the agency is about changes, uncertainty, and downstream interpretability.
