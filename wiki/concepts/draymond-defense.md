# DRAYMOND

DRAYMOND (Defensive Rating Accounting for Yielding Minimal Openness by Nearest Defender) was FiveThirtyEight's predecessor to [[RAPTOR]] for measuring on-ball defense. It used opponent shooting statistics from player tracking data. It was a first step toward incorporating tracking data into projections but had two major limitations: it over-weighted shots opponents *made* (noise) and treated all shot types equally.

RAPTOR's defensive component superseded DRAYMOND by weighting opponent shooting more carefully: missed 2-pointers defended are worth +1.05, made 2-pointers -0.33, and 3-point attempts (made or missed) +0.17.
