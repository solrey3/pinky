# Totalitarianism

A political system in which the state holds total authority over every aspect of public and private life — including thought, language, and memory. Unlike authoritarianism, which controls behavior, totalitarianism seeks to colonize the inner life itself, making dissent literally unthinkable.

## Notes

Orwell's totalitarianism and Revelation's "Babylon the Great" both identify the same mechanism: power that seduces through language and spectacle while demanding total submission. The difference is that Orwell offers a secular, psychological analysis while Revelation frames it theologically. Both texts warn that comfortable indifference (lukewarmness, in Revelation's terms; the proles' apathy, in Orwell's) enables oppression more effectively than active resistance.
