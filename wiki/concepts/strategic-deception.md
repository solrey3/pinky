# Strategic Deception

The deliberate misleading of opponents about one's true intentions, capabilities, or plans in order to gain advantage. Strategic deception operates at every scale — from battlefield tactics to political maneuvering to card games — and rests on the principle that information asymmetry is the most powerful weapon in competitive environments.

## Notes

Strategic deception spans this corpus from Sun Tzu's military theory to Sklansky's poker strategy to Ellison's racial survival tactics. The difference is intent: Sun Tzu and Machiavelli deceive to win; Sklansky's poker deception is bounded by game rules; Ellison's "subversive compliance" is survival within an oppressive system. The common thread is that direct confrontation is rarely optimal — whether in war, politics, poker, or resistance.
