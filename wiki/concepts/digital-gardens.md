# Digital Gardens

A metaphor and practice for maintaining a personal knowledge space on the web as a living, evolving collection — contrasted with the "stream" model of chronological social media posts. A digital garden is **topographically organized** (by topic and link) rather than chronologically organized (by date).

## Core Characteristics

- **Imperfect by design.** Pages are published in early drafts and revised over time. There is no "publish" vs. "draft" dichotomy.
- **Interlinked.** The value is in the connections between ideas, not in individual posts.
- **Owned.** Typically on a personal domain, not a platform.
- **Non-linear.** Readers navigate by interest, not by recency.
- **Long-evolving.** Pages grow and change over months or years.

## Relation to Our 2nd Brain System

Our wiki is conceptually a digital garden that is **LLM-maintained** rather than human-maintained. The "gardening" (linking, pruning, cross-referencing, updating) is done by the LLM during ingestion. The human is the gardener in the sense of choosing what to plant (what sources to add) and what to explore (what questions to ask).
