# Game Theory Optimal (GTO)

A strategy in competitive games that is unexploitable — no opponent can reduce your expected win rate below a guaranteed minimum by changing their own strategy. GTO strategies are derived from Nash equilibrium calculations and, in modern poker, computed by AI solvers that play billions of hands against themselves. GTO provides a safe baseline from which to deviate when opponents make predictable errors.

- [2026-05-10: The Theory of Poker — Wisdom Extracted](sources/the-theory-of-poker.md) — Sklansky's Fundamental Theorem: "Every time you play a hand differently from how you would play it with perfect information, your opponents gain"

## Notes

GTO thinking extends beyond poker. In any adversarial environment — negotiations, markets, military strategy — there exists an unexploitable baseline strategy. The insight from Modern Poker Theory that "human intuition about correct strategy was systematically wrong" mirrors Moneyball's finding about baseball scouts and The Signal and the Noise's finding about pundits. The paradox of GTO — that optimal play involves deliberate randomization — is one of the most beautiful counterintuitive results in applied mathematics.
