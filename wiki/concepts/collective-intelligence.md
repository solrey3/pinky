# Collective Intelligence

The emergent capacity of properly structured groups to solve problems, make predictions, and generate insights that exceed the capabilities of any individual member. Collective intelligence requires four conditions: diversity of opinion, independence of thought, decentralization of knowledge, and a mechanism for aggregation (averaging, markets, voting).

## Notes

Collective intelligence is fragile. The same mechanisms that make crowds smart can make them stupid when independence is compromised. Baseball's scouting consensus was a *dumb* crowd because everyone shared the same biases. The stock market is a *smart* crowd when diverse independent actors aggregate information, but becomes a dumb crowd during bubbles when herding destroys independence. Surowiecki's framework thus explains both Moneyball's opportunity (a broken aggregation system) and Silver's finding that properly structured groups beat experts.
