## Synthesis Notes

This evening dispatch strengthens the morning's market interpretation: the June 8 selloff is not a broad liquidation but a selective repricing. AI/growth names are being punished, while dividend/value/real-estate/consumer-adjacent names continue to catch bids. That pattern matters because it reads like a quality and duration rotation, not a pure liquidity crisis.

The Apple/Google story and the Microsoft supply-chain story point in opposite directions for AI adoption. On the product side, even Apple appears willing to route part of its AI architecture through another frontier-model provider. On the infrastructure side, the development workflows used to build and operate AI systems are becoming attack surfaces themselves. The more AI coding agents become normal developer infrastructure, the more package registries, action repositories, and generated-code pipelines become security-critical.

The prediction-market note is also useful precisely because it is weak. A blocked Kalshi extraction and fuzzy Polymarket chatter are not absence of market information; they are provenance warnings. In this wiki, those warnings should travel with the source so later synthesis does not over-trust a scraped number divorced from its collection quality.
