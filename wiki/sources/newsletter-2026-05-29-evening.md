## Synthesis Notes

The evening of May 29 is the capstone of one of the more remarkable market weeks of the cycle. Every major US equity index is within striking distance of its all-time high simultaneously — the last time all four major indices were this synchronized near peaks was the runup to the 2024 correction. The engine is the Iran deal overhang: oil falling nearly 7% in a single session is a downstream inflation signal, and markets are pricing it as a soft-landing accelerant rather than a demand-collapse signal.

The Fonseca-Djokovic result is the sports story of the day, arguably the week. Djokovic at Roland Garros is the most statistically dominant player-tournament combination in modern tennis — he had won the title four times. A 19-year-old Brazilian qualifier coming back from two sets down to take the fifth in a final-set tiebreak equivalent (7-5, 7-5) is not a fluke; it is a regime-change moment.
