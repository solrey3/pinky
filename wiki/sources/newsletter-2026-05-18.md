## Summary

A heavy news day. Three people killed at the Islamic Center of San Diego — two suspects found dead on scene, motive unclear. The WHO declared Ebola a global health emergency as the DRC/Uganda outbreak widened and an American became infected. Trump announced he was canceling a planned Iran strike (the same day Breaking Points was reporting Iran expected war within 48 hours), keeping the Middle East on a knife's edge. Elon Musk lost his lawsuit against Sam Altman and OpenAI — the jury ruled unanimously he took too long to file. The administration's TrumpRx initiative added 600+ generic drugs with Mark Cuban at the rollout. And a familiar Reason/libertarian theme played out with two long pieces on the Trump-Massie split and the death of the Tea Party.

In markets, Russell 2000 took the hardest hit of the day (-3.33%), AMD dropped 8.24%, and TSLA fell 7.87% — a much uglier session for the "edges" of the market than the headline indices suggest. SCHW was the standout gainer (+5.19%). The 30-year Treasury touched its 52-week high at 5.154%. Oil held above $100. Bitcoin fell 2.59%.

Mets were live in DC in the evening, up 5-4 in the 7th inning. NBA had Spurs at Thunder (Q1). NHL had Canadiens at Sabres.

## Synthesis Notes

May 18 accelerates the pattern established in the May 13–17 window: the "edges" of the market (small-caps, high-beta tech) are experiencing significantly more volatility than headline indices suggest. AMD lost ~8% for the second time in four trading days. The Russell is down ~100 points from its May 14 high. Meanwhile, NVDA is holding its gains and SCHW spiked on unclear catalyst — possibly rate expectations shifting. The 30-year Treasury at its 52-week high is the macro constraint that may finally force a broader reckoning.

The Musk/OpenAI loss is a clean ending to one of tech's longest-running feuds. The jury didn't rule on the merits — just timing. The underlying question of whether Altman and OpenAI misappropriated a charitable mission remains unanswered legally.
