## Synthesis Notes

This source strengthens the selective-risk-off synthesis from June 24. The Nasdaq and S&P remain red, oil and Bitcoin confirm caution, and several mega-cap growth names are weak. But the Russell 2000, AMD, and TSM remain resilient, so the market is still sorting exposures rather than selling everything. Treat the label as rotation/split tape, not panic.

The most durable tech update is that AI infrastructure is becoming more vertically integrated and more physically constrained at the same time. OpenAI/Broadcom custom silicon is about lowering inference cost and securing capacity; Nvidia cooling is about heat and water; TSM and AMD's market resilience shows investors still separate specific chip/foundry exposure from broad Nasdaq weakness.

The Anthropic/Alibaba story adds a new security axis. Model safety is usually discussed as output refusal, user identity, or access control, but capability extraction is about whether a model's behavior can be copied, distilled, or benchmarked away through illicit probing. That belongs in the same operational graph as API monitoring, rate limits, evaluation leakage, model-watermarking/provenance, and vendor trust.
