The rotation from speculative risk to predictable earnings that was visible in the split-tape data of prior dispatches is no longer a thesis — it's a confirmed trend. The 90-day T-bill at a 52-week low while the 30-year Treasury knocks on 5% is the rates picture underlying all of it: short end pricing in Fed cuts (buy duration, rate-sensitive stocks), long end pricing in fiscal chaos (sell bonds, sell growth).

The **pg_durable open-source from Microsoft** is quietly significant. Durable execution (workflows that survive crashes, restarts, and network partitions) has been an infrastructure primitive that required separate services — Temporal, Apache Conductor, Amazon SWF. Baking it into Postgres as a native extension changes the deployment calculus for any team already on Postgres. The fact that Microsoft is open-sourcing it (rather than productizing through Azure) signals a strategy of winning the developer trust layer first.
