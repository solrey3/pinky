# NBA Rankings — Personal All-Time Player Pyramid

**Capture Date:** 2026-05-25  
**Source:** [raw/journal/nba-rankings.md](../raw/journal/nba-rankings.md)

## Summary

Personal all-time NBA player rankings organized as a tiered pyramid with five primary tiers plus two additional "Level" groupings and a "Too Old School For Me" category. 113 players ranked total. The pyramid reflects a personal basketball canon, not an objective statistical ranking — acknowledges era bias ("Too Old School For Me" is a candid admission).

## Key Observations

- **Jordan #1, LeBron #2** — the canonical mainstream GOAT ordering with LeBron as the only real challenger
- **Wilt at #9 in Tier 3, below Russell (#4) in Tier 2** — a clear valuation of winning and team impact over raw statistical dominance
- **Steph Curry at #6 (Tier 2)** — high placement reflects recognition of the 3-point revolution and dynasty-era dominance
- **Shai Gilgeous-Alexander in Level 2** — contemporary player already earning historical respect; positioned behind Paul Pierce
- **Victor Wembanyama added in Level 2** — placed behind Jason Kidd as an early-career historical projection
- **"Too Old School For Me" category** — a rare honest admission of era bias in personal rankings; respects reputation without false enthusiasm

## Synthesis Notes

Compared with [[the-book-of-basketball]] (Bill Simmons' pyramid), this canon shares Jordan at #1 and similar tier structures but has notable differences: Curry higher (reflects post-Simmons era), Jokic + Giannis + SGA + Wembanyama included (modern players), "Too Old School" category is a candid departure from the usually-reverent treatment of early NBA legends.

The [[raptor]] and [[plus-minus-statistics]] analytics concepts from the FiveThirtyEight ingest inform how one might think about quantifying these rankings, but this list is explicitly personal — intuitive over algorithmic.
