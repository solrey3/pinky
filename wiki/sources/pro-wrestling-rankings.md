## Key Observations

- **Austin #1** — the Monday Night Wars era and the "Attitude Era" as the peak of pro wrestling as mainstream entertainment
- **Flair #2** — the archetypal heel/legend; WOOOOO factor
- **Danielson #7** — AEW's indie-to-mainstream pipeline; highest current-era active worker
- **The Rock and Undertaker in top 6** — two pillars of the Attitude/Ruthless Aggression eras
- **Modern AEW/independent talent well-represented** — Jericho (#11), Moxley (#18), Hangman (#19), MJF (#21), Kenny Omega (#23), Will Ospreay (#32), Claudio Castagnoli (#56), Kyle Fletcher (#63)
- **NJPW representation:** Okada (#35), Nakamura (#37), Takeshita (#53)
- **Chris Benoit (#24)** — included in the historical canon despite personal controversy; the list doesn't editorialize
- **Hulk Hogan #50** — notably low for cultural impact; reflects a personal assessment of in-ring ability vs. cultural footprint

## Synthesis Notes

The wrestling canon closely mirrors the music and movie canons in structure — personal, unapologetic, covering multiple eras and promotions without false reverence for name value. Bryan Danielson at #7 is the most telling placement: it signals a valuation of technical wrestling craft (his ring work, submission wrestling, storytelling) above the mainstream popularity ceiling reached by bigger names. Compare with the NBA pyramid: the same principle applies — Jordan #1 for performance, not just cultural weight.
