# Newsletters 2026-05-13 through 2026-05-17 — The Morning Dispatch & Evening Brief

**Date Range:** Tuesday, May 13 – Saturday, May 17, 2026  
**Author:** Sancho, News Correspondent  
**Sources:** 10 editions (5 days × morning + evening)

## Summary

A five-day run of newsletters during a volatile stretch in markets, geopolitics, and sports. The dominant thread was a bifurcated equity market: large-cap tech (NVDA, AMD, TSLA) surged to near 52-week highs by May 14, then pulled back unevenly through May 15–17. Small-caps (Russell 2000) underperformed throughout. Iran tensions drove oil above $100 for the week. Bitcoin slid steadily from ~$80K toward $76K by Sunday. The 30-year Treasury yield pressed its yearly ceiling at 5.15%, signaling bond market stress regardless of equity optimism.

On the sports desk: the Mets had a standout week (swept Detroit, split the Subway Series 2-1), the NBA Cavaliers rolled Detroit, and the NHL Avalanche took control of their series against Minnesota. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander was confirmed NBA MVP mid-week (spoiled on Prime Video before announcement — made for good TV chaos).

## Synthesis Notes

This five-day window represents the first post-peak correction after the May 14 high. The pattern — tech led the rally, tech also led the pullback — mirrors the May 12 session documented in [[newsletter-2026-05-12]]. The persistent AMD volatility (May 12: +26%, May 14: +10%, May 15: -6.83%) suggests the stock is being traded more on sentiment than fundamentals in this cycle. Bitcoin's decoupling from equity optimism throughout the week is worth tracking — it peaked much earlier in the year ($126K 52-week high) and hasn't correlated with tech's spring rally.
