The evening of June 1 represents a structural inflection in the AI era's capital formation story. **Anthropic filing its S-1** is not just a company milestone — it's the signal that the AI lab phase (private, speculative, runway-funded) is transitioning to the public markets phase. Paired with Alphabet's $80B raise and the continued AMD/NVDA/TSM repricing, the market is pricing in a multi-year infrastructure build-out that dwarfs anything in the cloud era. The numbers are startling: Alphabet raises $80B in a single equity action on the same day Anthropic moves toward a public offering. This is what capital concentration at a technology inflection looks like.

**Florida v. OpenAI** matters beyond the specific case. The legal theory — that a commercial AI product bears liability for criminal acts its outputs allegedly enabled — is untested US law. Whether or not the case survives motions to dismiss, it establishes the legal vocabulary, the jurisdiction, and the template for every state AG who wants to pursue this line of action. Cross-reference `concepts/ai-augmented-development.md` and the Illinois AI regulation law (documented in the May 28 evening dispatch) for the legislative-versus-litigation race.

The **Meta AI Instagram exploit** is technically simple but architecturally damning: the attack vector was not a network vulnerability or a credential leak — it was a social engineering request to the AI itself. The model was the attack surface. This is a categorically different security problem than anything the OWASP or traditional security frameworks have modeled. The model doing exactly what it was trained to do (be helpful and responsive to requests) became the mechanism of account compromise. Cross-reference `concepts/security-and-privacy-toolkit.md`.

The Nasdaq at 27,087, 103 points from its all-time high, is almost entirely explained by this sector.
