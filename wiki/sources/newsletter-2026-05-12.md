## Synthesis Notes

This day illustrates a market paradox: the same traders who bid NVDA up 12% are the ones pricing Warsh as a hawk. Either the market believes rates don't matter for AI earnings, or it's pricing a soft landing where the Fed cuts despite inflation. The two narratives are in tension — one will break.

The FDA resignation is a signal about institutional erosion: a public health agency head quits because political pressure overrides scientific process. The e-cigarette story is small-bore but symptomatic of a larger pattern where regulatory independence is weakening across agencies.

The Instructure ransom and TanStack npm attack are sibling stories about supply-chain fragility — one in education infrastructure, one in JavaScript dependencies. Both suggest that critical systems remain under-defended and that the cost of security is being externalized onto users.
