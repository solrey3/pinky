# Jake 'The Snake' Roberts

#47.

**Real Name:** Aurelian Smith Jr.
**Born:** 1955, Gainesville TX
**Nationality:** American
**Active:** 1975–2013
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Personal Rank:** #47 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#47. The greatest psychological heel in wrestling history. Roberts introduced the concept that the scariest thing in wrestling was not violence but inevitability — that the DDT could come at any moment, that Damien the snake was always nearby, that Jake was smarter than everyone in the building. His promos operated on a genuinely different intellectual level from his contemporaries.

## Highlights

- WWF mid-card legend; never held the world title
- The DDT is wrestling's most dangerous-looking finisher and was genuinely dangerous when first deployed
- His feud with Rick Rude (1990) and his match with Ted DiBiase (Saturday Night's Main Event) are underrated classics
- His personal struggles with addiction are documented in 'Beyond the Mat' (1999) — one of wrestling's most honest documentary moments
- His 'resurrection' story (working with DDP Yoga, returning for AEW) is one of wrestling's best late-career narratives
