# Reggie Miller

The greatest clutch three-point shooter of the pre-Curry era.

**Born:** 1965, Riverside CA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** SG
**Active:** 1987–2005
**Personal NBA Tier:** Level 1

## Summary

The greatest clutch three-point shooter of the pre-Curry era. 18 years with one franchise (Indiana Pacers). The 8 points in 8.9 seconds against the Knicks (1995 playoffs) is the most concentrated single scoring burst in playoff history. His rivalry with Spike Lee at Madison Square Garden is basketball's most entertaining celebrity-player feud.

## Highlights

- 8 All-Star selections; all-time three-point leader when he retired
- His 8 points in 8.9 seconds against New York (1995 playoffs) is the most dramatic late-game burst in NBA history
- 18 seasons with the Indiana Pacers — the most loyalty to a single franchise in his era
- His rivalry with Spike Lee became the defining Madison Square Garden narrative of the 1990s
- His two Finals appearances (2000) with Indiana as the primary star are his most complete team achievements
