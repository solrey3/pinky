# Adaptation

**Director:** Spike Jonze
**Genre:** Comedy-drama, Meta-fiction
**Runtime:** 114 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Charlie Kaufman (Nicolas Cage) is hired to adapt Susan Orlean's *The Orchid Thief* and cannot. The film becomes about his inability to write the film, his twin brother Donald (also Cage) who writes formulaic screenplays, Susan Orlean (Meryl Streep) and her subject John Laroche (Chris Cooper), and the nature of adaptation itself. The film eventually does the Hollywood thing Kaufman despises, aware that it is doing it.

## Key Themes

- The recursive loop of artistic self-consciousness — writing about not being able to write
- Adaptation as transformation, not reproduction — the orchid, the screenplay, the self
- Twin selves and the war between authentic difficulty and commercial ease
- The gap between the writer on the page and the subject they're writing about
- The third act as formal surrender that is simultaneously formal argument

## Why It Matters

Charlie Kaufman and Spike Jonze's most formally radical collaboration. The film credits itself as written by Charlie and Donald Kaufman — Donald Kaufman does not exist. Chris Cooper won the Oscar for Best Supporting Actor as Laroche. The film's self-devouring structure makes it impossible to discuss without meta-commentary, which is the point. Nicolas Cage plays twins and is good at both.
