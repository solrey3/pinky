# Maja Chwalinska

**Nationality:** Polish  
**Born:** 2002, Poland  
**WTA Ranking:** Outside the top 25 (unseeded at Roland Garros 2026)  
**Active:** ~2019–present  
**Status at Roland Garros 2026:** Unseeded finalist

## Summary

A Polish professional tennis player who entered the 2026 Roland Garros draw with no seed and left it as a finalist. Chwalinska's 2026 French Open run is one of the more remarkable individual two-week stretches in recent WTA history — she eliminated the world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka somewhere in the draw's later rounds, then defeated (25th seed) Diana Shnaider in the SF with a 7-6(4), 6-4 result that required her to hold off a player who had herself just shocked Sabalenka.

Her path to a Grand Slam final, entirely unseeded, against an all-Russian opponent (Mirra Andreeva), produces one of the more historically unusual women's Grand Slam finals in the Open Era. An all-unseeded women's Grand Slam final is an exceptionally rare result by any measure.

## Context

Chwalinska's elimination of Sabalenka is the result that opened up this entire bracket of the draw. Sabalenka entered as the WTA No. 1 and a heavy favorite, having won Roland Garros multiple times before. Beating Sabalenka in a slam — let alone going on to reach the final — is the kind of tournament-defining run that puts a player on the map internationally regardless of outcome. She then beat Shnaider — who had herself beaten Sabalenka in the bracket's other half, wait, no — Shnaider beat Sabalenka in the QF, and Chwalinska is in the other SF bracket. Both women eliminated Sabalenka in different rounds of the draw (Shnaider in QF, Chwalinska in an earlier round), which itself speaks to how Sabalenka was eliminated and then Chwalinska continued through to the SF.

Her 7-6(4), 6-4 SF win over Shnaider required winning a tiebreak — this wasn't a dominance win but a resilience win against a player with momentum. The women's final at Roland Garros 2026 features zero players from the pre-tournament top-3 favorites (Swiatek, Gauff, Sabalenka all out).
