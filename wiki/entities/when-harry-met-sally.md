# When Harry Met Sally

**Director:** Rob Reiner
**Genre:** Romantic comedy
**Runtime:** 96 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Harry Burns (Billy Crystal) and Sally Albright (Meg Ryan) meet in 1977, disagree about whether men and women can be friends, and keep running into each other over 12 years until they fall in love. Nora Ephron's screenplay is structured around the central argument and features talking-head interviews with real couples intercut throughout. The deli orgasm scene is cinema history.

## Key Themes

- The friendship vs. romance false binary — proximity and honesty create love
- Gender and communication: Harry's bluntness vs. Sally's performance of fine-ness
- Time and the question of when you're ready to love someone
- New York as the romantic landscape — the city is a third character
- The lie of 'I want my face to be the last face you see at night' — Harry has always meant it

## Why It Matters

Nora Ephron's greatest screenplay. The film essentially codified the modern romantic comedy's structure and dialogue style. Crystal and Ryan have a naturalistic chemistry that directors have been trying to replicate ever since. The 'I'll have what she's having' line — delivered by Rob Reiner's mother — may be the single most famous punchline in the genre. The December finale is one of film's great declarations.
