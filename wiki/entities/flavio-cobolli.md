# Flavio Cobolli

**Nationality:** Italian  
**Born:** 2002, Rome, Italy  
**ATP Ranking:** Outside the seeded players (unseeded at Roland Garros 2026)  
**Active:** ~2020–present  
**Status at Roland Garros 2026:** Unseeded finalist

## Summary

An Italian professional tennis player who entered the 2026 Roland Garros draw without a seed and advanced to his first Grand Slam final. Cobolli's path through the 2026 French Open included a semifinal he never had to play — his SF opponent, Matteo Arnaldi, withdrew before the match — which gave Cobolli a walkover into the final.

The walkover adds a curious wrinkle to the narrative: Cobolli reaches a Grand Slam final without playing a semifinal, meaning his legs are fresh but he has no match rhythm from a five-set battle that his opponent (Zverev) worked through. Whether that's an advantage or a disadvantage against an experienced finals player is genuinely debatable.

## Context

An all-Italian Roland Garros final scenario was avoided when Cobolli became the only Italian in the final (Arnaldi — who would have been the second — withdrew). For Italian tennis, Cobolli reaching the final is nevertheless a significant milestone for a generation of players following Berrettini's 2021 Wimbledon run.

Cobolli is unseeded, which makes the structural parallel to the women's draw striking: Roland Garros 2026 has unseeded finalists in both the men's (Cobolli) and women's (Chwalinska) draws. No other Open Era Grand Slam has produced unseeded finalists in both draws simultaneously, to the best of available knowledge — this tournament has been historically unusual from the quarterfinals onward.
