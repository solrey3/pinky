# Joao Fonseca

19-year-old Brazilian tennis player. In May 2026, became the defining story of Roland Garros by defeating **Novak Djokovic** (3rd seed) in five sets from two sets down — the most significant upset at the French Open in years and a career-defining win before he is old enough to vote in most countries.

**Born:** 2006, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil  
**Nationality:** Brazilian  
**Ranking (as of May 29, 2026):** ~28th seed at Roland Garros  
**Active:** ~2022–present

## Roland Garros 2026 — The Upset

**R3 Result:** Joao Fonseca (28) def. Novak Djokovic (3): **4-6, 4-6, 6-3, 7-5, 7-5**

Fonseca dropped the first two sets against the most accomplished clay-court player in history — a man who had won Roland Garros four times and reached the final nine times. Rather than fold, he reversed the match entirely: won the third, took the fourth in a grueling 7-5, and closed the fifth the same way. House of Highlights captured his post-match reaction; the footage circulated widely within hours.

What makes the result remarkable beyond the scoreline: Djokovic was not injured, not distracted by controversy, and was playing at his standard level. Fonseca won by playing better tennis in the back half of a five-set match against the best player the surface has ever seen.

## Why This Matters

- Djokovic at Roland Garros is the most statistically dominant player-tournament combination in the Open Era. Fonseca's win is not a fluke upset over a shell of a champion — Djokovic is still a top-3 player at 38.
- The previous week saw Belgian qualifier Raphael Collignon upset 5-seed Ben Shelton in straight sets at the same tournament. Back-to-back major seeded upsets at Roland Garros 2026 signal this edition as one of the most volatile in years.
- At 19, Fonseca is exactly the kind of "next gen" player the ATP has been waiting for since Carlos Alcaraz arrived. If he converts on clay against Djokovic, the ceiling conversation starts now.
