# John Havlicek

Eight championships with the Boston Celtics — the second most in NBA history.

**Born:** 1940, Martins Ferry OH
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** SF/SG
**Active:** 1962–1978
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 5 (#29)

## Summary

Eight championships with the Boston Celtics — the second most in NBA history. Havlicek was the most durable and versatile player of his era, playing both forward and guard at a time when such flexibility was novel. His 'Havlicek stole the ball!' moment (1965 Eastern Conference Finals) is the most famous single play call in NBA broadcasting history.

## Highlights

- 8x NBA Champion (Boston Celtics, 1963–1976)
- 13x All-Star; 11x All-NBA
- His 1976 Finals performance (leading the Celtics against Phoenix in the triple-overtime Game 5 — possibly the greatest game ever played) is the peak of his Finals career
- 'Havlicek stole the ball!' — Johnny Most's call on the 1965 steal that ended the game against Philadelphia is the most famous radio call in NBA history
- He played 16 professional seasons averaging 20.8 ppg and 6.3 rpg — the highest sustained scoring average for a wing of his era
