# Charles Barkley

The Round Mound of Rebound and the best player who never won a championship in the modern era.

**Born:** 1963, Leeds AL
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PF
**Active:** 1984–2000
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 5 (#31)

## Summary

The Round Mound of Rebound and the best player who never won a championship in the modern era. One regular-season MVP (1993), eleven All-Star selections, and a career defined by doing more with his physical frame (a supposedly too-small power forward at 6'6") than was thought possible. His 1993 Suns took Jordan's Bulls to 6 games in the Finals.

## Highlights

- 1x NBA MVP (1993); 11x All-Star; 5x All-NBA First Team
- His 1993 season (25.6 ppg, 12.2 rpg, 5.1 apg) is the most complete power forward season outside of Malone in that era
- His 1993 Finals performance against Jordan is one of the best Finals losing-team performances in history
- His rebounds-per-game records at the power forward position rank him as the most efficient rebounder for his size in history
- His Hall of Fame broadcasting career (Inside the NBA) is the most entertaining analyst partnership in sports television
