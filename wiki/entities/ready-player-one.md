# Ready Player One

**Director:** Steven Spielberg
**Genre:** Sci-fi, Adventure
**Runtime:** 140 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

2045. Wade Watts (Tye Sheridan) lives in the 'Stacks' — a vertical trailer park in Columbus, Ohio — and escapes into the OASIS, a vast virtual reality world built by the late James Halliday (Mark Rylance). Halliday hid three Easter eggs in the OASIS with his ownership as the prize. The corporate IOI wants the keys. A pop-culture treasure hunt ensues. The Shining sequence is a technical miracle.

## Key Themes

- Nostalgia as industry and escape: the OASIS is built from the 80s pop culture Halliday loved
- Corporate vs. community ownership of digital public spaces
- The creator's loneliness: Halliday's Easter egg is about his real regret
- Virtual identity and the question of whether digital selves are real selves
- Spielberg directing a film full of his own era's references with genuine affection and mild critique

## Why It Matters

Spielberg directs a film about loving movies with the same craftsmanship he brought to *Raiders of the Lost Ark*. The Shining sequence — a recreation of Kubrick's *The Shining* done in CGI — is jaw-dropping regardless of your feelings about the premise. Mark Rylance's Halliday is one of the best performances in a Spielberg film. The film knows nostalgia is a trap and still indulges it, which is honest.
