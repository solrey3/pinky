# Mirra Andreeva

**Nationality:** Russian  
**Born:** April 29, 2007, Krasnoyarsk, Russia  
**WTA Ranking:** ~8 (seeded 8th at Roland Garros 2026)  
**Active:** 2023–present  
**Age at Roland Garros 2026 Final:** 18

## Summary

One of the most precocious young players on the WTA Tour. Andreeva turned professional at 15 and reached the Roland Garros Round of 16 in her first major main draw appearance (2023), making her one of the youngest players to reach that stage at the French Open in the professional era. Her clay game matured rapidly — big baseline groundstrokes, excellent footwork on red clay, and a competitive composure that belies her age.

At **Roland Garros 2026**, seeded 8th, Andreeva made her first Grand Slam final. Her SF win — a clinical **6-1, 6-3 dismantling of Marta Kostyuk (15th seed)** — showcased the depth of her clay-court game against an opponent who had already beaten both Swiatek and Sabalenka's conquerors in the draw. The women's final is **Andreeva vs. Maja Chwalinska** — both unseeded or low-seeded relative to the tournament favorites — an all-outsider final that reflects how wide open the 2026 draw became after Swiatek (R4), Gauff (R4), and Sabalenka (QF) all exited without reaching the final.

## Context

Andreeva's path to the 2026 Roland Garros final is partially the product of a chaotic women's draw — three of the top-4 seeds eliminated before the semifinals — but her SF win was not a gift. Beating Kostyuk 6-1, 6-3 after Kostyuk had already beaten Swiatek and advanced through a brutal QF is a genuine statement. At 18, if she wins, she would be one of the youngest Roland Garros women's champions in the Open Era.
