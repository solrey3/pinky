# There's Something About Mary

**Director:** Bobby Farrelly, Peter Farrelly
**Genre:** Romantic comedy
**Runtime:** 119 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Ted (Ben Stiller) was stood up on prom night by his dream girl Mary (Cameron Diaz) after a zipper incident. Thirteen years later he hires a private detective (Matt Dillon) to find her — who falls in love with her himself. Multiple men's lives have been reorganized around Mary's existence. She has no idea. The film contains jokes that should not work but do.

## Key Themes

- Male obsession reframed as comedy: all these men have built their lives around one woman
- The manic pixie dream girl concept before it was named — and a film that interrogates it
- Physical comedy and transgression: the Farrellys test the limits of what audiences permit
- Authenticity vs. performance: every suitor has created a persona for Mary; she sees through them
- The underdog as genuine romantic ideal vs. the successful competitor

## Why It Matters

The Farrelly brothers' peak. Cameron Diaz's comedic timing and genuine charm is the reason the film works — Mary has to be worth all of this, and she is. The hair gel scene. Matt Dillon's character lying constantly. Jonathan Richman serenading the action. The film pushed gross-out comedy as far as it could go while remaining genuinely romantic.
