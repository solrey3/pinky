# Dog Day Afternoon

**Director:** Sidney Lumet
**Genre:** Crime drama, Thriller
**Runtime:** 125 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Based on a true story. Sonny Wortzik (Al Pacino) and Sal (John Cazale) rob a Brooklyn bank to fund Sonny's partner's gender-reassignment surgery. The robbery goes sideways immediately and becomes a media circus, a standoff with the FBI, and an accidental countercultural event. Sonny rallies the crowd outside with 'Attica! Attica!' as the hostage situation descends into absurdist tragedy.

## Key Themes

- Outsider identity and the impossible negotiation with a hostile society
- Media spectacle converting crisis into entertainment in real time
- Class rage and institutional failure — Sonny is genuinely beloved by the crowd
- Gender and queerness in 1970s New York: Leon's story treated with unexpected dignity
- The amateur criminal as tragic everyman: competence vs. desperation

## Why It Matters

Pacino's greatest performance — raw, funny, exhausting, heartbreaking. Lumet shoots it like a documentary, giving 1975 Brooklyn an irreplaceable texture. John Cazale plays Sal with the quiet danger of a man who has nothing to lose. The film was politically explosive on release (gay rights, trans rights, anti-FBI sentiment) and has only grown more resonant. The real Sonny said he hated that Pacino was too handsome.
