# Doctor Zhivago

**Director:** David Lean
**Genre:** Epic romance, Drama
**Runtime:** 197 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Russia across the Revolution and Civil War. Yuri Zhivago (Omar Sharif), a doctor and poet, is caught between his wife Tonya (Geraldine Chaplin) and his great love Lara (Julie Christie), against the backdrop of historical upheaval. Filmed on location in Spain and Finland for the Russian winter. Maurice Jarre's 'Lara's Theme' is one of cinema's most recognizable melodies.

## Key Themes

- Private love vs. historical necessity — the Revolution does not care about Yuri and Lara
- The artist in political catastrophe: Zhivago's poetry survives where everything else is destroyed
- Epic scale and intimate emotion: Lean's genius for containing both
- The Russian soul and its literary tradition (Pasternak's Nobel Prize, the Soviet ban)
- Fate and historical determinism: characters swept by forces larger than any individual

## Why It Matters

One of the last great studio epics. David Lean made films at a scale that no longer exists — *Lawrence of Arabia*, *Bridge on the River Kwai*, *Doctor Zhivago* are each monuments. Omar Sharif and Julie Christie's chemistry is genuine. The film was banned in the Soviet Union until 1994. Maurice Jarre's score won the Oscar. The ice palace sequence required building an actual ice palace.
