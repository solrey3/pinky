# Vertigo

**Director:** Alfred Hitchcock
**Genre:** Psychological thriller, Mystery
**Runtime:** 128 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Scottie Ferguson (James Stewart), a retired San Francisco detective with acrophobia, is hired to follow the mysterious Madeleine (Kim Novak), who seems possessed by a dead woman's spirit. After tragedy, Scottie becomes obsessed with remaking a new woman, Judy, in Madeleine's image — not knowing he is the victim of an elaborate deception and his own need to control.

## Key Themes

- Male obsession and the male gaze literalized — Scottie literally molds Judy into a fantasy
- The unattainability of the idealized woman as a form of necrophilia
- Memory, grief, and the impossibility of resurrection
- San Francisco's geography as psychological map — height as control, fall as loss
- Identity as performance: every character in the film is playing someone else

## Why It Matters

Consistently ranked #1 on the Sight & Sound greatest films poll (2012, 2022). Hitchcock built the 'dolly zoom' (Hitchcock zoom) for this film. Stewart plays against his wholesome image to disturbing effect — his obsession is not romantic, it is pathological. Bernard Herrmann's score is one of cinema's finest. The film was out of circulation for decades because Hitchcock withheld it from re-release.
