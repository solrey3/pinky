# Bloodsport

**Director:** Newt Arnold
**Genre:** Martial arts, Action
**Runtime:** 92 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Based (loosely) on the claimed real story of Frank Dux (Jean-Claude Van Damme), the first Westerner to win the Kumite — an underground full-contact martial arts tournament held in Hong Kong. Van Damme enters to honor his late sensei Senzo Tanaka, faces the undefeated champion Chong Li (Bolo Yeung), and survives being blinded to win. The splits scene is iconic.

## Key Themes

- Honor and filial loyalty: Frank fights for his dead sensei's memory, not glory
- The Western outsider proving himself within Eastern martial tradition
- Pure physical competition as moral arena — the Kumite's no-rules clarity
- Friendship under pressure: Ray Jackson's hospitalization raises the stakes
- The body as the only language that matters in competition

## Why It Matters

Van Damme's American breakthrough. The film is objectively a B-movie — the acting is thin, the direction serviceable, the actual Frank Dux story largely fabricated. None of that matters. Van Damme's physical performance, the Kumite tournament format, and Bolo Yeung's genuinely menacing Chong Li create a kinetic entertainment that has outlasted most of its contemporaries. Perfectly serves its purpose.
