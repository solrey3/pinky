# The Great Muta / Keiji Mutoh

#73.

**Real Name:** Keiji Muto
**Born:** 1962, Kakegawa, Japan
**Nationality:** Japanese
**Active:** 1985–2023
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Personal Rank:** #73 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#73. The greatest Japanese character for Western audiences in wrestling history. The Great Muta character — green mist, the moonsault, the eerie presence — was the first Japanese wrestling character to genuinely cross cultural lines. His match with Sting at Clash of the Champions (1989) produced what was, at the time, the highest-rated match in wrestling history (★★★★¾, Meltzer). His knees were destroyed by the moonsault — he had 10 knee surgeries.

## Highlights

- Multiple IWGP/NWA/WCW Champion
- His match with Sting at Clash of the Champions 1989 is the match that invented 'match ratings culture'
- The Muta moonsault is the most imitated wrestling move in history — everyone from HBK to Omos has done a version
- His green mist has been sprayed in more faces than any other wrestling weapon
- His retirement match (2023) after 38 years ended a career built on genuine sacrifice — his knee damage is visible in his walk
