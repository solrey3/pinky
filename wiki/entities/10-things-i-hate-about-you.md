# 10 Things I Hate About You

**Director:** Gil Junger
**Genre:** Romantic comedy
**Runtime:** 97 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

High school adaptation of *The Taming of the Shrew* set in Seattle. Bianca (Larisa Oleynik) can only date if her antisocial older sister Kat (Julia Stiles) does. Cameron (Joseph Gordon-Levitt) pays Patrick (Heath Ledger) to date Kat. The money changes hands; the feelings do not. The Shakespeare sonnet read at the end ('I hate the way you don't see me') is the film's earned climax.

## Key Themes

- The 'shrew' as a woman with standards in a world that rewards compliance
- Authenticity vs. the performance of detachment — Kat's anger is real, her coldness is protective
- The transaction at the heart of courtship: Patrick is paid but means it
- Feminist identity in high school culture: Kat's intelligence as social liability
- Shakespeare's comedic architecture surviving translation to 1990s teen film

## Why It Matters

Heath Ledger's American breakthrough. The bleacher scene — 'Can't Take My Eyes Off You' with the marching band — is one of the most purely charming romantic scenes of the decade. Julia Stiles's Kat is sharper and angrier than most teen film heroines are allowed to be. The film treats its Shakespeare source with intelligence rather than distance. The sonnet at the end lands.
