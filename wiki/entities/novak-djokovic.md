# Novak Djokovic

Serbian professional tennis player. The most decorated player in men's tennis history by Grand Slam count. On any surface, and particularly on clay at Roland Garros, Djokovic represents the apex of what a professional tennis player can achieve. At 38 years old in 2026, still ranked as a top-3 player — the longevity is itself a data point.

**Born:** 1987, Belgrade, Serbia  
**Nationality:** Serbian  
**Career Grand Slams:** 24+ (Open Era record)  
**Roland Garros titles:** 4 (2016, 2021, 2023, 2024)

## Career Context

Djokovic holds the men's record for most Grand Slam singles titles in the Open Era, having surpassed Nadal's clay-court dominance across all surfaces combined. At Roland Garros specifically, he is one of the few players who regularly defeated Rafael Nadal on clay — an achievement that by itself places him in a separate historical tier.

His longevity at the top of the game past 35 is among the most remarkable physical and competitive feats in professional sport. He has adapted his game, recovered from surgeries, navigated political controversy (COVID vaccine deportation from Australia in 2022), and continued to contend at Slams.

## 2026 Roland Garros — Elimination

**R3:** Joao Fonseca (BRA, 28-seed) def. Djokovic (3-seed) **4-6, 4-6, 6-3, 7-5, 7-5**

Djokovic led 2-0 sets against the 19-year-old Brazilian before losing the next three. The result stands as the marquee upset of Roland Garros 2026 — Fonseca's comeback from two sets down to eliminate the tournament's most accomplished champion was the defining tennis moment of the week.
