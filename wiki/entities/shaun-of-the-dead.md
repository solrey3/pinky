# Shaun of the Dead

**Director:** Edgar Wright
**Genre:** Horror comedy, Zombie film
**Runtime:** 99 min
**Country:** UK

## Summary

Shaun (Simon Pegg) is a 29-year-old underachiever whose girlfriend has dumped him. The zombie apocalypse breaks out. His plan to save the day involves going to his local pub, the Winchester. The film is a romantic comedy that happens to be a zombie film, and a zombie film that happens to be a romantic comedy, and both are sincere.

## Key Themes

- Adult stagnation and the zombie as metaphor — the undead look exactly like the commuters
- Friendship and its demands: Ed vs. Liz as the film's real conflict
- The pub as sanctuary and the British relationship with it
- Genre commitment: Wright never winks at the horror, so the horror works
- Love requiring growth: Shaun has to become an adult, the apocalypse just expedites it

## Why It Matters

The first entry in the Cornetto trilogy and Wright's feature debut. The opening sequence — Shaun walking to the shop, oblivious to the zombie apocalypse beginning around him — is one of the great comic set-ups in modern British cinema. The film respects George Romero enough to earn its comedy. Dylan Moran's David death scene is unexpectedly devastating. Pegg and Frost's friendship is the movie.
