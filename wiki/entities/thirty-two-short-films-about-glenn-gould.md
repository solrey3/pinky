# Thirty Two Short Films About Glenn Gould

**Director:** François Girard
**Genre:** Biographical drama, Experimental
**Runtime:** 94 min
**Country:** Canada

## Summary

32 vignettes — corresponding to the 32 Goldberg Variations — about Glenn Gould: his childhood at the piano, his final public concert at 31, his hermitic recording life in Toronto, his obsession with temperature and medication, his phone calls with strangers, his ideas about music and technology, and his death. Colm Feore plays Gould. The film's structure is the film's argument about how a life holds together.

## Key Themes

- The Goldberg Variations as life structure — form containing and releasing feeling
- Solitude as artistic choice and psychological necessity
- Technology as intimacy: Gould preferred the recording studio to the concert hall
- Perfectionism and its cost: Gould's pharmaceutical dependency and physical decline
- Canadian identity and the wilderness as interior landscape

## Why It Matters

One of the most formally inventive biographical films ever made. The 32-fragment structure means there is no single narrative — only the accumulation of a person through different lenses (documentary interview, narrative scene, animation, pure performance). Feore's performance is physical and internal simultaneously. The film makes you want to listen to the Goldberg Variations immediately after, which is the intended effect.
