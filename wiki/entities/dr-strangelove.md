# Dr. Strangelove or: How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love the Bomb

**Director:** Stanley Kubrick
**Genre:** Satirical black comedy
**Runtime:** 95 min
**Country:** UK/USA

## Summary

An insane general (Sterling Hayden) launches a nuclear strike on the Soviet Union without authorization. The US President (Peter Sellers) and his advisors in the War Room, a British RAF officer (also Sellers), and the nuclear scientist Dr. Strangelove (Sellers again) try to stop it while the Soviet Ambassador reveals the Doomsday Machine. The bomb drops. Peter Sellers plays three roles; George C. Scott is unhinged.

## Key Themes

- Mutually assured destruction as the logical endpoint of military deterrence theory
- Bureaucratic absurdity and the impossibility of rational actors in irrational systems
- Masculinity and nuclear anxiety — every missile, every bodily fluid is a phallic symbol
- The comedy of human inability to prevent the disasters we create
- Political and military institutions as self-perpetuating regardless of outcome

## Why It Matters

Kubrick was adapting Peter George's serious novel *Red Alert* and gradually realized the only honest treatment was comedy. Sellers improvised most of his Strangelove performance, including the arm that rises with a mind of its own. The War Room set (designed by Ken Adam) is one of cinema's greatest. The final image — Major Kong riding the bomb down, waving his hat — is 20th century history compressed to a single frame.
