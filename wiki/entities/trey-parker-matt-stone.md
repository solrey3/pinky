# Trey Parker & Matt Stone

Co-creators of South Park — the longest-running and most consistently sharp satirical animated series in American television.

**Born:** 1969, Conifer CO / 1971, Houston TX
**Nationality:** American
**Active:** 1997–present
**Profession:** Animated television creators, writers, directors

## Summary

Co-creators of South Park — the longest-running and most consistently sharp satirical animated series in American television. Parker and Stone's six-day production model (writing, animating, and publishing an episode in six days) allows South Park to comment on current events with a topicality no other animated show can match. Their Broadway musical The Book of Mormon (2011) is the most decorated in Tony history.

## Highlights

- South Park (1997–present) — #4 personal TV ranking; the sharpest sustained American satire
- The Book of Mormon (Broadway, 2011) — 9 Tony Awards including Best Musical; the most decorated Broadway debut by film/TV creators
- Their six-day production model (writing to broadcast in one week) is the fastest production cycle in animation history
- Team America: World Police (2004) — the most subversive political satire film of the 2000s
- Their willingness to satirize Islam with the same energy as Christianity (and the network censorship that followed) documented free speech limits in American media
