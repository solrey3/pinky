# My Blue Heaven

**Director:** Herbert Ross
**Genre:** Comedy, Crime
**Runtime:** 95 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Flamboyant New York mobster Vinnie Antonelli (Steve Martin) is relocated to a bland San Diego suburb under WITSEC while testifying against the mob. Straight-arrow FBI agent Barney Coopersmith (Rick Moranis) is his minder. Vinnie cannot stop being a criminal — but his flamboyant criminality charms everyone in Suburbicon, including the DA (Joan Cusack). A culture-clash comedy written by Nora Ephron.

## Key Themes

- The irreducible self: Vinnie cannot suppress his nature regardless of circumstance
- Suburbia as a form of death — the mob, perversely, offers more authentic community
- The cop who becomes the criminal's accomplice through sheer proximity
- Italian-American identity and the performance of masculinity
- Nora Ephron's satire of WASPish suburban conformity

## Why It Matters

Steve Martin's most underrated performance — physical, musical, genuinely warm. The film exists in a sweet spot of 90s comedy that no longer gets made. Martin's dancing and physical presence elevate every scene. Rick Moranis's deadpan exasperation is the perfect foil. Written by Nora Ephron (who reportedly based Vinnie on the real Henry Hill, whose story also became *Goodfellas* the same year).
