# Darren Aronofsky

The director most committed to depicting psychological extremity in the human body.

**Born:** 1969, Brooklyn NY
**Nationality:** American
**Active:** 1998–present
**Profession:** Film director, writer, producer

## Summary

The director most committed to depicting psychological extremity in the human body. Pi (1998), Requiem for a Dream (2000), The Wrestler (2008), Black Swan (2010), Mother! (2017) — each film uses physical extremity as a mirror for psychological disintegration. His visual style (extreme close-up, fish-eye lens, hip-hop montage) was entirely original at the time of Pi.

## Highlights

- Pi (1998) — in personal film rankings; debut film shot for $60,000; Sundance Award for Direction
- Requiem for a Dream (2000) — in personal film rankings; the most formally aggressive addiction film ever made
- The Wrestler (2008) — Mickey Rourke's career resurrection; the most emotionally precise film about athletic aging
- Black Swan (2010) — the most commercially successful film of his career; Natalie Portman Oscar
- His hip-hop montage technique (extreme fast-cut sequences with sound effects) is the most imitated formal innovation from the late 1990s indie cinema
