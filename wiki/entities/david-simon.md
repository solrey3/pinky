# David Simon

The creator of The Wire — widely considered the greatest television drama ever made.

**Born:** 1960, Washington DC
**Nationality:** American
**Active:** 1993–present
**Profession:** Television writer, producer, journalist

## Summary

The creator of The Wire — widely considered the greatest television drama ever made. Simon's background as a Baltimore Sun police reporter gave everything he wrote a forensic accuracy that prestige TV had not previously achieved. His subsequent work (Treme, Show Me a Hero, The Deuce, We Own This City) maintains the same institutional critique at different scales.

## Highlights

- The Wire (2002–2008) — the greatest television drama ever made; created from his journalism experience
- Homicide: Life on the Street (1993–1999) — co-creator; the prototype for The Wire's moral universe
- Treme (2010–2013) — his most culturally rich work; the most complete portrait of post-Katrina New Orleans
- We Own This City (2022) — his most recent Baltimore story; the most focused examination of police corruption
- His book 'The Corner' (1997) is the direct raw material for The Wire's most devastating season (Season 4)
