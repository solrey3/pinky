# City of God

**Director:** Fernando Meirelles, Kátia Lund
**Genre:** Crime drama
**Runtime:** 130 min
**Country:** Brazil

## Summary

The Cidade de Deus housing project in Rio de Janeiro, 1960s–1980s. Rocket (Alexandre Rodrigues), a boy who wants to be a photographer, narrates the rise of Lil' Zé from child killer to drug lord, the escalating gang wars, and the city-within-a-city logic of the favela. Based on Paulo Lins's novel, drawn from real events. The opening chicken chase is one of cinema's most kinetic sequences.

## Key Themes

- Structural violence: poverty and housing policy as the architects of crime
- The observer vs. the participant — Rocket's camera as survival tool and moral position
- The seductiveness and futility of criminal power in a closed system
- Childhood as a site of violence: the Runts are child soldiers by any measure
- Photography as the only escape from a system designed to trap

## Why It Matters

One of the greatest films of the 2000s. Meirelles and Lund used hyperkinetic editing, handheld work, and a largely non-professional cast to create something that feels documentary and operatic simultaneously. The film was shot on location with community cooperation. Nominated for four Oscars including Director. Roger Ebert called it 'one of the best films of our time.'
