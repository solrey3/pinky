# Magnolia

**Director:** Paul Thomas Anderson
**Genre:** Drama
**Runtime:** 188 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Eight storylines in San Fernando Valley's TV culture, converging over one extraordinary day. A dying game show producer (Jason Robards), his dying host (Philip Baker Hall), a motivational misogynist (Tom Cruise), a former child prodigy (William H. Macy), a current child prodigy (Jeremy Blackman), a nurse (Philip Seymour Hoffman), a cop (John C. Reilly), a drug addict (Melora Walters). They all sing Aimee Mann. It rains frogs.

## Key Themes

- Coincidence, fate, and the possibility of meaning in chaos — the frog rain
- Fathers and sons: damage passed down, apology arrived at too late
- Television culture and the exploitation of children for entertainment
- Forgiveness as both necessary and insufficient
- The ensemble as an argument: these lives are connected whether they know it or not

## Why It Matters

PTA's most ambitious and divisive film. Three hours of operatic American unhappiness that culminates in a biblically literal event (Exodus 8:2) treated as matter-of-fact. Tom Cruise gives the best performance of his career. The 'Wise Up' group sing is either the most audacious scene of 1999 or a miscalculation — the debate is the point. Philip Seymour Hoffman's nurse Phil is quietly the film's moral center.
