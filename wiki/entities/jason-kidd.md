# Jason Kidd

The most complete point guard of the 2000s.

**Born:** 1973, San Francisco CA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PG
**Active:** 1994–2013
**Personal NBA Tier:** Level 2

## Summary

The most complete point guard of the 2000s. One championship (2011 Dallas Mavericks), ten All-Star selections, four assists titles, five steals titles. Kidd's combination of rebounding (nearly 7 rpg career for a guard), passing, and defense makes him the most statistically versatile PG in NBA history. Led two different teams to the Finals.

## Highlights

- 1x NBA Champion (2011 Dallas Mavericks); 10x All-Star
- Led both New Jersey and Dallas to the NBA Finals — the only player to take two different teams to the Finals as the primary option
- 5x All-Defensive First Team; 5x steals champion; 4x assists champion
- Near triple-double career averages (12.6 ppg, 8.7 rpg, 8.7 apg) for a point guard
- His development from a poor shooter to an adequate three-point shooter defined his late-career utility
