# Bill Walton

The most complete center of his generation when healthy — which was rarely.

**Born:** 1952, La Mesa CA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** C
**Active:** 1974–1988
**Personal NBA Tier:** Level 2

## Summary

The most complete center of his generation when healthy — which was rarely. Walton's foot and ankle injuries limited him to 468 games in 14 seasons, but those games produced two championships (Portland 1977, Boston 1986), one MVP, and one Finals MVP. His 1977 Finals performance against the Philadelphia 76ers is the most dominant center performance of that era.

## Highlights

- 2x NBA Champion (1977 Portland, 1986 Boston as Sixth Man); 1x MVP; 1x Finals MVP
- His 1977 Blazers were the most complete team of that era — center-driven, defense-first
- 1x Sixth Man of the Year (1986 Celtics) — the rare MVP-caliber player willing to come off the bench
- His UCLA career (2x NCAA Champion) was the best center college career before Patrick Ewing
- His broadcasting career and unique personality made him beloved beyond his playing days
