# Hot Fuzz

**Director:** Edgar Wright
**Genre:** Action comedy, Mystery
**Runtime:** 121 min
**Country:** UK

## Summary

Nicholas Angel (Simon Pegg), London's most efficient police officer, is reassigned to the quiet village of Sandford, Gloucestershire, where he is partnered with Danny Butterman (Nick Frost) who loves action films. A series of 'accidents' leads Angel to discover that Sandford's Neighbourhood Watch Alliance has been murdering anyone who threatens the village's 'Village of the Year' status. References *Point Break* and *Bad Boys II* constantly, then becomes them.

## Key Themes

- Conformity enforced by violence: the village destroys anything that threatens its image
- The outsider as threat: Angel's competence is what makes him dangerous to the community
- Action cinema as a shared language between the two protagonists — Danny's love is literal
- British parochialism and the dark comedy of the 'perfect' community
- Buddy cop partnership as genuine platonic love

## Why It Matters

The technical peak of the Cornetto trilogy. Wright's editing here — match cuts, sound bridges, rapid-fire visual callbacks — is a graduate seminar in film language disguised as entertainment. Every genre reference pays off as plot. The third act becomes the action film Danny always wanted to see, and the camera honors that. The reveal of the NWA is one of the great third-act pivots in British cinema.
