# Rod Serling

The creator of The Twilight Zone — the most influential anthology series in television history.

**Born:** 1924, Syracuse NY
**Nationality:** American
**Active:** 1951–1975
**Profession:** Television writer, producer, host

## Summary

The creator of The Twilight Zone — the most influential anthology series in television history. Serling wrote 92 of the 156 original episodes, using science fiction as cover for social commentary on race, McCarthyism, and nuclear anxiety that CBS would not have permitted as straight drama. His introductions to each episode are the most imitated TV monologue form in history.

## Highlights

- The Twilight Zone (1959–1964) — in personal TV rankings; invented the sci-fi anthology format as social commentary vehicle
- Wrote 92 of the 156 original episodes — the most output by a single writer in any television anthology
- His use of genre as cover for political speech about McCarthyism and racism is the foundational technique of speculative fiction television
- His introductions ('You're traveling through another dimension, a dimension not only of sight and sound but of mind') are the most recognizable hosting monologues in TV history
- Night Gallery (1969–1973) — his second major anthology extended the formula into darker Gothic territory
