# The Godfather

**Director:** Francis Ford Coppola
**Genre:** Crime drama
**Runtime:** 175 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

The aging patriarch of the Corleone crime family, Vito Corleone (Marlon Brando), is shot in a power struggle. His youngest son Michael (Al Pacino), a war hero who wanted nothing to do with the family business, is drawn in step by step until he is fully transformed into his father — and something colder. A saga of family, power, loyalty, and the seductive gravity of violence.

## Key Themes

- The corruption of the good man by power and circumstance — Michael's arc
- Family as both sanctuary and prison — loyalty demands absolute compliance
- The parallel between organized crime and legitimate business/government
- Assimilation and the immigrant American Dream's dark underside
- Fathers and sons: Vito builds something; Michael perfects it and hollows it out

## Why It Matters

Won the Academy Award for Best Picture, Best Actor, Best Adapted Screenplay. Brando's Don Vito remains one of cinema's defining performances. Nino Rota's score is inseparable from the word 'cinema.' Coppola fought the studio on virtually every casting decision (Brando, Pacino, Keaton) and was vindicated on all of them. The baptism sequence cross-cut with the Five Families massacre is one of film's great montage achievements.
