# Enter the Dragon

**Director:** Robert Clouse
**Genre:** Martial arts, Action
**Runtime:** 99 min
**Country:** USA/Hong Kong

## Summary

Lee (Bruce Lee), a Shaolin martial artist, is recruited by British intelligence to infiltrate a martial arts tournament hosted by Han (Shih Kien), a rogue Shaolin monk operating a drug empire on a private island. Lee competes alongside Roper (John Saxon) and Williams (Jim Kelly) while gathering evidence — and avenging his sister's death. The climax is a hall-of-mirrors fight for the ages.

## Key Themes

- Martial arts as philosophy made physical — Lee's fighting is an extension of Shaolin ethics
- Colonialism and intelligence exploitation: Britain uses Lee as a deniable asset
- The corruption of mastery — Han perverts Shaolin teaching for criminal empire
- Black American experience at the margins of action cinema (Jim Kelly's Williams)
- The mirror fight: identity, reflection, and the enemy within

## Why It Matters

Bruce Lee's masterpiece and the film that brought Hong Kong martial arts cinema to Western audiences. Lee died six days before its release. His charisma and physicality remain unmatched on screen — the scene where he explains his fighting style ('It's like a finger pointing at the moon') is one of cinema's most distilled expressions of Zen. Jim Kelly is criminally underrated as Williams. The film launched the 1970s kung fu craze.
