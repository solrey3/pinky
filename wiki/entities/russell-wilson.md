# Russell Wilson

**Position:** Quarterback  
**Born:** 1988, Cincinnati, OH  
**Active (playing):** 2012–2026  
**Teams:** Seattle Seahawks (2012–2021), Denver Broncos (2022–2023), Pittsburgh Steelers (2024), various  
**Post-Playing:** CBS Sports analyst

## Summary

One of the more decorated quarterbacks of his generation — a dual-threat QB whose combination of accuracy, mobility, and command presence made him a franchise-level player for the Seahawks across a decade. His tenure in Seattle produced a Super Bowl championship (**Super Bowl XLVIII**, 43–8 over the Denver Broncos — one of the most lopsided Super Bowl wins in history), a second Super Bowl appearance (Super Bowl XLIX), and multiple Pro Bowl selections.

His post-Seattle career (Denver, Pittsburgh) was characterized more by decline and dysfunction than his Seattle peak. The contract situation in Denver in particular became a cautionary tale about franchise over-payment and roster mismanagement. By the time he was released in 2023, the quarterback who had seemed capable of sustaining elite play into his mid-30s had effectively been reduced to a backup/bridge role.

On **June 4, 2026**, Wilson **announced his retirement from professional football**, transitioning immediately to **CBS Sports** as a broadcast personality. The move to CBS puts him alongside a network that carries the NFL — a natural landing spot for a recognizable face who has cultivated a media-friendly persona throughout his career.

The r/sports community noted with characteristic affection and sharpness: *"The Super Bowl XLVIII interception will follow him to the broadcast booth"* — a reference that, whatever the specific game intended, captures the persistent narrative shadow that has followed Wilson out of Seattle.
