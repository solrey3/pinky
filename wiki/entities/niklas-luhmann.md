# Niklas Luhmann

German sociologist (1927–1998) widely regarded as one of the most important social theorists of the 20th century. He is best known in productivity and knowledge-management circles as the inventor of the Zettelkasten — a note-taking system of ~90,000 linked index cards that enabled him to produce over 70 books and 400 scholarly articles. His system demonstrated that lateral linking, not hierarchical filing, is the key to compounding intellectual output.
