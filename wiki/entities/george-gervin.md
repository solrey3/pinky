# George Gervin

The Iceman.

**Born:** 1952, Detroit MI
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** SG
**Active:** 1972–1986
**Personal NBA Tier:** Level 2

## Summary

The Iceman. The most creative scorer in ABA/NBA history — his finger roll is the most aesthetically unique finishing move in basketball. Four scoring titles (1978–1980, 1982). His dueling scoring title race against David Thompson (April 9, 1978) — Gervin scoring 63 in the final game to edge Thompson who had scored 73 in the earlier game — is the most dramatic single-day title race in basketball history.

## Highlights

- 4x NBA scoring champion (1978–80, 1982); 9x All-Star
- His 63-point game on April 9, 1978, to win the scoring title over Thompson is the most dramatic individual title race in basketball history
- The Iceman's finger roll is the most distinctive individual finishing move in NBA history
- His ABA career (1972–1976) in San Antonio established his scoring credentials before the NBA
- His relaxed demeanor ('Iceman') contrasted with his aggressive scoring — a combination that seemed impossible
