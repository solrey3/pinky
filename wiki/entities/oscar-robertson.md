# Oscar Robertson

The player who averaged a triple-double for an entire season — 1961-62: 30.

**Born:** 1938, Charlotte TN
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PG
**Active:** 1960–1974
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 4 (#15)

## Summary

The player who averaged a triple-double for an entire season — 1961-62: 30.8 ppg, 12.5 rpg, 11.4 apg. Robertson's combination of scoring, rebounding, and playmaking at the guard position was not repeated until Russell Westbrook in 2016-17 (who required the modern pace-and-space era to do it). One championship (1971 Milwaukee Bucks). His Robertson v. NBA antitrust lawsuit broke the reserve clause.

## Highlights

- 1x NBA Champion (1971 Milwaukee Bucks with Kareem)
- Averaged a triple-double for the 1961–62 season — the most complete single season statistical achievement for a guard
- 12x All-Star; 1x regular-season MVP (1964); 11x All-NBA
- His Robertson v. NBA lawsuit (1970) was the legal foundation for free agency in basketball
- His scoring (25,192 career points), rebounding, and assists are all all-time top performers at his position
