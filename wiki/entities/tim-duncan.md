# Tim Duncan

The greatest power forward in NBA history and the most consistent winner of the post-Jordan era.

**Born:** 1976, Saint Croix, USVI
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PF/C
**Active:** 1997–2016
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 3 (#11)

## Summary

The greatest power forward in NBA history and the most consistent winner of the post-Jordan era. Five championships, three Finals MVPs, two regular-season MVPs — all with one franchise (San Antonio Spurs). The Fundamental's career is defined by the absence of unnecessary highlight plays: the bank shot, the pick-and-roll with Parker, the quiet defense that anchored five championship teams.

## Highlights

- 5x NBA Champion (1999, 2003, 2005, 2007, 2014); 3x Finals MVP; 2x regular-season MVP
- 19x All-Star; 15x All-NBA; 15x All-Defensive
- His 2003 Finals performance (24.2 ppg, 17.0 rpg, 5.3 ast, 5.3 blk) against the Nets is the most complete big man Finals performance of the 2000s
- The San Antonio Spurs dynasty (1999–2016) is the most sustained championship culture in the modern NBA
- His bank shot off the backboard is the most reliable midrange scoring technique in the post-Jordan era
