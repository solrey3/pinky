# One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest

**Director:** Milos Forman
**Genre:** Drama
**Runtime:** 133 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Randle P. McMurphy (Jack Nicholson) feigns insanity to avoid prison labor and is committed to a psychiatric ward run by the icily authoritarian Nurse Ratched (Louise Fletcher). McMurphy's anarchic energy galvanizes the institutionalized patients, threatening the ward's order. The conflict ends in lobotomy, mercy killing, and an escape — freedom purchased in blood.

## Key Themes

- Institutional control as a form of violence — Ratched is the system, not a villain
- Sanity as a political category: who gets to define it and for whose benefit
- The liberator's paradox — McMurphy's freedom-giving leads to his own destruction
- Chief Bromden: the most sane person in the building plays deaf and dumb to survive
- Conformity vs. individuality as the core American psycho-political tension

## Why It Matters

One of only three films to win all five major Oscars (Picture, Director, Actor, Actress, Screenplay). Nicholson is incandescent; Fletcher's Nurse Ratched became cinema's definitive female villain — not because she's evil but because she's sincerely right, by her own logic. Kesey, who wrote the novel, hated the film for sidelining Chief Bromden's perspective. He was right, but the film works anyway.
