# I'm Gonna Git You Sucka

**Director:** Keenen Ivory Wayans
**Genre:** Blaxploitation parody, Comedy
**Runtime:** 88 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Jack Spade (Keenen Ivory Wayans) returns to his hometown of Any Ghetto USA after his brother's death from OG (Over Gold — too many gold chains). To fight Mr. Big, he assembles a team of retired blaxploitation heroes. A loving and merciless parody of 1970s blaxploitation cinema featuring Jim Brown, Isaac Hayes, Bernie Casey, Antonio Fargas, and Steve James playing versions of their classic personas.

## Key Themes

- Satire as homage — every joke is built on genuine love for the genre
- Masculinity and its absurdities: gold chains literally killing people
- Black American popular culture self-critique from inside the tradition
- The reunion of legends as comedy: what happens when the muscle runs to fat
- Community, neighborhood ownership, and gentrification-adjacent resistance

## Why It Matters

Wayans assembled the actual blaxploitation icons and got them to parody themselves. Isaac Hayes's entrance scene is legendary. The 'how much for those fish' scene between Hayes and his backup singers is improvised brilliance. The film launched the Wayans family's Hollywood career and is the direct creative ancestor of the *In Living Color* sensibility.
