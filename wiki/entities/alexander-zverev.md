# Alexander Zverev

**Nationality:** German  
**Born:** April 20, 1997, Hamburg, Germany  
**ATP Ranking:** ~2 (seeded 2nd at Roland Garros 2026)  
**Active:** 2013–present  
**Age at Roland Garros 2026:** 29

## Summary

One of the most accomplished players of the post-Big Three ATP generation. Zverev has won ATP Finals titles, Olympic gold (Tokyo 2020), and reached multiple Grand Slam finals — including the 2020 US Open final (lost to Thiem in five sets). The persistent knock on him for years was his inability to convert Grand Slam final opportunities; his Roland Garros 2026 campaign offers another shot.

His baseline game is elite — enormous forehand, disguised backhand, and one of the better serves in the game. On clay he is a genuine threat: the slower surface suits his heavy topspin groundstroke game, and he's been near the top of the Roland Garros contender list for several years. At 29, this is not a "young player still learning" story — Zverev has been on the ATP Tour for over a decade and knows exactly what he needs to do in a Grand Slam final.
