# Virgil

Roman poet (70–19 BCE), author of the *Aeneid*, *Eclogues*, and *Georgics*. Commissioned by Augustus to write a Roman national epic, Virgil produced the *Aeneid* as a deliberate response to Homer's *Iliad* and *Odyssey*. He died before completing final revisions and reportedly requested the manuscript be destroyed; Augustus overruled him. His work shaped Western literature, Dante's *Divine Comedy*, and the concept of imperial destiny.
