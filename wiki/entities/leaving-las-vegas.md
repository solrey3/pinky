# Leaving Las Vegas

**Director:** Mike Figgis
**Genre:** Drama, Romance
**Runtime:** 111 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Ben Sanderson (Nicolas Cage) is fired from his Hollywood job, sells everything, and goes to Las Vegas to drink himself to death. He meets sex worker Sera (Elisabeth Shue) and they form a relationship predicated on a single rule: she will not ask him to stop drinking. The film is about what love looks like in the absence of hope. Cage won the Academy Award for Best Actor.

## Key Themes

- Unconditional acceptance as a form of love — and its limits
- Self-destruction as a chosen path, not a symptom: Ben has decided
- The sex worker's humanity and the male gaze the film works against
- Las Vegas as a city built for the destruction of inhibition
- Grief and the body: Ben is drinking himself to death and both characters know it

## Why It Matters

Cage's career performance. He reportedly drank heavily for weeks and filmed himself to study drunk behavior. Figgis shot on Super 16mm with a small crew and natural light — the film has a texture that studio productions can't reproduce. Elisabeth Shue was also Oscar-nominated. The film refuses redemption: Ben dies, Sera survives, the ending is neither hopeful nor despairing. It is honest.
