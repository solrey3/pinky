# Blue Origin

Private aerospace company founded by Jeff Bezos. Develops and operates launch vehicles including the suborbital New Shepard (crewed tourism) and the orbital-class New Glenn heavy-lift rocket. Blue Origin has been commercially and technically outpaced by SpaceX's Falcon 9, Falcon Heavy, and Starship programs, though New Glenn was positioned as a serious competitor in the medium-to-heavy lift market.

## New Glenn — 2026 Setback

On May 29, 2026, **New Glenn exploded during a static fire test at LC-36** (Launch Complex 36, Cape Canaveral). The explosion was described as the most spectacular rocket launch-site failure since the Soviet N1 — a comparison that is not hyperbole given the N1's status as one of the largest uncontrolled explosions in space history. The **NG-4 mission is delayed indefinitely**.

Blue Origin's press statement described the event as an "anomaly" — a piece of aerospace corporate language that does considerable work when a rocket is visibly destroyed on a launch pad.

**Context:** SpaceX's Starship V3 had a mostly successful inaugural flight weeks earlier (noted in `newsletter-2026-05-20-to-05-25.md`), widening the development gap between the two programs.
