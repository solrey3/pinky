# Hard Boiled

**Director:** John Woo
**Genre:** Action, Crime
**Runtime:** 128 min
**Country:** Hong Kong

## Summary

Hong Kong cop Tequila (Chow Yun-fat) and undercover cop Tony (Tony Leung) independently infiltrate gun-smuggling operations run by the Triad villain Johnny Wong (Anthony Wong). Their paths collide in a hospital — a location Woo turns into a three-story war zone for the film's final 45-minute single-take-structured siege. A gunfight while holding a newborn baby is the film's moral image.

## Key Themes

- Honor among killers — Tony's loyalty tested between duty and personal code
- Ballistic choreography as dance: Woo's slow-motion gunplay is operatic grief
- The hospital as sacred space violated by violence — innocents as moral stakes
- Partnership and trust forged in combat
- Heroic bloodshed genre: death treated as tragedy, not spectacle

## Why It Matters

The peak of Hong Kong heroic bloodshed cinema. The two-minute hospital corridor scene — continuous tracking, gunfire, baby in arms — is one of the most technically demanding action sequences ever filmed. Chow Yun-fat and Tony Leung are both at their best. Woo's dual-pistol, slow-motion, dove-releasing aesthetic was so influential it became a cliché — which is a mark of genuine originality.
