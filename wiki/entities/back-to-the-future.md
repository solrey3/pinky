# Back to the Future

**Director:** Robert Zemeckis
**Genre:** Sci-fi, Comedy, Adventure
**Runtime:** 116 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Marty McFly (Michael J. Fox) is accidentally sent back to 1955 in a DeLorean time machine built by eccentric scientist Doc Brown (Christopher Lloyd). He inadvertently prevents his parents from meeting, must arrange for them to fall in love, and needs 1.21 gigawatts of power to get back. A nearly flawless screenplay that plants every setup three scenes before the payoff.

## Key Themes

- The past as constructed: your origin story is contingent, not inevitable
- Class aspiration and the American Dream — George McFly's transformation
- Oedipal anxiety played for comedy: Marty's mother wants to sleep with him
- Technology and hubris: Doc's invention causes every problem
- Small-town America in two eras — how much and how little changes

## Why It Matters

One of the most structurally perfect screenplays ever written by Zemeckis and Bob Gale. Every line of dialogue in the first 20 minutes pays off by the end. Originally shot with Eric Stoltz as Marty (six weeks of footage) before Fox replaced him. The DeLorean with gull-wing doors is cinema's most iconic prop vehicle. Steven Spielberg produced.
