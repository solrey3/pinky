# West Side Story

**Director:** Robert Wise, Jerome Robbins
**Genre:** Musical, Drama, Romance
**Runtime:** 152 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Romeo and Juliet in 1950s New York. The Jets (white) and Sharks (Puerto Rican) are rival gangs. Tony (Richard Beymer), a former Jet, falls for Maria (Natalie Wood), sister of Sharks leader Bernardo. The feud escalates to murder. Bernstein's score and Sondheim's lyrics, Robbins's choreography. Won 10 Academy Awards including Best Picture.

## Key Themes

- Immigration and racial anxiety — the Sharks are fighting for the same American Dream the Jets want to deny them
- Love as transcendence and its failure against structural hatred
- Dance as both communication and combat — 'America' is a full debate set to music
- The tragedy of the peace-seeker in a war culture: Tony's refusal to fight costs him everything
- The cycle of violence and the impossibility of individual love solving collective hatred

## Why It Matters

The stage musical that changed Broadway; the film that brought it to the world. Bernstein's score is one of music's masterworks — 'Maria,' 'Tonight,' 'America,' 'Somewhere,' 'Cool' are all standards. Robbins's choreography transforms street fighting into art without aestheticizing the violence. The casting controversies are real and documented; Spielberg's 2021 remake corrected several of them. The original stands as a cultural monument.
