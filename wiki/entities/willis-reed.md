# Willis Reed

The Knicks' greatest player and the source of the most emotionally resonant single moment in NBA Finals history.

**Born:** 1942, Hico LA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** C
**Active:** 1964–1974
**Personal NBA Tier:** Level 2

## Summary

The Knicks' greatest player and the source of the most emotionally resonant single moment in NBA Finals history. Reed's emergence from the tunnel for Game 7 of the 1970 Finals — limping, injured, unable to fully play — inspired the Knicks to the championship. Two championships, two Finals MVPs. The heart of the championship Knicks teams that defined New York City basketball.

## Highlights

- 2x NBA Champion (1970, 1973 New York Knicks); 2x Finals MVP
- His Game 7 tunnel walk (1970 Finals) is the most emotionally resonant moment in NBA Finals history
- 1970 NBA MVP; 7x All-Star
- His presence on the court was psychological — he couldn't fully play in Game 7 but his teammates could
- The 1969–1973 Knicks are the most intellectually sophisticated team in NBA history — coached by Red Holzman
