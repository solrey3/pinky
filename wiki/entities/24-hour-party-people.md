# 24 Hour Party People

**Director:** Michael Winterbottom
**Genre:** Biographical comedy-drama, Music
**Runtime:** 117 min
**Country:** UK

## Summary

Tony Wilson (Steve Coogan) founds Factory Records in Manchester after seeing the Sex Pistols in 1976 and proceeds to sign Joy Division, New Order, and the Happy Mondays — while running the Haçienda nightclub, managing chaos, and failing at business at a spectacular scale. The film acknowledges its own fictions and unreliabilities. Wilson winks at the camera throughout.

## Key Themes

- Cultural entrepreneurship: building scenes without business sense
- Manchester as a specific geography of post-industrial British culture
- The Factory Records ethos — art over commerce, to destructive excess
- Ian Curtis's genius and death as the shadow over everything
- Post-punk to Madchester: the musical and cultural lineage that made the 80s and 90s

## Why It Matters

The best music biopic ever made, largely because it refuses to be sincere about anything except the music. Coogan is brilliant as Wilson — vain, prescient, ridiculous, correct. The film acknowledges invented scenes while they are happening. Ian Curtis appears and disappears. Bernard Sumner's bass line in 'Love Will Tear Us Apart' plays and the film stops to let you feel it. Winterbottom's direction is loose and alive.
