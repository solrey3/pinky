# David Fincher

The most technically precise director in American cinema.

**Born:** 1962, Denver CO
**Nationality:** American
**Active:** 1990–present
**Profession:** Film director, producer

## Summary

The most technically precise director in American cinema. Fincher's films — Se7en, Fight Club, The Social Network, Zodiac, Gone Girl, Mank, The Killer — are defined by their meticulous visual control: precise camera movement, color grading as emotional language, and the use of digital compositing to create frames that look impossible. His television work (House of Cards, Mindhunter) extended his influence.

## Highlights

- Fight Club (1999) — in personal film rankings; the most technically accomplished psychological thriller of its era
- The Social Network (2010) — won multiple Oscars; the definitive document of Silicon Valley's founding mythology
- Zodiac (2007) — the most procedurally accurate true crime film in American cinema
- Se7en (1995) — redefined the serial killer genre; the most imitated visual style in crime film
- Mindhunter (2017–2019, Netflix) — the most rigorous procedural television in the streaming era
