# Chad Gable

**Real Name:** Charles Betts  
**Ring Name:** Chad Gable (also worked as "El Grande Americano" in 2026)  
**Nationality:** American  
**Born:** July 11, 1986, Colorado Springs, CO  
**Active:** 2012–present  
**Promotion:** WWE (SmackDown)  
**Background:** NCAA wrestling (college), Olympic alternate (freestyle wrestling, 2012)

## Summary

A technically gifted professional wrestler whose amateur wrestling background (NCAA Division I, 2012 Olympic alternate) gives him legitimate credibility in a business full of performance athletes. His mat work and European-style technical wrestling set him apart in an era dominated by high-fliers and brawlers.

Gable has cycled through multiple character iterations in WWE — the straightforward "Chad Gable" babyface, the "Shorty G" humiliation gimmick (one of WWE's less inspired experiments), and most recently the **El Grande Americano** masked character in early 2026, which required him to mock lucha libre culture while wearing a luchador mask. The character played against Rey Fenix and the lucha tradition.

## El Grande Americano Arc and Return (2026)

The El Grande Americano gimmick spent months working as a heel antagonist to the lucha libre community in WWE, mocking the tradition while masked. On **SmackDown from Bologna, Italy (June 5, 2026)**, Gable unmasked — revealing himself as El Grande Americano to a **monster pop** from the European crowd. The unmasking was accompanied by an in-ring apology to Rey Fenix for making a mockery of lucha libre, signaling the end of the heel run and the beginning of a redemption arc.

The Bologna pop was notable: a European crowd recognizing the babyface turn of a character who had been working heel, suggesting Gable's underlying goodwill with the audience survived the gimmick entirely intact. War Raiders also appeared alongside Gable in the segment.

This is the kind of WWE long-form storytelling that works when executed correctly: take a beloved worker, run him through a demeaning/misaligned character, let the audience miss who he really is, then return him to that identity as a genuine emotional moment.

The June 14 and June 15 dispatches keep Gable in the active weekly WWE feed by advertising **Chad Gable vs. Rusev** for Monday's Raw. That moves him from comeback segment into ordinary match-booking continuity.
