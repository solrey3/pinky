# Magnus Carlsen

Norwegian chess grandmaster and five-time World Chess Champion (2013–2023). Widely regarded as the greatest chess player in history — the highest peak Elo rating ever recorded, a world title relinquished voluntarily (not in defeat) in 2023 to focus on other competitions, and a cultural footprint that extends far beyond the chess world. In May 2026, Carlsen reportedly hit **5000 ELO** at Norway Chess 2026, defeating tournament leader Alireza Firouzja in Round 6 — a milestone that, if confirmed in final standings, represents a new outer limit for competitive chess performance.

## Notable

- Peak FIDE Elo pre-2026: ~2882 (highest rated player in history)
- The "5000 ELO" figure almost certainly refers to an internal Norway Chess scoring system (which uses a different scoring methodology than classical FIDE Elo, awarding bonus points for Armageddon wins), not standard FIDE Elo. Classical Elo cannot reach 5000 in any realistic sense. Context matters — Norway Chess uses a 3-1-0 scoring format with Armageddon tiebreaks that inflates displayed point totals.
- World Chess Champion 2013, 2014, 2016, 2018, 2021 — vacated the title rather than defend it against Nepomniachtchi's replacement in 2023.
- Dominant in blitz and rapid chess, which contributes to his overall competitive standing across formats.
