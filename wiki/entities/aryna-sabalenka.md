# Aryna Sabalenka

**Nationality:** Belarusian  
**Born:** 1998, Minsk  
**WTA Ranking:** No. 1 (as of May 2026)  
**Active:** 2015–present  
**Grand Slams:** Australian Open 2023, 2024, 2025; US Open 2023

## Summary

The current WTA world No. 1. A powerful, aggressive baseliner with one of the biggest serves in women's tennis. Sabalenka has been the most consistent player on tour for multiple years, winning the Australian Open in 2023, 2024, and 2025, and the 2023 US Open. Her game is built on big first-strike tennis — fast serves, heavy groundstrokes, dictating from the first ball. Her mental game, which was notably fragile earlier in her career (double-fault problems under pressure), has matured significantly alongside her technical improvement.

At the **2026 Roland Garros**, with Swiatek (the three-time defending champion) already eliminated in Round 4 by Kostyuk, Sabalenka is the clear new favorite entering the quarterfinals. On **June 2, 2026 (Round 4)**, she defeated **Naomi Osaka (16-seed) 7-5, 6-3** in a match that showed her transition from heavy-hitting to multi-dimensional clay-court dominance. The 6-3 second set after a competitive first set is the Sabalenka template: she absorbs early pressure and then escalates.
