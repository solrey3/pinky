# Moses Malone

The greatest offensive rebounder in NBA history and the player who made the Philadelphia 76ers' 1983 championship possible.

**Born:** 1955, Petersburg VA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** C
**Active:** 1974–1995
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 4 (#16)

## Summary

The greatest offensive rebounder in NBA history and the player who made the Philadelphia 76ers' 1983 championship possible. Fo, Fo, Fo — Moses's prediction that the Sixers would sweep three playoff series went one game wrong (they went 12-1). Three regular-season MVPs, one Finals MVP. He was the first player to go directly from high school to professional basketball (ABA, 1974).

## Highlights

- 3x NBA MVP (1979, 1982, 1983); 1x Finals MVP (1983)
- His 1983 Sixers led one of the most dominant playoff runs in NBA history (12-1)
- All-time offensive rebounds leader (6,731 career) — a record that will not be broken in the pace-and-space era
- Went directly from high school to the ABA in 1974 — the first to do so, breaking the precedent before it became common
- His partnership with Julius Erving (Dr. J) on the 1983 Sixers is the best interior-wing combination in NBA history
