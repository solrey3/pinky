# Wilt Chamberlain

The most physically dominant individual statistical force in NBA history.

**Born:** 1936, Philadelphia PA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** C
**Active:** 1959–1973
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 3 (#12)

## Summary

The most physically dominant individual statistical force in NBA history. 100 points in a single game. 50.4 points per game for an entire season. 55 rebounds in a single game. 22.9 rebounds per game for a career. Wilt's statistical record is a fact that does not require interpretation — it simply is. Two championships, four scoring titles, eleven rebounding titles, one assists title (because he decided to lead the league in assists for one year to prove he could).

## Highlights

- 100 points in a single game (March 2, 1962 vs. New York Knicks) — the most unbreakable record in sports
- 50.4 points per game for the 1961–62 season — another record that cannot be approached
- Career averages: 30.1 ppg, 22.9 rpg in 1045 games
- The only center to lead the NBA in assists for a season (1968)
- 2x NBA Champion (1967 76ers, 1972 Lakers); 4x MVP; 13x All-Star
