# Bill Russell

Eleven NBA championships in thirteen seasons — the most dominant winning record in team sports history.

**Born:** 1934, Monroe LA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** C
**Active:** 1956–1969
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 2 (#4)

## Summary

Eleven NBA championships in thirteen seasons — the most dominant winning record in team sports history. Russell's defensive philosophy — using shot-blocking and positioning to control the entire defensive end — invented the concept of the NBA center as a defensive anchor. The Celtics dynasty of 1957–1969 was built entirely around his presence.

## Highlights

- 11 NBA Championships in 13 seasons — the most in any major American sport by any player
- 5x NBA MVP
- 12x NBA All-Star; the defining defensive player of the pre-shot-block-statistic era
- Player-coach (1966–1969) — one of the first Black head coaches in major American professional sports
- His 1962 playoff performance against Wilt Chamberlain (50 rebounds in one game) is the most dominant individual defensive performance in history
