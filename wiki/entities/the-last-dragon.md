# The Last Dragon

**Director:** Michael Schultz
**Genre:** Martial arts, Comedy, Musical
**Runtime:** 109 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Leroy Green (Taimak), a young Black martial artist in Harlem who has studied kung fu his whole life, searches for the ultimate level of mastery — 'The Glow.' He falls for music video hostess Laura Charles (Vanity) and runs afoul of the villainous Sho'nuff, the Shogun of Harlem (Julius Carry). Produced by Berry Gordy, the film is part kung fu homage, part Motown musical, all NYC.

## Key Themes

- The Glow as inner mastery and self-belief — a metaphor for self-actualization
- Black American ownership of kung fu mythology and martial arts culture
- Masculinity, community pride, and Harlem identity in the 1980s
- The villain Sho'nuff as theatrical id: 'Who's the master?' is the film's central question
- Innocence and spiritual purity as actual competitive advantages

## Why It Matters

A cult classic with genuine heart. Taimak's earnestness is infectious. Julius Carry's Sho'nuff is one of cinema's most deliriously committed villain performances — the 'Shogun of Harlem' monologue alone earns the film a place in history. The Motown soundtrack is fantastic. The film occupies a unique space: too sincere to be camp, too absurd to be purely earnest, entirely its own thing.
