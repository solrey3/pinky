# Don Quixote

> "Sancho's spirit ascends from reality to illusion, while Don Quixote's declines from illusion to reality."
> — Salvador de Madariaga

## Overview

First modern novel and a foundational work of Western literature. A satire of chivalric romances about Alonso Quijano, a middle-aged hidalgo who imagines himself a knight-errant and sets out with his squire Sancho Panza to revive chivalry. Publication spanned a decade between Parts One and Two, enabling metafictional depth where characters had read Part One.

## SUMMARY

Miguel de Cervantes' *Don Quixote* follows Alonso Quijano, who adopts chivalry, travels with squire Sancho Panza, and explores the tension between human idealism and harsh reality.

### FACTS

- *Don Quixote* is widely considered by literary scholars to be the very first modern European novel.
- Miguel de Cervantes published the first part in 1605; Part Two followed ten years later in 1615.
- The novel remains one of the most translated and best-selling books in all of human history.
- The term *quixotic* entered the English language as a direct result of this literary work.
- Cide Hamete Benengeli is presented as the fictional Moorish historian who wrote the original Arabic text.
- The novel serves as a direct satire of the chivalric romances popular during the sixteenth century.
- Barcelona serves as a key setting where the protagonist finally meets his defeat.
- Alonso Quijano is the protagonist's original name before he assumes his chivalric knight identity.
- Rocinante is the name of the old, weary workhorse that accompanies the knight on his journey.
- Aldonza Lorenzo is the real-world woman upon whom the knight projects the ideal of his Dulcinea.
- The Knight of the White Moon is the rival who defeats the protagonist in his final battle.
- The library of the protagonist is purged by his niece, housekeeper, curate, and the local barber.
- Many well-known authors have labeled this book the best and most central work in all literature.
