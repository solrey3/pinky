# SUMMARY

Modern Poker Theory by Michael Acevedo explores game-theory-optimal poker strategies using solvers, mathematical models, and equilibrium concepts for improved decision-making.

# IDEAS

- Game theory optimal play makes you unexploitable regardless of what strategy your opponents choose to use.
- Solvers calculate equilibrium strategies by having two AI versions play billions of hands against each other iteratively.
- Nash equilibrium in poker means neither player can improve results by unilaterally changing their own strategy.
- Mixed strategies require randomizing between actions at specific frequencies to remain balanced and completely unexploitable overall.
- Minimum defense frequency prevents opponents from profitably bluffing you by forcing enough calls against their bets.
- The indifference principle means optimal strategy makes opponents equally profitable whether they call, fold, or raise.
- Pot odds directly determine whether calling a bet is mathematically profitable based on equity versus cost.
- GTO play serves as a baseline strategy that can be deviated from against weaker exploitable opponents.
- Blockers influence optimal decisions because holding certain cards reduces the probability opponents hold specific strong hands.
- Range advantage occurs when one player's overall hand distribution is stronger on a particular board texture.
- Nut advantage means one player can have more very strong hands than their opponent on specific boards.
- Position provides enormous strategic advantage because acting last gives you more information before making decisions.
- Bet sizing in equilibrium depends on polarization with larger bets used for more polarized range compositions.
- Geometric bet sizing spreads bets across multiple streets to efficiently get all chips in by the river.
- Overbetting becomes optimal when one player has significant nut advantage and their opponent lacks strong hands.
- Check-raising from out of position compensates for the informational disadvantage of acting first on every street.
- Continuation betting frequency depends heavily on range advantage, nut advantage, and the specific board texture encountered.
- Equity denial motivates small bets that prevent opponents from freely realizing their equity with marginal hands.
- Solver outputs reveal that humans systematically deviate from optimal play in predictable and exploitable patterns overall.
- Multi-way pots require significantly tighter ranges because the probability someone holds a strong hand increases dramatically.
- Preflop raising ranges are constructed considering position, stack depth, and the actions of previous players carefully.
- Three-betting ranges should contain a balanced mix of value hands and strategically selected bluff combinations always.
- ICM pressure in tournaments changes optimal strategy by making chip preservation more valuable than chip accumulation.
- Short stack play simplifies decisions dramatically because fewer streets of betting reduce the complexity of strategy.
- Flop texture categorization helps players quickly identify whether to bet frequently or check at high rates.
- Equity realization measures how effectively a hand converts its raw equity into actual chips won overall.
- Dominated hands perform poorly because they win less often than their raw equity would initially suggest.
- Range morphology describes how a player's range composition changes across flop, turn, and river streets progressively.
