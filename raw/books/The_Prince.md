FACTS
- The Prince was written in 1513 during Machiavelli's exile period.
- Machiavelli served as diplomat for the Florentine Republic earlier.
- The book was dedicated to Lorenzo de Medici for favor.
- It was published posthumously in 1532 after his death.
- The work focuses primarily on principalities rather than republics.
- Cesare Borgia serves as a key example throughout the text.
- Machiavelli viewed Christianity as weakening Roman martial virtues.
- The treatise influenced modern political realism significantly later.
- It separates ethics from politics in practical advice given.
- Renaissance Italy featured frequent power shifts among city states.
- Fortuna represents unpredictable external forces in human affairs.
- Virtù refers to personal excellence and political effectiveness combined.
- The book was placed on the papal index of prohibited books.
- It contains twenty six chapters of varying lengths total.
- Historical examples draw heavily from Roman and Greek sources.

REFERENCES
