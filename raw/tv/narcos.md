# Narcos (2015–2017)

**Creator:** Chris Brancato, Carlo Bernard, Doug Miro
**Network:** Netflix
**Genre:** Crime drama, Biographical drama
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #51 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The rise and fall of Pablo Escobar (Wagner Moura) and the Medellín Cartel, narrated by DEA agent Steve Murphy (Boyd Holbrook). Three seasons cover Escobar (Seasons 1–2) and then the Cali Cartel (Season 3). Based on documented events. Escobar's charisma and brutality are held in the same frame without romanticizing either.

## Key Themes

- The drug war as a structural failure: the DEA's victories are tactical, the system is strategic
- Escobar's populism: his genuine support among Colombia's poor and his weaponization of it
- Moral cost of the pursuit: Murphy and his partner's own lines are blurred
- The narco state: when the criminal enterprise is as powerful as the government, what is the government
- Wagner Moura's Escobar: a performance that refuses the easy villain

## Why It Matters

Wagner Moura's Escobar is the most compelling crime drama performance in Spanish-language television. The show's use of actual archival footage is forensic. The production's genuine shooting in Colombia adds authenticity no studio set could provide. The show helped establish Netflix's prestige drama credentials internationally. The real Steve Murphy and Javier Peña served as consultants.
