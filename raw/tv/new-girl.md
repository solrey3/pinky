# New Girl (2011–2018)

**Creator:** Elizabeth Meriwether
**Network:** Fox
**Genre:** Sitcom, Romantic comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #27 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Zooey Deschanel's Jessica Day moves in with three male roommates — Nick Miller (Jake Johnson), Schmidt (Max Greenfield), and Winston Bishop (Lamorne Morris) — after a breakup. The show is ostensibly a quirky romantic comedy but is genuinely excellent character comedy, particularly Schmidt's season-long redemption arcs and the Nick/Jess will-they/won't-they.

## Key Themes

- Adulthood and its discontents — the loft is a holding pattern between youth and responsibility
- Schmidt as the show's comedic engine: his self-consciousness, his ambition, his genuine heart
- The sitcom apartment as a utopia that real life keeps threatening to end
- Winston's increasingly elaborate bit-based humor as the show's secret weapon
- Nick and Jess as a romantic pair who work better than they should on paper

## Why It Matters

An underrated comedy that found its voice in Season 2. Max Greenfield's Schmidt performance is one of the most committed comedic acts of the decade — every line is delivered like his life depends on it. Lamorne Morris's Winston bits (particularly the pranks arc) are quietly brilliant. The show ran long enough to find all its characters' depths and the ensemble became genuinely warm.
