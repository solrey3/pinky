# Deadwood (2004–2006)

**Creator:** David Milch
**Network:** HBO
**Genre:** Western drama
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #55 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The Black Hills mining camp of Deadwood, South Dakota, 1876–1877. Sheriff Seth Bullock (Timothy Olyphant) arrives to open a hardware store. Saloon owner Al Swearengen (Ian McShane) runs the camp's informal power structure. Wild Bill Hickok (Keith Carradine) arrives and is shot in the back. The show is a meditation on how civilization is built from violence, compromise, and profanity.

## Key Themes

- The camp becoming a town: civilization as an emergent property of self-interested cooperation
- Al Swearengen as the most complete character in Western television — villain, politician, philosopher
- Milch's Shakespearean language: the anachronistic formality of the dialogue as a stylistic statement
- Power and legitimacy: who has it, who confers it, and what the answer reveals about both
- The profanity: historically anachronistic but thematically accurate — the show translates period vulgarity

## Why It Matters

Ian McShane's Al Swearengen is the greatest character in Western television — a man who runs a brothel, commits murder for expedience, and is also the most pragmatic and honest figure in Deadwood. The dialogue is written in a heightened register that has no historical precedent but feels absolutely right. Cancelled by HBO after three seasons. The 2019 movie gave it partial closure. Milch was diagnosed with Alzheimer's during the film's production.
