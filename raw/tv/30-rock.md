# 30 Rock (2006–2013)

**Creator:** Tina Fey
**Network:** NBC
**Genre:** Sitcom, Satirical comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #26 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Liz Lemon (Tina Fey) is head writer of a fictional NBC sketch comedy show managed by network executive Jack Donaghy (Alec Baldwin) and starring narcissistic Tracy Jordan (Tracy Morgan) and delusional Jenna Maroney (Jane Krakowski). A show about the television industry making fun of itself with a joke-per-minute rate that rivals Arrested Development.

## Key Themes

- Television as an industrial product that occasionally produces art
- Jack Donaghy as the most self-aware corporate villain in sitcom history
- Liz Lemon's feminism vs. her actual behavior — the show's most honest joke is about its creator
- Kenneth Parcell as the eternal optimist — ageless, innocent, and possibly immortal
- The GE/Comcast corporate ecosystem as a permanent backdrop for petty human drama

## Why It Matters

Won the Outstanding Comedy Series Emmy for three consecutive years (2007–2009). Alec Baldwin's Jack Donaghy is a comedic masterclass — every scene is perfectly calibrated. The joke density rivals *Arrested Development*. The show was never a ratings success and always critical darling — the gap between the two is itself a 30 Rock joke. Tina Fey's willingness to make Liz Lemon the butt of the show's feminist critique is sophisticated.
