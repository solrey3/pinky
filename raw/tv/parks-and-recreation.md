# Parks and Recreation (2009–2015)

**Creator:** Greg Daniels, Michael Schur
**Network:** NBC
**Genre:** Mockumentary sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #21 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Deputy director of the Pawnee, Indiana Parks Department Leslie Knope (Amy Poehler) fights bureaucracy, incompetence, and a deeply apathetic citizenry to do good things for her community. With Ron Swanson (Nick Offerman), Tom Haverford (Aziz Ansari), Ann Perkins (Rashida Jones), Ben Wyatt (Adam Scott), April Ludgate (Aubrey Plaza), and Chris Traeger (Rob Lowe). One of the warmest comedies ever made.

## Key Themes

- Optimism as praxis: Leslie Knope believes government can do good and acts accordingly
- Ron Swanson's libertarianism as a foil that the show ultimately defeats through kindness
- The ensemble as a genuine family — the show is about people who make each other better
- Pawnee, Indiana as a satirical portrait of American civic dysfunction
- Leslie and Ben as the show's moral center: love and ambition can coexist

## Why It Matters

The antidote to cynicism — a show that believes in institutions, community, and the possibility of doing good work. Nick Offerman's Ron Swanson is one of the great comedy characters of the era. Amy Poehler's Leslie Knope is a genuinely heroic character treated with comedy rather than sentimentality. The *Harvest Festival* arc (Season 3) is the show's peak. 'A government is just a word for the things we do together.'
