# The Office (US) (2005–2013)

**Creator:** Greg Daniels
**Network:** NBC
**Genre:** Mockumentary sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #23 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The Scranton, Pennsylvania branch of Dunder Mifflin Paper Company, filmed as a mockumentary. Regional manager Michael Scott (Steve Carell) is oblivious, occasionally profound, and deeply human. Jim (John Krasinski) and Pam (Jenna Fischer) are the romantic center. Dwight Schrute (Rainn Wilson) is the show's greatest comedic creation. The first two seasons adapt the British original; Seasons 3–5 are arguably better.

## Key Themes

- The workplace as a site of genuine connection despite its absurdity
- Michael Scott's need for love and his catastrophic methods of seeking it
- Jim and Pam as the audience's surrogate — and the show's eventual interrogation of that comfort
- Dwight Schrute as an authoritarian who would thrive in any century except his own
- The mockumentary format's dual consciousness: characters performing for the camera they acknowledge

## Why It Matters

Steve Carell's Michael Scott is one of the great comedic performances in TV history — cringe comedy as an expression of genuine pathos. 'Dinner Party' (Season 4) is the platonic ideal of the cringe episode. Dwight and Jim's rivalry generates some of the best physical comedy in American television. The show's move to streaming (Netflix) introduced it to a new generation and made it the most-watched show on the platform for years.
