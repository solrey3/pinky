# Mad Men (2007–2015)

**Creator:** Matthew Weiner
**Network:** AMC
**Genre:** Period drama
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #30 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Set at the fictional Sterling Cooper advertising agency in New York City, 1960–1970. Don Draper (Jon Hamm), creative director, is a man who literally stole his identity and has spent his life in fear of exposure. The show uses the advertising industry to examine how American culture sells itself to itself — and how Don is the ultimate product of that process.

## Key Themes

- Identity as a performance and the exhaustion of maintaining it — Don Draper is Dick Whitman
- The 1960s as a decade of colliding moral certainties: the show tracks every rupture
- Advertising as the art of making people feel inadequate then selling them a solution
- Peggy Olson's arc: the only character who actually grows — from secretary to creative director
- The finale's Coca-Cola moment: does Don find peace, or does he just make an ad

## Why It Matters

Won Outstanding Drama Series four consecutive years. Jon Hamm's Don Draper is the defining antihero of the early prestige TV era. Weiner's attention to period detail is forensic. The show's visual language — color, costume, composition — is among the most deliberate in television history. Peggy Olson and Joan Holloway are two of the most fully realized female characters in the medium. The finale is ambiguous and perfect.
