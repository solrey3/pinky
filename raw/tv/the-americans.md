# The Americans (2013–2018)

**Creator:** Joe Weisberg
**Network:** FX
**Genre:** Spy drama, Thriller
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #37 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Elizabeth (Keri Russell) and Philip Jennings (Matthew Rhys) are KGB agents living as an American married couple in suburban Washington, D.C. during the Cold War (1981–1987). They have two American-born children who don't know their parents are Soviet spies. Their neighbor Stan Beeman (Noah Emmerich) is an FBI counterintelligence agent. The show is about marriage, ideology, and the self-destruction of true believers.

## Key Themes

- The marriage as mission: Elizabeth and Philip's relationship is the show's emotional core
- Ideology as identity: what happens to the true believer when the ideology fails
- Philip's crisis of faith: he sees America clearly and is losing the conviction to fight it
- Elizabeth's conviction: her commitment never wavers and it costs her everything
- The finale: the most emotionally devastating season finale in prestige TV history

## Why It Matters

The most underrated drama of the prestige TV era. Keri Russell and Matthew Rhys's performances are both Emmy-winning caliber for the entire run (Rhys won for Season 6). The garage scene in the finale is one of the most emotionally precise sequences in TV history. Creator Joe Weisberg was a former CIA officer — the tradecraft is real. The show's willingness to make its protagonists genuinely sympathetic while they do genuinely terrible things is the tightest moral wire in TV.
