# Billions (2016–2023)

**Creator:** Brian Koppelman, David Levien, Andrew Ross Sorkin
**Network:** Showtime
**Genre:** Drama, Finance thriller
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #46 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

US Attorney Chuck Rhoades (Paul Giamatti) pursues hedge fund billionaire Bobby Axelrod (Damian Lewis) across six seasons of legal maneuvering, financial strategy, and ego warfare in New York. The show is a premium cable drama about finance that takes the mechanics seriously while using them as a backdrop for character conflict. Maggie Siff's Wendy Rhoades is the show's most interesting figure.

## Key Themes

- Power as the only commodity — money is how power is measured, not the goal itself
- Performance of dominance: every interaction is a status game
- Wendy Rhoades as performance coach, therapist, and moral compass caught between two alpha males
- The legal/illegal line as the show's structural tension — who is actually the criminal
- New York's financial world as a specific cultural ecosystem with its own rules

## Why It Matters

Paul Giamatti and Damian Lewis's scenes together are some of the best acted exchanges in Showtime history. The show takes hedge fund mechanics seriously enough that financial journalists watch it for the plots. Andrew Ross Sorkin's involvement (he covers Wall Street for the NYT) gives the financial plotting credibility. The show ran longer than it should have but the first three seasons are a tight financial thriller.
