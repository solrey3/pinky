# All in the Family (1971–1979)

**Creator:** Norman Lear
**Network:** CBS
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #58 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Archie Bunker (Carroll O'Connor) is a bigoted, conservative, working-class man in Queens, New York, whose son-in-law Mike Stivic (Rob Reiner) is a liberal graduate student. The show tackled racism, feminism, homosexuality, the Vietnam War, and political polarization in the sitcom format — something American television had never done. Archie's bigotry is both lampooned and given human dimension.

## Key Themes

- The bigot as a fully human character — Archie's prejudice is ignorance, not evil
- The culture war made intimate: the generation gap within one household
- Norman Lear's use of comedy as a vehicle for topics television had avoided entirely
- Working-class authenticity: Archie's world is economically specific, not a TV fantasy
- The Bunker household as a microcosm of American political division

## Why It Matters

Five consecutive Emmy wins for Outstanding Comedy Series. Carroll O'Connor's Archie Bunker is one of the great creations in American television — a man who is wrong about almost everything and whose wrongness is human and recognizable rather than monstrous. Norman Lear changed what American sitcoms were permitted to say. The show spawned *The Jeffersons*, *Maude*, and *Good Times* — an entire progressive comedy ecosystem.
