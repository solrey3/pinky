# Arrested Development (2003–2019)

**Creator:** Mitch Hurwitz
**Network:** Fox / Netflix
**Genre:** Sitcom, Comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #17 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The Bluth family — wealthy real estate developers whose father Michael Bluth (Jason Bateman) tries to keep together after patriarch George Sr. (Jeffrey Tambor) is arrested for fraud. Tobias Fünke (David Cross), Gob (Will Arnett), Lucille (Jessica Walter), Lindsay (Portia de Rossi), George Michael (Michael Cera), and Maeby (Alia Shawkat) complete one of the densest ensembles in sitcom history. The show rewards rewatching obsessively.

## Key Themes

- The density of callbacks — every joke was planted episodes earlier, the show demands repeat viewings
- Wealth and its insulation from consequences — the Bluths are disasters who face no reckoning
- Tobias Fünke as the show's purest comic engine: the unconscious double entendres are operatic
- Michael as the straight man who is not actually better than anyone else
- The narrator (Ron Howard) as an active participant in the comedy — omniscient and delighted

## Why It Matters

The greatest single-camera sitcom ever made by almost every critical measure. The joke density per minute is unmatched. The Season 1 chicken impression running gag is a perfect recurring joke. Will Arnett's Gob is one of the great comedic creations of the 2000s. The show was cancelled by Fox after three seasons, was saved by Netflix for a fourth (diminished) season, and returned for a fifth. The original three seasons are the work; the rest is epilogue.
