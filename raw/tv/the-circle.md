# The Circle (2018–present)

**Creator:** Tim Harcourt
**Network:** Channel 4 / Netflix
**Genre:** Reality competition, Social media simulation
**Category:** Reality Television
**Rank:** #6 (Reality Television)

## Summary

Contestants live in separate apartments and can only communicate through a social media-style platform called The Circle. Players can be themselves or catfish as another person. The most popular players become 'Influencers' and vote to block the least popular. The winner receives a cash prize. The show is about authenticity in social media performance.

## Key Themes

- Authenticity vs. performance in digital social spaces
- Catfishing as strategy: the disconnect between presented and real identity
- The popularity economy: The Circle gamifies the logic of social media followers
- Isolation and connection mediated entirely through text
- The moment of reveal: when catfish meet their marks

## Why It Matters

The show that most precisely gamifies the psychology of social media. The catfishing episodes interrogate assumptions about identity and presentation. The Netflix international versions (US, France, Brazil, Korea) function as cross-cultural experiments in what 'authenticity' means in different social contexts. The format's insight — that social media is itself a competition — arrived exactly when it was most culturally resonant.
