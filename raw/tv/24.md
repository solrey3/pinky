# 24 (2001–2014)

**Creator:** Joel Surnow, Robert Cochran
**Network:** Fox
**Genre:** Action, Political thriller
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #43 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Each season takes place over 24 consecutive hours in the life of CTU agent Jack Bauer (Kiefer Sutherland). Real-time format. The show premiered seven weeks after 9/11 and became the defining post-9/11 action drama: torture works, the ticking time bomb is real, civil liberties are an obstacle to survival. The show's politics were explicitly debated in the US Army and West Point.

## Key Themes

- The ticking time bomb scenario as both narrative device and torture-justification propaganda
- Jack Bauer as a man who does terrible things for good reasons — the post-9/11 fantasy
- Real-time structure as tension generator: every minute matters because it is a minute
- CTU's institutional dysfunction as both obstacle and bureaucratic satire
- The cost of the Jack Bauer model: the show eventually interrogates its own thesis

## Why It Matters

Sutherland won the Emmy and Golden Globe. The real-time format is one of TV's most successful gimmicks-that-became-a-feature. The show's influence on US torture policy debates was documented: Jane Mayer's *The Dark Side* reported military interrogators citing Bauer as a model. It's the rare TV show that directly entered a policy debate. Later seasons lost coherence but Seasons 1, 2, and 5 are first-rate thriller television.
