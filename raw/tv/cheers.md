# Cheers (1982–1993)

**Creator:** Glen Charles, Les Charles, James Burrows
**Network:** NBC
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #24 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

A bar in Boston where 'everybody knows your name.' Former baseball pitcher Sam Malone (Ted Danson) owns the bar; Diane Chambers (Shelley Long), then Rebecca Howe (Kirstie Alley), works there. The ensemble — Cliff Clavin (John Ratzenberger), Norm Peterson (George Wendt), Carla Tortelli (Rhea Perlman), Coach (Nicholas Colasanto), Frasier Crane (Kelsey Grammer), Woody Boyd (Woody Harrelson) — is one of the great ensemble casts in television history.

## Key Themes

- The bar as third place — between work and home, the space where community is built
- Sam and Diane as the will-they/won't-they template that every subsequent sitcom copied
- Class and aspiration: Cheers attracts people running away from or toward something
- The wisdom of the bar stool: Norm and Cliff's pseudo-profound pronouncements
- Frasier Crane as the show's intellectual pretension — the character so good they spun him off

## Why It Matters

11 seasons, 111 Emmy nominations, 28 wins. The show that defined the '80s sitcom and the template for workplace-as-community comedy. The finale (98.5 million viewers) remains one of the most-watched series finales in television history. Kelsey Grammer's Frasier is so complete a character that his spinoff ran 11 seasons. The theme song ('Where Everybody Knows Your Name') is the most comforting piece of television music ever written.
