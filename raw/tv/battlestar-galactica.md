# Battlestar Galactica (2004–2009)

**Creator:** Ronald D. Moore
**Network:** Sci-Fi Channel
**Genre:** Sci-fi drama
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #7 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The reimagined series: humanity is nearly wiped out by the Cylons — human-created robots who evolved into humanoid form and now believe in God. The survivors, led by Commander Adama (Edward James Olmos) and President Roslin (Mary McDonnell), flee in a ragtag fleet toward Earth. The show is an allegory for the post-9/11 world: terrorism, occupation, torture, religion, and what it means to be human.

## Key Themes

- What makes us human — the Cylons' spiritual awakening challenges every easy answer
- Terrorism and occupation seen from both sides simultaneously: who is the insurgent, who is the occupier
- Religion and prophecy as political tools and genuine transcendence in tension
- Military command and the moral costs of necessary decisions
- The cycle of violence and the possibility of breaking it — 'All of this has happened before'

## Why It Matters

The best science fiction television of the 2000s and one of the most sustained allegories for post-9/11 America ever produced. The Season 2 finale (New Caprica) and Season 3 premiere (occupation) are among the ballsiest narrative decisions in prestige TV. Edward James Olmos and Mary McDonnell are both remarkable. The finale is divisive — the 'it was God' answer is legitimately polarizing — but the journey earns the ambiguity.
