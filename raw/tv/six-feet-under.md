# Six Feet Under (2001–2005)

**Creator:** Alan Ball
**Network:** HBO
**Genre:** Drama
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #54 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The Fisher family runs a funeral home in Los Angeles. Patriarch Nathaniel (Richard Jenkins) dies in the opening episode and returns throughout as a ghost/conscience. Nate (Peter Krause), David (Michael C. Hall), Ruth (Frances Conroy), and Claire (Lauren Ambrose) deal with grief, identity, sexuality, and mortality. The show begins and ends every episode with a death. The finale is the best single episode of television of the 2000s.

## Key Themes

- Death as an organizing principle: the show forces confrontation with mortality in every episode
- David Fisher's arc: gay identity, religious faith, and the integration of both
- The family as a site of both love and damage — the Fishers wound each other with care
- Grief as a permanent condition rather than a passage
- The finale: every character's death shown in a single sequence — the fullest realization of the show's thesis

## Why It Matters

The series finale — Claire driving away while the show cuts forward to every surviving character's death — is the greatest single episode of any television drama. Michael C. Hall went from this to *Dexter*. Frances Conroy's Ruth is one of TV's great maternal performances. Alan Ball created *True Blood* after this; the gap in quality is instructive about what he was working with here.
