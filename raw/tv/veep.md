# Veep (2012–2019)

**Creator:** Armando Iannucci, David Mandel
**Network:** HBO
**Genre:** Political satire, Comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #38 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Selina Meyer (Julia Louis-Dreyfus) is the Vice President, then President, then ex-President of the United States. Her staff — Gary (Tony Hale), Amy (Anna Chlumsky), Dan (Reid Scott), Mike (Matt Walsh), Jonah (Timothy Simons) — are incompetent, self-serving, and occasionally brilliant. The show is a brutal portrait of American political ambition as pure ego and zero principle.

## Key Themes

- Political ambition as a personality disorder — every character sacrifices everything for nothing
- The staffers' self-abasement: Gary Walsh's complete subordination of self to Selina
- Power as the only currency in Washington — policy is irrelevant to every character
- The insult comedy: Iannucci's writers created an escalating insult vocabulary that is its own art form
- The finale's time jump: Selina achieves her goal and dies alone, succeeded by Jonah

## Why It Matters

Julia Louis-Dreyfus won six consecutive Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series Emmys — the most consecutive Emmy wins for a performer in the same role in history. The show's insult writing is among the funniest in television. 'Jonah, you are a human-shaped void' — the insults are carefully constructed to be both funny and precisely accurate. The finale (Selina dying at Tom Hanks's funeral, succeeded by Jonah Ryan) is the blackest possible comedy ending.
