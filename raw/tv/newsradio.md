# NewsRadio (1995–1999)

**Creator:** Paul Simms
**Network:** NBC
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #16 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The ensemble staff of fictional New York radio station WNYX: station manager Dave Nelson (Dave Foley), eccentric billionaire owner Jimmy James (Stephen Root), reporter Lisa Miller (Maura Tierney), anchor Bill McNeal (Phil Hartman), and news director Catherine Duke (Khandi Alexander), plus Andy Dick and Vicki Lewis. A show about competent, intelligent people being idiots, shot through with genuine warmth.

## Key Themes

- Workplace as ecosystem: the ensemble's interdependence and mutual dysfunction
- Phil Hartman's Bill McNeal as the anchor of the anchor — pompous, hilarious, strangely lovable
- Jimmy James: the eccentric billionaire as anti-authority authority figure
- The ensemble comedy's demand for equal distribution of funny — every cast member has a lane
- The Season 5 pivot after Hartman's death: how a show processes genuine grief through fiction

## Why It Matters

The most underrated sitcom of the 1990s. Phil Hartman's death in 1998 robbed the show of its center and effectively ended its run. The Season 5 tribute episode ('Bill Apologizes') is one of the most moving things in sitcom history. Stephen Root's Jimmy James — 'I'm a millionaire; I don't need to make sense' — is a perfectly constructed comedy character. The show was never a hit and never deserved the obscurity it received.
