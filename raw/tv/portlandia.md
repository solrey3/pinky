# Portlandia (2011–2018)

**Creator:** Fred Armisen, Carrie Brownstein, Jonathan Krisel
**Network:** IFC
**Genre:** Sketch comedy, Satire
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #45 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Fred Armisen and Carrie Brownstein play dozens of Portland, Oregon characters in interconnected sketches satirizing hipster culture, progressive self-satisfaction, and the boutique politics of the Pacific Northwest. 'Put a bird on it.' The dream of the 90s is alive in Portland. 'Is it local?' The show was beloved by the culture it mocked and the culture that mocked that culture simultaneously.

## Key Themes

- The self-defeating logic of progressive authenticity seeking
- Portlandia's Portland as a city that takes everything too seriously and nothing seriously enough
- Niche culture and the comedy of extreme specificity — every sketch is about one very particular type
- Armisen and Brownstein's chemistry: the show needed two performers willing to commit completely
- Gentrification, artisanal culture, and the politics of the local

## Why It Matters

Carrie Brownstein (of Sleater-Kinney) became a major comedic talent here. The show's satirical targets were more specific and more affectionate than most sketch comedy — it was made by people who partially lived in the culture they mocked. 'Put a Bird on It' and 'Dream of the 90s' became cultural shorthand. Brownstein's book *Hunger Makes Me a Modern Girl* is an essential companion text.
