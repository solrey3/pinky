# Star Trek (1966–1969)

**Creator:** Gene Roddenberry
**Network:** NBC
**Genre:** Sci-fi, Adventure
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #48 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The original series. Captain James T. Kirk (William Shatner), Mr. Spock (Leonard Nimoy), and Dr. Leonard 'Bones' McCoy (DeForest Kelley) aboard the USS Enterprise. Three seasons of science fiction that consistently used the genre to address race relations, the Cold War, nuclear anxiety, Vietnam, and what humanity could become if it survived its worst impulses.

## Key Themes

- The Kirk/Spock/McCoy triad as id/superego/ego — the complete rational-emotional-moral human
- The Prime Directive: non-interference as a principle immediately violated in every episode that tests it
- The multicultural crew as a statement: Uhura, Sulu, Chekov, and Scotty as the integrated future
- Boldly going: exploration as a moral imperative — curiosity as a virtue
- The alien as mirror: every alien culture examines something about humanity

## Why It Matters

The show that launched one of the most successful science fiction franchises in history and proved that genre television could carry serious ideas. Nichelle Nichols as Uhura was reportedly about to quit until Martin Luther King Jr. told her that the role was too important to leave. The interracial kiss between Kirk and Uhura was a first for American television. Everything *Trek* — the Next Generation, Deep Space Nine, all of it — descended from here.
