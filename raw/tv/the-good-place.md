# The Good Place (2016–2020)

**Creator:** Michael Schur
**Network:** NBC
**Genre:** Fantasy sitcom, Philosophical comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #44 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Eleanor Shellstrop (Kristen Bell) accidentally ends up in the Good Place after death — a heavenly afterlife designed by architect Michael (Ted Danson). The show is a philosophy class disguised as a network sitcom, covering Kant, Scanlon's contractualism, Chidi's ethical frameworks, and the trolley problem across four seasons of genuine moral seriousness deployed as comedy.

## Key Themes

- Moral philosophy made genuinely accessible — the show assigned academic papers by Scanlon and Kierkegaard
- The premise twist in the Season 1 finale: one of the greatest single episode reveals in network television
- Growth as the show's thesis: people can become better, but only if they choose to
- Ted Danson's Michael as the show's philosophical heart: a being who chooses ethics when he doesn't have to
- The finale's argument: the good place is meaningless without an ending — a defense of finite existence

## Why It Matters

Michael Schur assigned the cast *What We Owe to Each Other* by T.M. Scanlon. The Season 1 finale reveal is the rare twist that recontextualizes everything that came before without cheapening it. Ted Danson's performance is one of the great comic-dramatic turns of the decade. The finale's argument that endless paradise would be meaningless is a genuinely philosophical claim made persuasively through comedy.
