# In Living Color (1990–1994)

**Creator:** Keenen Ivory Wayans
**Network:** Fox
**Genre:** Sketch comedy, Variety
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #19 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Keenen Ivory Wayans's sketch comedy show featuring the Wayans family (Damon, Shawn, Kim, Marlon), Jim Carrey, Jamie Foxx, Jennifer Lopez (as a Fly Girl dancer), and Tommy Davidson. The show was the first Black-controlled sketch comedy series on American TV to achieve major commercial success — and it achieved it by being genuinely funnier than *Saturday Night Live* for most of its run.

## Key Themes

- Black American cultural ownership of comedy in a mainstream format
- The Wayans family as a comedic collective — the ensemble chemistry is genetic
- Homey D. Clown, Fire Marshall Bill, Wanda: recurring characters that became cultural shorthand
- Jim Carrey's physical comedy pushed to its rubber-faced extreme — his breakthrough
- The Fly Girls as choreographic talent deployed as entertainment and as a statement

## Why It Matters

Jim Carrey, Jamie Foxx, and Jennifer Lopez all broke through here. The show ran during the halftime show of Super Bowl XXVI, directly competing with the halftime show in a programming stunt. 'Homey D. Clown' and 'Men on Film' are sketches that have outlived their era. Keenen Ivory Wayans built a comedy institution from scratch on a network (Fox) that was itself a scrappy outsider. The show is the direct predecessor of the Wayans family's film output.
