# Community (2009–2015)

**Creator:** Dan Harmon
**Network:** NBC / Yahoo Screen
**Genre:** Sitcom, Postmodern comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #11 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

A disgraced lawyer (Jeff Winger, Joel McHale) enrolls at Greendale Community College and forms a study group with Britta (Gillian Jacobs), Abed (Danny Pudi), Troy (Donald Glover), Shirley (Yvette Nicole Brown), Annie (Alison Brie), and Pierce (Chevy Chase). Dan Harmon's show operates simultaneously as a sincere character comedy and a meta-analysis of sitcom conventions, genre film, and the nature of storytelling itself.

## Key Themes

- Abed and the meta-layer: he understands reality through TV tropes and he's not entirely wrong
- The study group as a found family — broken people who are better together than apart
- Genre episodes as character revelation: the paintball wars, the Dungeons & Dragons episode, the claymation Christmas
- Greendale as a place for people the world has discarded — community college as second chance
- Dan Harmon's story circle and the show's relationship to its own structure

## Why It Matters

The show for people who watch a lot of TV, made by someone who watched too much of it. The two paintball episodes are perfect genre pastiches. 'Modern Warfare' is the best 22-minute comedy episode of the 2000s. Donald Glover's Troy is one of the great comedic performances of the decade. 'Six seasons and a movie' became a rallying cry — the show was saved by fan pressure multiple times. Harmon's firing and rehiring added a Season 4 artifact of what the show is without him.
