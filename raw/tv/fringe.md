# Fringe (2008–2013)

**Creator:** J.J. Abrams, Alex Kurtzman, Roberto Orci
**Network:** Fox
**Genre:** Sci-fi drama, Procedural
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #29 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

FBI agent Olivia Dunham (Anna Torv) investigates 'fringe science' events with eccentric scientist Walter Bishop (John Noble) and his son Peter (Joshua Jackson). The show begins as a monster-of-the-week procedural and expands into a mythology of parallel universes, shape-shifters, and a war between two worlds. Walter Bishop is one of television's great characters.

## Key Themes

- Parallel universes as a metaphor for the roads not taken — who you'd be with different choices
- The father-son relationship at the center: Walter's sacrifice of Peter's parallel-self haunts everything
- Science without ethics as the show's recurring threat — mad science as mad love
- Olivia Dunham as the show's moral anchor in a world of moral relativity
- The Observers: consciousness divorced from emotion as a vision of evolutionary endpoint

## Why It Matters

John Noble's Walter Bishop is one of the great TV performances of the 2000s — a man whose genius and madness are genuinely indistinguishable. The show's willingness to strand itself in an alternate universe for half a season is an audacious creative choice. The parallel universe storytelling is the best use of that sci-fi trope in television. The series finale is respectful and earned.
