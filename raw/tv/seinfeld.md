# Seinfeld (1989–1998)

**Creator:** Jerry Seinfeld, Larry David
**Network:** NBC
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #25 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

'A show about nothing.' Jerry Seinfeld plays himself, a stand-up comedian in New York. His friends: George Costanza (Jason Alexander), the neurotic, lying, baldness-obsessed failure; Elaine Benes (Julia Louis-Dreyfus), his ex-girlfriend; Cosmo Kramer (Michael Richards), the eccentric neighbor. The show codified the 'no hugging, no learning' rule — the characters are selfish and never grow. The Soup Nazi. The contest. The close-talker.

## Key Themes

- 'No hugging, no learning' as a creative and moral principle — the refusal of sentimentality
- The comedy of small social violations — what do the rules of etiquette actually mean
- New York City as a world unto itself — Manhattan as moral universe
- George Costanza as pure American male anxiety made external and hilarious
- The finitude of the 'about nothing' premise: the finale's genius is making them pay for nine years of nothing

## Why It Matters

The most influential sitcom of the 1990s and the one that split the sitcom form in two: pre-Seinfeld sentimentality (*Cheers*, *Family Ties*) and post-Seinfeld irony. Larry David's 'no hugging, no learning' rule became a philosophy. Jason Alexander's George Costanza is the greatest secondary character in sitcom history. The finale was divisive on first airing and is now correctly understood as the only honest ending a show this morally inverted could have.
