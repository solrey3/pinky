# Survivor (2000–present)

**Creator:** Mark Burnett
**Network:** CBS
**Genre:** Reality competition
**Category:** Reality Television
**Rank:** #1 (Reality Television)

## Summary

The defining reality competition format: a group of strangers are marooned in a remote location, divided into tribes, and must compete in challenges while voting each other off until one person remains and wins $1 million. Seasons are defined by their casts, locations, and strategic patterns. The game rewards social intelligence, physical endurance, and the ability to manage relationships under pressure.

## Key Themes

- Social game theory in practice: alliances, betrayal, and the management of information
- The jury system: you cannot win without the votes of people you voted out
- Physical and mental endurance as overlapping but distinct skill sets
- The 'big move' vs. the 'goat': the tension between making history and making the finale
- Location as character: each season's setting shapes its tone and available strategies

## Why It Matters

The show that launched the modern reality competition genre in America. Richard Hatch's Season 1 win (the first jury management win in reality TV) established the game's logic. Russell Hantz's back-to-back villain runs showed that a dominant strategic game without social management loses to juries. 'Boston Rob' Mariano's four appearances and eventual win are a complete arc of a reality TV contestant's relationship with the format.
