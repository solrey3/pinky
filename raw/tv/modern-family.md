# Modern Family (2009–2020)

**Creator:** Christopher Lloyd, Steven Levitan
**Network:** ABC
**Genre:** Mockumentary sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #28 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Three interconnected families: the Dunphy family (Phil, Claire, Haley, Alex, Luke), the Pritchett family (Jay, Gloria, Manny), and the Tucker-Pritchett family (Mitch, Cam, Lily). A multi-generational sitcom that found comedy in the gap between how families present themselves and how they actually function. Won Outstanding Comedy Series for five consecutive years (2010–2014).

## Key Themes

- The modern family's self-consciousness about its modernity
- Phil Dunphy's earnestness as the show's emotional engine — a father who tries too hard and it works
- Gloria and Jay: the intergenerational, cross-cultural couple as a lens on American change
- Mitch and Cam: the gay couple whose arc normalized without tokenizing
- The family unit as performance — the mockumentary format is always asking who they're performing for

## Why It Matters

The most award-winning comedy of the 2010s. Ed O'Neill's Jay is a masterclass in late-career dramatic-comedy. Ty Burrell's Phil Dunphy is a comedic creation of genuine depth — earnest without being cloying. The show ran one season too many (or four) but its peak (Seasons 1–5) is consistently funny and surprisingly warm. Sofia Vergara's Gloria is a role that could easily have been a caricature and consistently isn't.
