# Dragon Ball Z / Dragon Ball Super (1989–1996 / 2015–2018)

**Creator:** Akira Toriyama
**Network:** Fuji TV / Toei Animation
**Genre:** Shonen anime, Action
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #8 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Goku, the last survivor of the Saiyan warrior race, defends Earth from increasingly powerful threats — from his brother Raditz to the tyrant Frieza to the androids to Cell to Majin Buu. *Dragon Ball Super* continues after the Buu saga, introducing Gods of Destruction and the Tournament of Power. The franchise is the defining shonen anime and the origin point of power-level culture, transformation reveals, and training arcs.

## Key Themes

- Power through training and will — every arc is about pushing past the apparent limit
- Family and friendship as the actual source of strength, not just raw power
- The Saiyan warrior race and its contradiction: Goku rejects its violence while embodying its potential
- Super Saiyan as a cultural icon — the transformation moment as pure cathartic release
- Tournament arcs as moral universe: the best fighter should also be the best person, usually

## Why It Matters

The Frieza saga and Cell saga are the defining shonen anime of the 1990s — formative viewing for an entire generation globally. The Super Saiyan transformation reveal remains one of the most emotionally effective moments in anime. *Dragon Ball Super*'s Tournament of Power arc (Ultra Instinct) recaptured the franchise's energy. Toriyama's power-scaling and transformation design became the template every shonen manga has followed since.
