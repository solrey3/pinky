# The White Lotus (2021–present)

**Creator:** Mike White
**Network:** HBO
**Genre:** Anthology drama, Dark comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #50 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

An anthology series in which each season focuses on guests and staff at a White Lotus resort property in a different location (Hawaii, Sicily, Thailand). Someone always dies; the mystery is who. The show is a precise satire of wealthy people's emotional dysfunction — their therapy-speak, their entitlement, their inability to enjoy what they have. Jennifer Coolidge's Tanya McQuoid became the show's accidental icon.

## Key Themes

- Privilege as both a shield and a prison — the wealthy cannot access genuine experience
- Tourism and its exploitation: the resort staff exist to absorb the guests' pathologies
- Class performance: every character is performing for their social peers at all times
- The dead body as a structural device — the season begins with a death, then shows how we got there
- Jennifer Coolidge's Tanya as a figure of both satire and genuine pathos

## Why It Matters

Mike White wrote all the episodes solo. Season 2 in Sicily is one of the most visually gorgeous seasons of television made in the 2020s. The ensemble acting across both seasons is extraordinary — Murray Bartlett, Jennifer Coolidge, F. Murray Abraham, Michael Imperioli, Aubrey Plaza. The show won 10 Emmy Awards for Season 1 and became HBO's most-watched new series in years. The finale of Season 2 is devastating.
