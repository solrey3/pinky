# Dollhouse (2009–2010)

**Creator:** Joss Whedon
**Network:** Fox
**Genre:** Sci-fi drama
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #36 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Echo (Eliza Dushku) is a 'doll' — an operative whose personality is wiped and replaced with new imprints for each mission at the Dollhouse, a clandestine organization serving wealthy clients. The show's premise is a metaphor for objectification, consent, and identity. Despite a troubled first season (network interference), the mythology that emerges in Season 2 is genuinely ambitious.

## Key Themes

- Consent, identity, and the ethics of erasing personhood for service
- The Dollhouse as a commentary on the entertainment industry's use of performers
- Who is Echo when no one has programmed her — the show's central question
- The unaired pilot 'Epitaph One' as the show's most realized vision of its own mythology
- Adelle DeWitt and Laurence Dominic as institutional characters who slowly cross moral lines

## Why It Matters

A Joss Whedon show cancelled before it could fully realize itself. The unaired episode 'Epitaph One' (set in a post-apocalyptic future created by the Dollhouse technology going wrong) is better than most of the produced episodes and shows what the show could have been. Enver Gjokaj's Victor is one of the great underappreciated TV performances — his ability to play different imprints convincingly was extraordinary.
