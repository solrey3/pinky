# Better Call Saul (2015–2022)

**Creator:** Vince Gilligan, Peter Gould
**Network:** AMC
**Genre:** Crime drama, Legal drama
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #14 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The origin and transformation of Jimmy McGill into Saul Goodman — the morally flexible lawyer from *Breaking Bad* — and the parallel story of Mike Ehrmantraut (Jonathan Banks) entering Gus Fring's (Giancarlo Esposito) drug operation. The show is about the seduction of the wrong path, shot in a palette that shifts from black-and-white (the present/future) to color (the past) with meticulous craft.

## Key Themes

- The slow corruption arc — Jimmy becomes Saul through a series of choices that each seem small
- Chuck McGill as Jimmy's mirror: the legitimate brother whose disdain made the criminal inevitable
- Mike Ehrmantraut as a man with a code operating in a world that punishes codes
- Kim Wexler as the show's tragic center — her complicity and her departure
- The black-and-white Gene timeline as the cost of becoming someone else entirely

## Why It Matters

Many critics argue it surpassed *Breaking Bad* — the character work is deeper, the cinematography more adventurous, the moral universe more complex. Bob Odenkirk transforms Saul from a comic relief figure into a genuinely tragic one. Rhea Seehorn's Kim Wexler is one of the great characters in prestige TV. The finale — Jimmy confessing in open court, choosing prison to honor the people he destroyed — is the most morally complete ending in the *Breaking Bad* universe.
