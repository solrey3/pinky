# Big Brother (2000–present)

**Creator:** John de Mol
**Network:** CBS
**Genre:** Reality competition
**Category:** Reality Television
**Rank:** #3 (Reality Television)

## Summary

Houseguests live together under constant camera surveillance while competing in Head of Household (HoH) and Power of Veto (PoV) competitions that determine who is nominated for eviction. The jury votes for the winner. The game's social dynamics are compressed by the constant 24/7 camera exposure — houseguests cannot escape each other or strategic conversations.

## Key Themes

- Constant surveillance and the performance of authenticity under observation
- The social game's dominance: competition wins matter less than alliance management
- The Jury House: management of jury members is a game within the game
- Floaters vs. competitors: different paths to the finale, each with risks
- Live feeds as a separate show: the CBS broadcast is heavily edited from a rawer reality

## Why It Matters

The show that made alliance strategy the central vocabulary of American reality competition. Dr. Will Kirby's Season 2 win (zero competition wins, pure social game) redefined what was possible. The introduction of the PoV competition created a structural check on HoH power that gave the game lasting strategic depth. The CBS edit vs. live feed disconnect has created a media ecosystem of reality TV journalism.
