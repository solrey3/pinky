# Breaking Bad (2008–2013)

**Creator:** Vince Gilligan
**Network:** AMC
**Genre:** Crime drama, Thriller
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #5 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

High school chemistry teacher Walter White (Bryan Cranston) is diagnosed with terminal lung cancer and begins cooking methamphetamine with former student Jesse Pinkman (Aaron Paul) to secure his family's financial future. The show tracks Walter's transformation from desperate provider to ruthless drug kingpin Heisenberg. Gilligan's stated premise: take 'Mr. Chips and turn him into Scarface.'

## Key Themes

- Pride masquerading as necessity — Walter's real motivation is ego, not his family
- The corruption arc as a slow accumulation of small compromises, each individually justifiable
- Masculinity and the provider myth: Walter equates financial provision with identity
- Chemistry as metaphor — transformation, reaction, irreversibility
- Jesse Pinkman as the show's moral center: the one who actually feels the cost

## Why It Matters

The most tightly plotted drama in television history — every Chekhov's gun fires. Cranston won four consecutive Outstanding Drama Actor Emmys. The 'I am the danger' scene is the precise moment Walter White fully becomes Heisenberg. Bob Odenkirk's Saul Goodman spawned a spinoff that arguably surpassed the original. The finale is perfect: Walter dies, but only after admitting — once, to Skyler — that he did it for himself.
