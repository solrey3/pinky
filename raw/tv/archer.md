# Archer (2009–present)

**Creator:** Adam Reed
**Network:** FX
**Genre:** Animated sitcom, Spy comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #32 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Sterling Archer (H. Jon Benjamin), the world's most dangerous and most dysfunctional secret agent, works for ISIS (later renamed) alongside his overbearing mother Malory (Jessica Walter), his ex Lana (Aisha Tyler), and a brilliantly incompetent support staff. The show's joke density rivals *Arrested Development*; the callbacks run across seasons; every character has both a comedic and a tragic dimension.

## Key Themes

- The spy genre demolished: Archer's incompetence is the punchline to Bond's wish fulfillment
- Malory and Sterling's co-dependent relationship as the show's psychological engine
- The callbacks: jokes that pay off three seasons later require absolute commitment from writers and audience
- H. Jon Benjamin's vocal performance as a feat of comic timing — every line reads as written and improvised simultaneously
- The coma seasons: a creative risk that let the show reinvent itself as noir, PI fiction, and space opera

## Why It Matters

Adam Reed wrote almost all the early seasons essentially solo — the joke density reflects one extremely funny mind operating at maximum. H. Jon Benjamin's Archer is the best vocal comedic performance in animation since the golden age of Looney Tunes. 'Do you want ants? Because that's how you get ants.' The Archer/Mallory dynamic is one of the great comic relationships in television. The coma seasons kept the show alive while giving Reed room to experiment.
