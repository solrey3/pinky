# The Genius (2013–2015)

**Creator:** CJ ENM
**Network:** tvN (South Korea)
**Genre:** Reality competition, Game show
**Category:** Reality Television
**Rank:** #5 (Reality Television)

## Summary

Korean reality competition in which 13 contestants (Korean celebrities, athletes, and intellectuals) compete in weekly games requiring strategy, deception, and game theory — rather than physical ability. The 'Death Match' elimination format means one losing contestant can choose another to face in a head-to-head game. The games range from poker variants to territory control to social deduction.

## Key Themes

- Pure game theory in a competitive format: the games are genuinely mathematically complex
- Social deduction and information management as the core skills
- The Death Match as a structural incentive toward targeted elimination of threats
- Korean celebrity culture as the cast's social subtext — players have existing relationships
- The show as a pure distillation of what reality competition can be when it prioritizes intellect

## Why It Matters

The most intellectually rigorous reality competition ever produced. Season 1 winner Hong Jin-ho (a professional StarCraft player) demonstrates the game's demands — pattern recognition, opponent modeling, and cold-blooded calculation. The show has a significant international cult following and directly influenced *Squid Game*'s creator. The gap between *The Genius* and Western reality TV formats is a complete argument about what the genre could be.
