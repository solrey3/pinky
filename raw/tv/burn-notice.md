# Burn Notice (2007–2013)

**Creator:** Matt Nix
**Network:** USA Network
**Genre:** Action, Spy thriller
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #52 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Michael Westen (Jeffrey Donovan) is a spy who receives a 'burn notice' — a termination of employment with no explanation. Stranded in Miami with his ex-girlfriend Fiona (Gabrielle Anwar), his mother Madeline (Sharon Gless), and former CIA operative Sam Axe (Bruce Campbell), he uses his skills to help locals while investigating who burned him. The show's narration explains spy tradecraft as Michael uses it.

## Key Themes

- Tradecraft as text: Michael narrates spy techniques as he employs them — the show as instruction manual
- The improvised solution: MacGyver-style problem-solving with whatever is available
- Miami as a setting that rarely gets prestige television treatment
- Family and loyalty vs. institutional betrayal
- Bruce Campbell's Sam Axe as the show's comedic heart: the ex-Navy SEAL who solves everything with charm

## Why It Matters

USA Network's highest-rated scripted series. The 'voice of God' tradecraft narration — Michael explaining in real time how to tail a car, run a con, or build a shaped charge from hardware store components — is a genuinely inventive format device. Bruce Campbell elevated the show from procedural to enjoyable. Jeffrey Donovan's physicality and charisma make Michael a compelling lead. The show ran long and found an honorable ending.
