# Cobra Kai (2018–2025)

**Creator:** Josh Heald, Jon Hurwitz, Hayden Schlossberg
**Network:** YouTube Premium / Netflix
**Genre:** Martial arts drama, Sequel series
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #35 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Set 34 years after *The Karate Kid* (1984). Johnny Lawrence (William Zabka) reopens the Cobra Kai dojo and reconnects with his past; Daniel LaRusso (Ralph Macchio) still runs his family dealership and is drawn back into the karate world. The show's genius is reversing the original film's moral framework: Johnny was not simply the villain, and Daniel is not simply the hero.

## Key Themes

- Moral complexity of the sequel: both Johnny and Daniel were right and wrong in 1984
- Mentorship and its corruption: what Kreese taught Johnny vs. what Johnny tries to teach
- Generational trauma: the students inherit their teachers' feuds without choosing to
- Redemption as a process, not an event — Johnny's arc is the show's best writing
- The revisionist sports narrative: who was really the bully

## Why It Matters

The show that made William Zabka a dramatic actor and recontextualized one of the 80s' most beloved films. The decision to treat Johnny Lawrence with empathy rather than nostalgia is what makes the show work. The fight choreography is genuinely excellent. Netflix's acquisition after YouTube Premium launched it to a massive new audience. Martin Kove's Kreese return as a full villain gives the mythology its weight.
