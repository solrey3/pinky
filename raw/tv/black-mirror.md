# Black Mirror (2011–present)

**Creator:** Charlie Brooker
**Network:** Channel 4 / Netflix
**Genre:** Sci-fi anthology, Dystopia
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #15 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

An anthology series where each episode is a standalone story exploring a dark near-future extrapolation of technology and human nature. The 'black mirror' is the blank screen of any device — phone, tablet, TV — when it's off. The best episodes ('Nosedive,' 'San Junipero,' 'USS Callister,' 'White Bear') range from devastating to transcendent. Brooker was a TV critic before he was a showrunner, which explains everything.

## Key Themes

- Technology as amplifier of human pathology, not its cause
- Social media, rating systems, and the gamification of human worth
- Memory and consciousness — if a digital copy of you exists, what are you
- The punishment instinct and its perversion in technologically enhanced systems
- 'San Junipero' as the show's counterweight: technology as genuine liberation and love

## Why It Matters

Brooker's early Channel 4 episodes ('The National Anthem,' 'The Entire History of You,' 'White Bear') are genuinely disturbing in a way that lingers. 'San Junipero' is one of the most purely moving hours of television of the 2010s. The Netflix era diluted the intensity but added scale. The show's predictive accuracy is unsettling: the social rating episode predicted China's social credit system; the political episode predicted certain actual political events.
