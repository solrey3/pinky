# Friends (1994–2004)

**Creator:** David Crane, Marta Kauffman
**Network:** NBC
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #41 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Six friends in New York City: Ross (David Schwimmer), Rachel (Jennifer Aniston), Monica (Courteney Cox), Chandler (Matthew Perry), Joey (Matt LeBlanc), and Phoebe (Lisa Kudrow). A comfort object for a generation — the show that made NBC's 'Must See TV' Thursday nights. Warm, well-crafted, occasionally brilliant, and representing a New York that is a fantasy of community.

## Key Themes

- The apartment as utopia: the friends' proximity to each other is the show's foundational unreality
- Chandler Bing as the show's funniest character — the sarcasm as emotional armor made explicit
- Ross and Rachel as the will-they/won't-they that drove the show's mythology for ten years
- New York affordability as a background impossibility the show cheerfully ignores
- The show as a comfort text: rewatched more than almost anything because it produces a specific warm feeling

## Why It Matters

The most-watched sitcom in the world for decades. The finale drew 52.5 million viewers. Matthew Perry's Chandler Bing is the template for the self-deprecating, sarcasm-as-coping sitcom character that dominated 2000s comedy. The show's cultural saturation is so complete it exists separately from its quality — you can have complicated feelings about it and still know it by heart.
