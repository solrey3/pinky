# The Kids in the Hall (1988–1995)

**Creator:** Dave Foley, Bruce McCulloch, Kevin McDonald, Mark McKinney, Scott Thompson
**Network:** CBC / HBO
**Genre:** Sketch comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #53 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Five Canadian sketch comedy performers — Dave Foley, Bruce McCulloch, Kevin McDonald, Mark McKinney, and Scott Thompson — performing character-driven sketches with a specific sensibility: absurdist, surreal, queer-inflected, and often darker than American sketch comedy of the era. Scott Thompson's Buddy Cole monologues are openly gay at a time when that was genuinely transgressive on television.

## Key Themes

- Canadian identity as a comedic perspective: adjacent to American culture, not inside it
- Queer sensibility before queer was mainstream: the troupe's willingness to go there in 1990
- Character depth in sketch: recurring figures (Headcrusher, Chicken Lady, Buddy Cole) have actual arcs
- Surrealism as a natural comedic mode: the sketches follow dream logic
- The troupe as creative unit: the Kids in the Hall aesthetic cannot be attributed to any single member

## Why It Matters

The most consistently dark and strange sketch comedy on North American television of the era. Scott Thompson's Buddy Cole monologues were the most visible gay comedy on television when they aired. The 'I'm crushing your head' character became a cultural touchstone. The troupe reunited for an Amazon revival in 2022 that was better than anyone had any right to expect. Bruce McCulloch's writing voice is unlike anything else in comedy.
