# The Mary Tyler Moore Show (1970–1977)

**Creator:** James L. Brooks, Allan Burns
**Network:** CBS
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #56 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Mary Richards (Mary Tyler Moore) is a 30-year-old single woman who moves to Minneapolis after a breakup and works as an associate producer at WJM-TV news. The show was the first American sitcom centered on a single working woman who was defined by her career rather than her search for a husband. The ensemble — Lou Grant (Ed Asner), Ted Baxter (Ted Knight), Rhoda Morgenstern (Valerie Harper), Murray Slaughter (Gavin MacLeod) — is among the finest in TV history.

## Key Themes

- Women's professional identity as a complete life: Mary's career is the show's center, not a subplot
- The workplace as community: the newsroom is as much a family as any domestic sitcom setup
- Lou Grant's gruff respect as a new model of masculine mentorship
- The single woman's life as a valid and complete existence
- Comedy and pathos: the show handles genuine adult emotions within the sitcom format

## Why It Matters

29 Emmy Awards across its run — the most of any comedy series at the time. The show changed what a female sitcom protagonist could be: competent, professionally ambitious, romantically independent. James L. Brooks and Allan Burns created the template for the ensemble workplace comedy. Ed Asner's Lou Grant was so complete a character he earned his own dramatic spinoff. The finale (the entire newsroom being fired except Ted Baxter) is the perfect black comedy punctuation.
