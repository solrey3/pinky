# The Jeffersons (1975–1985)

**Creator:** Norman Lear
**Network:** CBS
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #60 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

George Jefferson (Sherman Hemsley) and his wife Louise (Isabel Sanford) move to a luxury apartment on Manhattan's East Side after George's dry-cleaning business succeeds. A spinoff from *All in the Family*, the show was the first American sitcom centered on an affluent Black family. George Jefferson is a Black Archie Bunker — bigoted, pompous, and human.

## Key Themes

- 'Moving on up': Black upward mobility as both aspiration and comedy
- George Jefferson as a mirror of Archie Bunker — the show consciously inverts *All in the Family*
- The interracial couple (Tom and Helen Willis) as a structural statement
- Louise's dignity vs. George's bluster: the marriage as a comedy of two people who complete each other
- Class and race in 1970s America: what does success look like for a Black family

## Why It Matters

The longest-running primetime series featuring an African American family at the time. Sherman Hemsley and Isabel Sanford are both irreplaceable. The show ran for 11 seasons and 253 episodes — a feat of sustained creative consistency. The theme song ('Movin' On Up') is one of the great sitcom openers. The show proved that a Black family as the center of an American sitcom was not a niche proposition.
