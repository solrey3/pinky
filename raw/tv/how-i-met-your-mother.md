# How I Met Your Mother (2005–2014)

**Creator:** Carter Bays, Craig Thomas
**Network:** CBS
**Genre:** Sitcom, Romantic comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #40 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Ted Mosby (Josh Radnor) narrates to his children the story of how he met their mother — nine seasons of context. Marshall (Jason Segel), Lily (Alyson Hannigan), Barney (Neil Patrick Harris), and Robin (Cobie Smulders) are the ensemble. The show is famous for its narrative tricks (the slap bet, the blue French horn, the locket) and for a finale so divisive it retroactively damaged how people felt about the entire run.

## Key Themes

- The framing device as a promise: nine seasons of build-up toward a specific emotional destination
- Barney Stinson as the show's comedic engine: NPH's performance elevates material that shouldn't work
- Marshall and Lily as the show's moral center: the successful relationship as the show's actual thesis
- Ted's romanticism as both his appeal and his problem — he loves the idea of love more than people
- The finale: a creative decision that was technically defensible and emotionally dishonest

## Why It Matters

Neil Patrick Harris's Barney Stinson is one of the great comedic inventions of 2000s TV. The show's structural tricks (flash-forwards, the slap bet countdown, the mother's presence before the reveal) kept the premise fresh for longer than it should have worked. The finale's decision to kill the mother and pair Ted with Robin invalidated nine years of character development for Robin and Barney — a case study in how a finale can damage a legacy.
