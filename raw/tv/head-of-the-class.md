# Head of the Class (1986–1991)

**Creator:** Rich Eustis, Michael Elias
**Network:** ABC
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #61 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Charlie Moore (Howard Hesseman), an itinerant actor, accidentally becomes a history teacher for the Individualized Honors Program at Fillmore High School in New York — a class of gifted misfits. The ensemble of student characters represents multiple ethnic and cultural backgrounds. A warm, intelligent sitcom about the relationship between a gifted teacher and students who don't need to be taught facts but need to be taught how to live.

## Key Themes

- The gifted student's specific alienation — too smart for regular class, too weird for social acceptance
- The teacher who reaches students by being genuinely curious rather than authoritative
- The class as an ensemble comedy — six or seven distinct characters with different comedic registers
- Knowledge vs. wisdom: the IHP students know everything and understand nothing yet
- The 1980s New York public school as a specific geography

## Why It Matters

A cult sitcom that influenced a generation of viewers who identified with the IHP students. Howard Hesseman's Charlie Moore is a genuine television teacher character — not a movie-sentimentalized version. The show ran five seasons and introduced a diverse ensemble cast in a primetime network show at a time when that was still unusual. Billy Connolly replaced Hesseman in Season 5.
