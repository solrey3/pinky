# The Boondocks (2005–2014)

**Creator:** Aaron McGruder
**Network:** Adult Swim
**Genre:** Animated satire, Comedy
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #9 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Huey and Riley Freeman — grandsons of Robert Jebediah Freeman (Granddad) — move from Chicago to the affluent suburb of Woodcrest, Maryland. Huey is a precocious radical intellectual; Riley idolizes gangster culture. The show is a precise and often furious satire of Black American culture, white liberal guilt, media, and politics — using anime-influenced animation and the comic strip source material as cover for genuine political fire.

## Key Themes

- Black respectability politics vs. authentic Black expression — Huey and Riley as the poles
- White suburbia's discomfort with Blackness it cannot commodify
- Media and the BET-ification of Black culture as self-harm
- Granddad as the integrationist generation — the costs and compromises of assimilation
- The radical imagination: Huey's politics are correct and completely ineffective, which is the joke and the tragedy

## Why It Matters

The most politically serious animated show ever made — McGruder was not playing. The Martin Luther King Jr. episode ('Return of the King') is one of the great satirical texts of the 2000s: MLK wakes from a coma on 9/11 and is called a traitor for opposing the war. The Stinkmeaner arc is pure aesthetic pleasure. The show was in constant tension with Cartoon Network/Adult Swim over its content, which is part of its credential.
