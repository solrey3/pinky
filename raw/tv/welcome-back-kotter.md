# Welcome Back, Kotter (1975–1979)

**Creator:** Eric Kotter, Gabe Kaplan
**Network:** ABC
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #57 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

Gabe Kotter (Gabe Kaplan) returns to his Brooklyn high school to teach the 'Sweathogs' — a remedial class of lovable delinquents including Vinnie Barbarino (John Travolta), Freddie 'Boom Boom' Washington (Lawrence Hilton-Jacobs), Juan Epstein (Robert Hegyes), and Arnold Horshack (Ron Palillo). The show is a warm, broadly comic portrait of urban education that launched John Travolta's career.

## Key Themes

- The returning teacher as a figure who can reach students no one else can
- Urban working-class Brooklyn as a specific cultural geography
- John Travolta's Vinnie Barbarino: the lovable delinquent as a star-making role
- The Sweathogs as a found family of misfits — the class that became a community
- Comedy's capacity to make institutional failure (the school system) into warmth

## Why It Matters

John Travolta's breakthrough before *Saturday Night Fever*. The show was a massive ABC hit that accurately captured the energy of Brooklyn working-class youth culture in the 1970s. Gabe Kaplan's comedy background kept the show from being purely sentimental. 'Up your nose with a rubber hose' entered the playground vocabulary of a generation.
