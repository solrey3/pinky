# Taxi (1978–1983)

**Creator:** James L. Brooks, Stan Daniels, David Davis, Ed Weinberger
**Network:** ABC / NBC
**Genre:** Sitcom
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #22 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

The drivers of the Sunshine Cab Company in New York City: Alex Reiger (Judd Hirsch), Jim Ignatowski (Christopher Lloyd), Latka Gravas (Andy Kaufman), Louie De Palma (Danny DeVito), Bobby Wheeler (Jeff Conaway), and Elaine Nardo (Marilu Henner). A workplace sitcom with genuine pathos about people whose lives didn't turn out as planned but who find meaning in each other.

## Key Themes

- The taxi garage as a last-chance ecosystem: everyone is between their real life and this one
- Andy Kaufman's Latka as pure comedic alien — a character operating on a different wavelength
- Danny DeVito's Louie as the show's engine: a man so small in every sense that he compensates enormously
- Alex Reiger's quiet wisdom: the straight man as emotional anchor
- Jim Ignatowski's drug-fried genius: Christopher Lloyd's most committed comedic performance

## Why It Matters

Five Emmy wins for Outstanding Comedy Series. Andy Kaufman's Latka is one of the great comedic inventions in television — an alien creature with its own internal logic. Danny DeVito's Louie De Palma launched his film career. Christopher Lloyd won two Emmys. The show treated its characters' disappointments with a dignity that sitcoms rarely manage. Jim Brooks co-created *The Simpsons* — the DNA runs through both.
