# Doctor Who (1963–present)

**Creator:** Sydney Newman, C. E. Webber, Donald Wilson
**Network:** BBC
**Genre:** Sci-fi, Adventure
**Category:** Scripted Series
**Rank:** #42 (Scripted Series)

## Summary

A Time Lord known as the Doctor (a role that has been played by many actors through the device of 'regeneration') travels through time and space in the TARDIS — a blue police box that is bigger on the inside. The modern revival (2005–) created by Russell T Davies with Christopher Eccleston, David Tennant, Matt Smith, and beyond. The show is children's television that rewards adult re-engagement.

## Key Themes

- Regeneration as a metaphor for change, death, and continuity of identity
- The Doctor's pacifism and its limits: the Last of the Time Lords as a study in survivor guilt
- Companions as the audience's proxy — each new companion re-teaches the Doctor to care
- British eccentricity as a superpower: the Doctor is the ultimate triumph of cleverness over force
- The timey-wimey: the show's relationship to causality is both its greatest freedom and its greatest cop-out

## Why It Matters

The longest-running sci-fi television series in history. David Tennant's Tenth Doctor is culturally defining — 'I'm sorry, I'm so sorry' became the character's emotional signature. The Steven Moffat era introduced narrative complexity that divided audiences. The show's format — the regeneration device — allows genuine reinvention while maintaining continuity, which is the most elegant piece of long-form TV architecture ever designed.
