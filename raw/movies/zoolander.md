# Zoolander (2001)

**Director:** Ben Stiller
**Genre:** Comedy, Parody
**Runtime:** 90 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Derek Zoolander (Ben Stiller), a male model who can only turn left and only do one face ('Blue Steel'), is brainwashed by the fashion industry's villainous Mugatu (Will Ferrell) to assassinate the Prime Minister of Malaysia. His rival Hansel (Owen Wilson) and journalist Matilda (Christine Taylor) try to stop it. A merciless satire of the fashion industry, masculinity, and celebrity culture.

## Key Themes

- Vanity and intellectual vacancy as industry-produced, not natural
- Fashion as a system for manufacturing and discarding identity
- The comedy of the man who cannot recognize his own limitations
- Celebrity and the labor politics the beautiful people ignore
- Bromance and male emotional openness — Derek and Hansel's friendship is the heart

## Why It Matters

One of the most quotable comedies of the 2000s. 'Blue Steel.' 'The files are in the computer.' 'A Center for Ants.' Will Ferrell's Mugatu is peak Ferrell. The 'Walk-Off' scene featuring David Bowie is perfect. The film's satirical targets (fashion, celebrity, male beauty standards) are sketched with genuine insight beneath the absurdism. Ben Stiller directed and wrote it and clearly hated something specific.
