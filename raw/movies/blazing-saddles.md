# Blazing Saddles (1974)

**Director:** Mel Brooks
**Genre:** Western parody, Comedy
**Runtime:** 93 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

A corrupt politician appoints a Black sheriff (Cleavon Little) to a racist frontier town, expecting him to fail. Instead, Sheriff Bart teams with the washed-up Waco Kid (Gene Wilder) to outwit the villains, the townspeople's racism, and the conventions of the Western genre itself — including, in the climax, the walls of the movie studio. The film ends at Grauman's Chinese Theatre.

## Key Themes

- Racism as stupidity made visible — the bigots are always outfoxed because they underestimate
- The Western as America's founding myth, here dismantled and exposed
- Meta-cinema as political commentary: breaking the fourth wall breaks the ideology
- The Black intellectual vs. white mob — Bart wins every confrontation with intelligence
- Satire's duty to make you laugh at something so you can think about it clearly

## Why It Matters

Reportedly the last film that could be made like this — Warner Bros. tried to cut the N-word usage, the campfire flatulence scene, and the ending. Brooks refused on all counts. The film was a massive commercial success and changed what Hollywood would permit in comedy. Richard Pryor co-wrote the screenplay. It remains the definitive test of whether someone understands what satire is actually for.
