# The Harder They Come (1972)

**Director:** Perry Henzell
**Genre:** Crime drama, Musical
**Runtime:** 104 min
**Country:** Jamaica

## Summary

Ivan Martin (Jimmy Cliff) arrives in Kingston from the country with dreams of becoming a reggae star. Exploited by the music industry, forced into marijuana dealing, and ultimately cornered by the police after killing two officers, he becomes a Robin Hood folk hero in the newspaper headlines. A soundtrack that changed what the world knew about reggae.

## Key Themes

- The music industry as an exploitative system — Ivan signs away everything for nothing
- The outlaw as cultural hero when institutions have failed
- Post-colonial Jamaica's class structure and the urban poor's lack of options
- Media mythologizing: Ivan performs for the cameras, becoming the legend they want
- The American Western as a template Ivan literally uses to understand himself

## Why It Matters

The film that brought reggae to the world. Jimmy Cliff's performance is raw and magnetic. The soundtrack — Cliff, Toots and the Maytals, Desmond Dekker, The Melodians — is one of the great film soundtracks and one of the first reggae albums to reach international audiences. The film was shot on a tiny budget in actual Kingston locations. It exists in both canons — the SolReyDio album list and the film list — which tells you everything.
