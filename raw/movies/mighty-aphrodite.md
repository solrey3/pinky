# Mighty Aphrodite (1995)

**Director:** Woody Allen
**Genre:** Comedy, Drama
**Runtime:** 95 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Lenny (Woody Allen) becomes obsessed with finding the biological mother of his adopted son, who is exceptionally gifted. He discovers she is Linda Ash (Mira Sorvino), a porn actress and escort with a relentlessly positive spirit. A Greek chorus in Grecian masks and robes appears periodically to comment on the proceedings. Sorvino won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress.

## Key Themes

- Nature vs. nurture: the gifted son and his seemingly opposite biological origin
- The Greek chorus as fate made visible and ridiculous simultaneously
- Male obsession and the Pygmalion compulsion — Lenny's need to improve Linda
- Comedy as a vessel for genuinely dark material (the Greek tragedy the chorus predicts)
- Maternal identity and the complexity of biological vs. chosen family

## Why It Matters

One of Allen's better 90s films, elevated entirely by Mira Sorvino. Her performance — warm, funny, unexpectedly touching — is one of the great comedic turns of the decade. The Greek chorus conceit is executed with commitment and genuine wit. The film also marks a point where Allen's classical film knowledge (Greek tragedy, screwball comedy) synthesizes cleanly.
