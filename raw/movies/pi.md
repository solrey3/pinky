# Pi (1998)

**Director:** Darren Aronofsky
**Genre:** Psychological thriller, Sci-fi
**Runtime:** 84 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Max Cohen (Sean Gullette), a paranoid and brilliant mathematician, believes that everything in nature can be understood through numbers and that he is close to finding the 216-digit key to the universe. He is pursued by a Wall Street firm seeking stock market prediction algorithms and a Kabbalah sect seeking the true name of God. Filmed on high-contrast black-and-white 16mm for $60,000.

## Key Themes

- The obsessive mind as both instrument of discovery and instrument of destruction
- Pattern recognition as psychosis — when does finding order become imposing it
- The sacred and the financial as competing interpretations of the same hidden truth
- Mathematics as mysticism and madness — Ramanujan, not Descartes
- The cost of understanding: Max's migraines are literal and figurative

## Why It Matters

Aronofsky's debut, shot guerrilla-style in Brooklyn for $60,000. The visual style — high-contrast, frenetic editing, extreme close-ups of mathematical notation — was entirely new and influenced a decade of independent thriller aesthetics. The film won the Directing Award at Sundance. It is a film about math that makes math feel genuinely terrifying.
