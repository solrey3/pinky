# Trading Places (1983)

**Director:** John Landis
**Genre:** Comedy
**Runtime:** 116 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Wealthy commodities broker Louis Winthorpe III (Dan Aykroyd) and poor street hustler Billy Ray Valentine (Eddie Murphy) are unknowingly used as pawns in a one-dollar bet by the Duke brothers — nature vs. nurture played out in real human lives. When they discover the bet, they team up to financially destroy the Dukes using frozen concentrated orange juice futures. The best economics lesson in film history.

## Key Themes

- Nature vs. nurture: environment and opportunity determine success, not character
- Class as performance and accident — both men can do each other's job
- Capitalism's willingness to sacrifice individuals for profit and sport
- Revenge as market mechanics — the heroes beat the villains at their own game
- Race and class in Reagan-era America: Murphy brings the political subtext forward

## Why It Matters

Murphy and Aykroyd are perfectly matched. The FCOJ trading scene is so accurate that the 'Eddie Murphy Rule' was added to the Commodity Exchange Act in 2010, banning trading on misappropriated government information. Don Ameche and Ralph Bellamy as the Dukes are irreplaceable villains. Landis at his commercial peak.
