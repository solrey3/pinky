# Fargo (1996)

**Director:** Joel Coen, Ethan Coen
**Genre:** Crime thriller, Dark comedy
**Runtime:** 98 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Jerry Lundegaard (William H. Macy) arranges the kidnapping of his own wife by two criminals (Steve Buscemi, Peter Stormare) to extract ransom from his wealthy father-in-law. The plan collapses immediately. Pregnant police chief Marge Gunderson (Frances McDormand) investigates. A wood chipper is involved. The film opens with a claim to be based on true events. It is not, mostly.

## Key Themes

- Greed and the specifically American delusion that more money will fix everything
- Competence vs. incompetence: every criminal plan fails; Marge succeeds by being thorough
- The Midwest as a moral landscape — Marge's decency is presented without irony
- Violence erupting from banal stupidity rather than evil genius
- The wood chipper as the film's thesis statement on where greed leads

## Why It Matters

Frances McDormand won the Oscar for Best Actress for Marge Gunderson — one of cinema's most complete creations. The Coens' screenplay won Best Original Screenplay. The film's tone — genuine comedy alongside genuine horror — is their signature and this is its definitive expression. 'I guess that was your accomplice in the wood chipper.' Marge's final speech to Grimsrud is the film's moral statement delivered as mundane observation.
