# Airplane! (1980)

**Director:** Jim Abrahams, David Zucker, Jerry Zucker
**Genre:** Comedy, Parody
**Runtime:** 88 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

A parody of airport disaster films (specifically 1957's *Zero Hour!*, which ZAZ purchased the rights to). Former fighter pilot Ted Striker (Robert Hays) must land a commercial flight after food poisoning takes out the crew. Lloyd Bridges, Leslie Nielsen, Robert Stack, and Peter Graves deliver deadpan absurdism at maximum density. Approximately three jokes every four seconds.

## Key Themes

- Deadpan as comedic principle — the joke lives in the commitment to seriousness
- Genre deconstruction: every disaster film trope is identified and detonated
- The gap between authority and competence — every expert is useless or destroyed
- Non-sequitur as a comic logic system: the film has internal rules, they're just absurdist
- Speed of gag delivery as a structural choice: no time to think, only to laugh

## Why It Matters

The most quoted comedy film ever made. Nielsen, Bridges, and Stack had never done comedy — their casting as straight men is the engine. The film reportedly plays every four years in the Antarctic because it's the only thing that reliably produces laughter in extreme isolation. ZAZ's formula here launched *Police Squad!*, *The Naked Gun*, and the entire deadpan parody tradition of the 80s and 90s.
