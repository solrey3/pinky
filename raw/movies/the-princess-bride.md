# The Princess Bride (1987)

**Director:** Rob Reiner
**Genre:** Fantasy, Adventure, Romance, Comedy
**Runtime:** 98 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

A grandfather reads a story to his sick grandson. The story: farm boy Westley (Cary Elwes) and noblewoman Buttercup (Robin Wright) are separated by assumed death and reunite through swashbuckling, a battle of wits to the death, a lightning-sand bog, a fire swamp, and the six-fingered man Inigo Montoya's two-decade quest for vengeance. A fairy tale that knows it is a fairy tale.

## Key Themes

- True love as the organizing principle of the universe — the story's ironclad moral law
- Revenge as vocation: Inigo Montoya's quest gives his life structure and meaning
- Meta-narrative: the grandson's resistance to the love story mirrors the audience's defenses
- Competence and wit as forms of love — Westley respects Buttercup by refusing to let her lose
- Death as negotiable (Miracle Max) — the film deflates tragedy through comedy

## Why It Matters

Goldman adapted his own novel and delivered one of cinema's most quotable scripts. Mandy Patinkin's Inigo Montoya is the emotional core. 'My name is Inigo Montoya. You killed my father. Prepare to die.' is not just a meme — it is a complete character arc in one sentence, said six times with increasing weight. The film earns its sentiment because it never stops being funny.
