# Do the Right Thing (1989)

**Director:** Spike Lee
**Genre:** Drama
**Runtime:** 120 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

The hottest day of the summer in Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn. Mookie (Spike Lee) delivers pizzas for Sal's Pizzeria (Danny Aiello) while racial tensions build across the block. Radio Raheem (Bill Nunn) blasts Public Enemy from his boombox. By evening, an argument over the 'Wall of Fame' and a police chokehold have killed Raheem and burned the pizzeria. The film refuses a clean moral.

## Key Themes

- Systemic racism and the point at which non-violence is no longer a viable answer
- Competing civil rights philosophies: MLK's 'hate for hate escalates' vs. Malcolm's 'by any means'
- Community, commerce, and ownership — who really owns the block
- Music as politics: Public Enemy's 'Fight the Power' is the film's thesis song
- The white gaze vs. the Black experience: the film refuses to explain itself to anyone

## Why It Matters

Lee's masterpiece and one of the most important American films ever made. Roger Ebert named it the best film of the 1980s. The Academy nominated it for nothing significant — no Best Picture, no Best Director — in 1989; *Driving Miss Daisy* won. That fact is part of the film's ongoing argument. The 'Love' and 'Hate' speech, Radio Raheem's death, and the final title cards citing MLK and Malcolm X remain unresolved.
