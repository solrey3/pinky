# Boogie Nights (1997)

**Director:** Paul Thomas Anderson
**Genre:** Drama
**Runtime:** 155 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Eddie Adams (Mark Wahlberg) is discovered by pornographic filmmaker Jack Horner (Burt Reynolds) in 1977 San Fernando Valley and becomes star Dirk Diggler. The film traces the ensemble of the porn industry's Golden Age through prosperity, cocaine, and the disaster the 80s brought. Boogie Nights is less about sex than about the need to belong to a family and the violence of that family's dissolution.

## Key Themes

- Family as chosen construct — the porn ensemble is a genuine family, not just a trope
- The 1970s to 1980s transition as cultural rupture: warmth into cocaine paranoia
- Artistic ambition and commercial exploitation — Horner wants to make 'real films'
- The male body as commodity and source of identity: Dirk's talent is his anatomy
- Addiction and the pastoral: the pool in Jack's backyard is paradise before the fall

## Why It Matters

PTA's breakthrough. The opening three-minute tracking shot through the nightclub is one of the great cinematic entrances. Reynolds was nominated for an Oscar and called it his best work. The ensemble — Wahlberg, Reynolds, Julianne Moore, Don Cheadle, John C. Reilly, William H. Macy, Heather Graham, Luis Guzmán — is one of the richest in American film. The 1983 drug deal sequence is pure dread.
