# Spaceballs (1987)

**Director:** Mel Brooks
**Genre:** Sci-fi parody, Comedy
**Runtime:** 96 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

A galactic parody of Star Wars. Lone Starr (Bill Pullman) and his half-man/half-dog sidekick Barf (John Candy) are hired to rescue Princess Vespa (Daphne Zuniga) from the villainous Dark Helmet (Rick Moranis) and the greedy Planet Spaceball, which is running out of air. Mel Brooks plays both President Skroob and the tiny wise Yogurt, keeper of 'The Schwartz.'

## Key Themes

- Satire of blockbuster sci-fi and corporate merchandising
- The Schwartz as a riff on The Force — spirituality reduced to a ring
- Meta-humor and fourth-wall demolition (characters watching themselves on VHS mid-film)
- Class and greed: Spaceball villains literally commodifying breathable air
- Loyalty and unlikely partnership (Lone Starr and Barf)

## Why It Matters

One of Mel Brooks's sharpest satirical weapons. *Spaceballs* doesn't just parody *Star Wars* — it skewers the entire blockbuster merchandise machine with jokes that have aged better than most of the franchise it mocks. The VHS-in-real-time gag is one of the most perfect meta-comedy moments in film history. Quote density per minute is elite.
