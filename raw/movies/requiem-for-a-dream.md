# Requiem for a Dream (2000)

**Director:** Darren Aronofsky
**Genre:** Drama, Psychological thriller
**Runtime:** 102 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Four people in Coney Island are destroyed by addiction. Harry (Jared Leto) and his girlfriend Marion (Jennifer Connelly) descend into heroin addiction. His friend Tyrone (Marlon Wayans) is alongside them. His mother Sara (Ellen Burstyn) becomes addicted to amphetamines chasing a television dream. The four storylines converge at rock bottom. Clint Mansell's 'Lux Aeterna' scores the disintegration.

## Key Themes

- Addiction as the pursuit of a dream that destroys the dreamer
- The American Dream as a pharmaceutical — Sara's TV show obsession is the same drug Harry takes
- The body under siege: Aronofsky's split-screen and fish-eye lens put us inside the dependency
- Maternal loneliness and the void that addiction fills
- The horror of the ordinary: all four characters wanted very small things

## Why It Matters

One of the most formally aggressive films ever made. Ellen Burstyn received an Oscar nomination for a performance so committed she said it was the hardest thing she had ever done. Clint Mansell's score is one of the most recognized pieces of music in any film from this era. Aronofsky used the hip-hop montage technique (extreme fast-cut sequences) to put the audience inside drug euphoria. Not a film you watch twice casually.
