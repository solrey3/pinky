# A Clockwork Orange (1971)

**Director:** Stanley Kubrick
**Genre:** Sci-fi, Drama, Crime
**Runtime:** 136 min
**Country:** UK

## Summary

Near-future Britain. Alex DeLarge (Malcolm McDowell) leads a gang of 'droogs' in recreational ultraviolence. Arrested, he undergoes the Ludovico Technique — forced behavioral conditioning that removes his capacity for violence and, collaterally, his capacity to enjoy his beloved Beethoven. The film interrogates whether a person robbed of free will — even the will to commit evil — can be called human.

## Key Themes

- Free will as the foundation of moral identity — the capacity for evil is inseparable from agency
- The state's violence as equal to or greater than the individual's
- Beethoven's Ninth as a test of the aesthetic experience vs. moral content of art
- Language as power: Nadsat (Alex's argot) makes the audience complicit in his worldview
- Rehabilitation vs. punishment — the Ludovico Technique is revealed as a political tool, not treatment

## Why It Matters

Kubrick withdrew the film from UK distribution after copycat violence concerns; it wasn't shown in Britain until after his death in 1999. McDowell's charisma makes Alex both repulsive and magnetic — the film's moral problem is that it works. The film adapts Burgess's novel while losing his structural conceit (the 21st chapter, where Alex genuinely matures). Kubrick chose not to include it.
