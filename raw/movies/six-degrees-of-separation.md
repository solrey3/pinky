# Six Degrees of Separation (1993)

**Director:** Fred Schepisi
**Genre:** Drama, Comedy-drama
**Runtime:** 112 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Paul (Will Smith), a young Black man, presents himself to wealthy New York art dealers Ouisa and Flan Kittredge (Stockard Channing, Donald Sutherland) as a Harvard classmate of their children and the son of Sidney Poitier. He is charming, erudite, and a fraud. The film traces the Kittredges' fascination and their eventual reflection on what Paul exposed about their own emptiness.

## Key Themes

- Performance and identity: Paul succeeds because he is more competent than the people he deceives
- Privilege, race, and the gatekeeping of elite culture
- The genuine connection that exists within the fraud — Ouisa's grief is real
- Six degrees as a metaphor for the illusion of intimacy across class distance
- Art, money, and the Kittredge painting: two-faced (Cézanne, Kandinsky) like everything in their life

## Why It Matters

Will Smith's first serious dramatic role and one of his best. Stockard Channing's Ouisa is the emotional center — she is genuinely changed by Paul in a way her husband is not. The film is John Guare's play and preserves its theatrical rhythms. The real Paul (David Hampton) was never prosecuted; he died in 2003. The film is about the transaction at the heart of every aspiration.
