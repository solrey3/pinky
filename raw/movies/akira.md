# Akira (1988)

**Director:** Katsuhiro Otomo
**Genre:** Animated sci-fi, Cyberpunk
**Runtime:** 124 min
**Country:** Japan

## Summary

Neo-Tokyo, 2019. A biker gang encounter triggers the awakening of Tetsuo's latent psychic powers. The government, the military, and a resistance movement all try to control or destroy him while Kaneda fights to save his friend. Tetsuo's powers grow beyond any containment, culminating in a cosmological transformation. Based on Otomo's manga.

## Key Themes

- Uncontrollable power and the horror of development without wisdom
- Post-war Japan's trauma processed through nuclear imagery and body horror
- The failure of the state to protect or serve its citizens — every institution is corrupt
- Friendship and loyalty tested against power and madness
- Cyberpunk dystopia: the city as both cage and expression of human ambition

## Why It Matters

The film that brought anime to Western audiences and proved animation could carry adult themes with cinematic sophistication. The animation budget was unprecedented — 68,000 hand-drawn frames, pre-recorded dialogue so lip movements could be animated precisely. The film influenced the visual language of *The Matrix*, *Ghost in the Shell*'s adaptation, and virtually every Western cyberpunk property since.
