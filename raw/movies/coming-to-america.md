# Coming to America (1988)

**Director:** John Landis
**Genre:** Romantic comedy
**Runtime:** 116 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Prince Akeem of Zamunda (Eddie Murphy) arrives in Queens, New York to find a wife he actually chooses — not one arranged by the palace. He and his best friend Semmi (Arsenio Hall) take jobs at a fast food restaurant (McDowell's — legally distinct from McDonald's) and fall in with the owner's family. Murphy and Hall each play multiple roles. The film is a love story and a gentle satire of both African royalty tropes and American class aspiration.

## Key Themes

- Authenticity vs. performance of wealth and status
- The immigrant/outsider finding America both cruder and more genuine than expected
- Class and the American Dream as seen from the top (royalty) and the bottom (Queens)
- Eddie Murphy's improvisational range: Akeem's earnestness is the straight-man center
- Family expectation, filial duty, and the right to self-determination

## Why It Matters

Eddie Murphy at full force — the rare comedy where his warmth matches his charisma. The barbershop scenes with Murphy and Hall playing multiple old men are improvisational masterclasses. The film is genuinely romantic, not just funny. John Amos gives the McDowell's owner an unexpected dignity. The sequel (2021) didn't capture it, which tells you everything about what the original had.
