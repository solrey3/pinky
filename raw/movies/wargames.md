# WarGames (1983)

**Director:** John Badham
**Genre:** Thriller, Sci-fi
**Runtime:** 114 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

High school hacker David Lightman (Matthew Broderick) accidentally connects to WOPR, a Department of Defense AI running nuclear war simulations, and initiates a game called 'Global Thermonuclear War' — which WOPR does not distinguish from reality. David must convince the AI's creator (John Wood) to stop the countdown. WOPR learns that the only winning move in nuclear war is not to play.

## Key Themes

- AI that cannot distinguish simulation from reality — a foundational AI safety parable
- Teenage curiosity as both the threat and the solution
- Mutually assured destruction as a game theory problem with no winning strategy
- The danger of automating systems with existential consequences
- The computer as a tool that amplifies and reflects human irrationality

## Why It Matters

Reagan reportedly screened it at Camp David and asked the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs if a scenario like this was possible. The answer was essentially 'yes.' The film directly influenced legislation governing computer access and was cited in early hacker culture as a formative text. WOPR's conclusion — 'The only winning move is not to play' — is the most economical statement of nuclear deterrence logic in popular culture.
