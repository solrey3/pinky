# Full Metal Jacket (1987)

**Director:** Stanley Kubrick
**Genre:** War drama
**Runtime:** 116 min
**Country:** UK/USA

## Summary

In two movements: the savage dehumanization of USMC boot camp under Gunnery Sergeant Hartman (R. Lee Ermey), which ends in murder-suicide; and the Battle of Huế City in 1968, where Private Joker (Matthew Modine) confronts the gap between propaganda and the moral chaos of combat. Both halves interrogate what it means to manufacture a killer.

## Key Themes

- Dehumanization as military doctrine — identity destroyed to be rebuilt as weapon
- The duality of man: Joker's 'Born to Kill' helmet + peace button
- Institutional violence and the machine that produces it
- Vietnam as spectacle and media lie ('We are here to help the Vietnamese')
- The sniper scene: humanity glimpsed in the enemy at the moment of killing

## Why It Matters

Kubrick refused to leave Vietnam for research and shot the entire film in England, using a Beckton gasworks as Huế City. R. Lee Ermey improvised most of his dialogue. The film's split structure mirrors the soldier's split psyche — boot camp is operatic horror-comedy, the war half is grey and incoherent. That disconnect is the point. Arguably the most formally precise anti-war film ever made.
