# Better Off Dead... (1985)

**Director:** Savage Steve Holland
**Genre:** Comedy, Romantic comedy
**Runtime:** 97 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Lane Myer (John Cusack) is dumped by his girlfriend Beth for the ski team captain. He spirals into theatrical, repeatedly failed suicide attempts, obsesses over winning a ski race against the captain on the killer K-12 slope, and slowly finds genuine connection with French exchange student Monique (Diane Franklin), who actually has problems worth having. A deeply weird, deeply 80s comedy.

## Key Themes

- Teenage heartbreak as apocalyptic event — subjective truth of adolescent emotion
- Surrealism as emotional expression: animated hamburgers, obsessive paperboy
- The ski race as existential reclamation — not winning the girl, proving to himself
- Class and aspiration in suburban 1980s America
- The foreigner as the only sane person: Monique sees Lane clearly when no one else does

## Why It Matters

A cult classic that Cusack famously disowned on release, calling it the worst film he'd ever seen. He was wrong. The film's surrealist logic — the Two Dollars paperboy, the animated hamburgers, Lane's cooking-obsessed mother — is consistent internal dream logic, not randomness. Monique is smarter and funnier than the film's marketing suggested. Rewatch value is extremely high.
