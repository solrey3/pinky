# Rounders (1998)

**Director:** John Dahl
**Genre:** Drama, Thriller
**Runtime:** 121 min
**Country:** USA

## Summary

Law student Mike McDermott (Matt Damon) gave up poker after being wiped out by Russian mobster Teddy KGB (John Malkovich). When his best friend Worm (Edward Norton) is released from prison with gambling debts to dangerous people, Mike returns to the tables. The film is a treatise on reading people, game theory in practice, and the difference between gambling and professional poker.

## Key Themes

- The poker tell as epistemology — Mike reads people, not cards
- Identity and vocation: law school is the wrong life for Mike, and everyone knows it
- Loyalty and its costs — Worm's self-destruction vs. Mike's discipline
- The poker table as meritocracy: skill asserts itself over time
- Oreo cookies: the most famous tell in cinema

## Why It Matters

The film that sparked the poker boom of the 2000s — entire generations of online poker players cite it. Damon and Norton's chemistry is excellent. Malkovich's Teddy KGB is a rare villain who loses credibly: he tilts, Mike reads it, and the film has the intellectual honesty to let that be the climax. 'He's a rounder' and the KGB speech are canonical poker culture texts.
