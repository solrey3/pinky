# Pearl Jam — Ten

**Release Date:** 1991-08-27
**Label:** Epic Records
**Producer:** Rick Parashar, Pearl Jam

## Overview

Ten is the debut studio album by American rock band Pearl Jam, released on August 27, 1991, through Epic Records. Following the dissolution of their previous band Mother Love Bone in 1990, bassist Jeff Ament and guitarist Stone Gossard began rehearsing with new guitarist Mike McCready.

Despite its reputation as a quintessential grunge album, Ten is often noted for displaying a stronger classic rock influence than other contemporary grunge releases, drawing comparisons to the work of Jimi Hendrix and Led Zeppelin. The album produced three hit singles: "Alive", "Even Flow", and "Jeremy".

By February 2013, it had sold 10 million copies in the US and has been certified 13× Platinum by the RIAA. In 2021, the album was inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame. Ten remains Pearl Jam's most commercially successful album.
