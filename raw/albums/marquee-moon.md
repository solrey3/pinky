# Television — Marquee Moon

**Release Date:** 1977-02-08
**Label:** Elektra Records
**Producer:** Tom Verlaine, Andy Johns

## Overview

Marquee Moon is the debut album by the American rock band Television, released on February 8, 1977, by Elektra Records. The group rehearsed extensively in preparation for Marquee Moon before recording it at A & R Recording in September 1976.

For Marquee Moon, Verlaine and fellow guitarist Richard Lloyd abandoned contemporary punk rock's power chords in favor of rock and jazz-inspired interplay, melodic lines, and counter-melodies. The resulting music is largely hook-driven with complex instrumental parts, while evoking themes of adolescence, discovery, and transcendence.

Released to widespread acclaim, Marquee Moon was hailed by critics as an original musical development in rock music. Among the most acclaimed music releases in history, it consistently features in professionally curated lists of top albums. Marquee Moon also proved to be a foundational record of alternative rock.
