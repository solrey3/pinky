# Wilco — Yankee Hotel Foxtrot

**Release Date:** 2002-04-23
**Label:** Nonesuch Records
**Producer:** Wilco

## Overview

Yankee Hotel Foxtrot is the fourth studio album by the American rock band Wilco, originally self-released on September 29, 2001, on Wilco's website as streamed audio, and later as a retail release on April 23, 2002, through Nonesuch Records. Recording sessions for the album began in late 2000.

The album showcased a more atmospheric and experimental sound than the band's previous work. It was the band's first album with drummer Glenn Kotche, and last with multi-instrumentalist and songwriter Jay Bennett. Reprise Records, Wilco's record label at the time, refused to release the album, leading to Wilco's departure from Reprise.

Yankee Hotel Foxtrot received widespread acclaim from music critics at release, and is widely regarded as one of the greatest albums of the 2000s and of all time. In 2020, Rolling Stone ranked the album at number 225 on its updated "500 Greatest Albums of All Time" list.
