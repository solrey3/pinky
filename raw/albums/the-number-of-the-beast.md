# Iron Maiden — The Number Of The Beast

**Release Date:** 1982-03-22

## Overview

## Wisdom / Themes

# SUMMARY
Iron Maiden presents their 1982 album The Number Of The Beast, discussing heavy metal themes, new vocalist, and classic tracks in music history.

# IDEAS
- Iron Maiden released their breakthrough album in March of 1982 worldwide.
- The album marked the debut of vocalist Bruce Dickinson with the band.
- Title track explores themes of nightmares, evil, and satanic imagery boldly.
- Hallowed Be Thy Name became one of their most enduring fan favorites.
- Run to the Hills addressed historical conflicts between settlers and Native Americans.
- The album cover featured Eddie battling the devil in dramatic style.
- Production was handled by Martin Birch who shaped their signature sound.
- Children of the Damned drew inspiration from classic horror movie concepts.
- 22 Acacia Avenue continued the story from previous album character Charlotte.
- The Prisoner track referenced the cult British television series directly.
- Album achieved massive commercial success reaching number one in UK charts.
- Controversies arose over alleged satanic messages sparking public debates then.
- Songs showcased complex riffs, galloping bass lines and powerful drumming.
- This release solidified Iron Maiden as leaders in New Wave of British.
- Live performances of these tracks became staples in their world tours.
- Lyrics often blended historical events with fictional horror storytelling elements.

# FACTS
- The album was recorded in Nassau at Compass Point Studios Bahamas.
- It sold over 15 million copies across the entire world.
- Title song reached number 18 on the UK singles chart.
- Dickinson joined after auditioning successfully with several other candidates.
- The cover art was painted by artist Derek Riggs originally.
- Several tracks were written during the previous tour by members.
