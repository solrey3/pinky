# Elgin Baylor

**Born:** 1934, Washington DC
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** SF
**Active:** 1958–1972
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 5 (#26)

## Summary

The first above-the-rim player in NBA history and Julius Erving's acknowledged inspiration. Baylor averaged 27.4 ppg and 13.5 rpg for his career — numbers that rank among the all-time best — while playing for a Lakers team that went to the Finals eight times and won zero. His scoring record (he once scored 71 points in a single game) predates the shot clock era changes.

## Career Highlights

- 11x NBA All-Star; 10x All-NBA First Team; averaged 27.4 ppg career
- His 71-point single-game performance (1960) stood as a record until Wilt's 100
- His Finals appearances without a championship (eight Finals losses) is the most unfortunate finals record in NBA history
- The first player to move laterally in midair — the hang time moves that Dr. J and Jordan copied are traced to Baylor
- Retired in 1971, nine games before the Lakers won their title that season — the cruelest timing in NBA history

## Why They Matter

Baylor's legacy is entirely statistical and stylistic — he was the first, and his championship record (eight Finals losses) is not his failure, it's the team construction's. His retirement nine games before the Lakers' 1972 championship is the NBA's most melancholy near-miss.
