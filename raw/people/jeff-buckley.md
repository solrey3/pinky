# Jeff Buckley

**Born:** 1966, Anaheim CA
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer, songwriter, guitarist
**Active:** 1990–1997

## Summary

The greatest male vocalist of his generation and the most tragic single-album artist in music. Grace (1994) is the most technically demanding vocal album in rock — his cover of Leonard Cohen's 'Hallelujah' is the definitive version, the one that replaced Cohen's in popular consciousness. Drowned at 30 in the Mississippi River while fully clothed.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Grace (1994) — #41 personal ranking; the most technically demanding vocal album in rock music
- His 'Hallelujah' cover is the definitive recording of Leonard Cohen's most famous song — displacing the original in popular consciousness
- His combination of Led Zeppelin-level guitar and Édith Piaf-level vocal range is technically unprecedented in rock
- He died before completing his second album — the unfinished My Sweetheart the Drunk demonstrates what was coming
- His four-octave range combined with emotional directness is the most complete vocal instrument in rock history

## Why They Matter

Buckley's death is music's most complete 'what if' — Grace is extraordinary, the incomplete second album suggests even more, and his live performances suggest a performer who was only beginning to realize his range. He is the most discussed single-album legacy in rock.
