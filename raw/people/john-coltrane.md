# John Coltrane

**Born:** 1926, Hamlet NC
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Saxophonist, composer
**Active:** 1955–1967

## Summary

The most technically advanced saxophonist in jazz history and the primary architect of the avant-garde movement in jazz. 'A Love Supreme' (1964) is the most spiritual recording in jazz. His sheets of sound technique and his ability to move through multiple chord changes in a single breath phrase are technically unrepeated.

## Key Works / Highlights

- A Love Supreme (1964) — the greatest statement of spiritual jazz, consistently voted the greatest jazz album of all time by musicians
- Giant Steps (1960) — 'Coltrane changes' (his chord substitution system) are still studied in conservatories as the most advanced harmonic theory in jazz
- His work on Kind of Blue (with Miles Davis) is the most important saxophone performance on the most important album in jazz
- The Classic Quartet (1961–1965: McCoy Tyner, Jimmy Garrison, Elvin Jones) is the greatest small group in jazz history
- His death at 40 (from liver cancer) ended a career that was moving into territory no one has fully mapped since

## Why They Matter

Coltrane is jazz's most intense figure — a man who practiced eight or more hours a day and whose music reflected that practice as pure spiritual inquiry rather than technique display. His influence on saxophone playing is total; most saxophonists since 1960 are either playing in his tradition or deliberately avoiding it.
