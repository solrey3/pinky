# Billie Holiday

**Born:** 1915, Baltimore MD
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer
**Active:** 1933–1959

## Summary

The greatest jazz vocalist and the most emotionally direct singer in American music. Holiday's ability to communicate suffering through vocal technique — delayed timing, note bending, emotional directness — is unmatched. 'Strange Fruit' (1939) is the most politically significant song in American music history.

## Key Works / Highlights

- 'Strange Fruit' (1939) — the first major anti-lynching protest song and the most politically courageous recording in American music
- Lady in Satin (1958) — recorded while dying, it's the most emotionally honest vocal album in jazz
- Her collaborations with Lester Young (who named her 'Lady Day') are the most harmonically intimate in jazz history
- Her autobiography 'Lady Sings the Blues' is the most honest memoir in popular music
- Her life — addiction, abuse, arrest for drug possession — is the original story of the music industry's exploitation of Black women artists

## Why They Matter

Holiday's voice on Lady in Satin is damaged, fragile, and irreplaceable — a document of what a life sounds like when it's almost over. 'Strange Fruit' alone justifies her place in history as a political as well as musical figure. She sang what she lived, which is the hardest thing.
