# Diesel / Big Daddy Cool

**Real Name:** Kevin Nash
**Born:** 1959, Detroit MI
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Active:** 1990–2011
**Personal Rank:** #57 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#57. The man who beat Goldberg. Nash held the WWF Championship for nearly a year (1994–1995) as Diesel — a reign that drew decent but not spectacular business. His nWo founding membership is what defines him: the idea that major WWF stars could jump to WCW and challenge the established order was revolutionary. His Powerbomb finisher on small opponents is genuinely dangerous and looks appropriately so.

## Career Highlights

- WWF Champion; WCW Champion; nWo founding member
- nWo founding member at Bash at the Beach 1996 — the invasion angle that changed wrestling forever
- His Jackknife Powerbomb ended Goldberg's 173-0 streak — the most significant single act of a post-Attitude Era big man
- The Wolfpac nWo red-and-black was the coolest wrestling aesthetic of the late 90s
- His friendship with Shawn Michaels (Kliq) shaped WWE's backstage power structure for a decade

## Why They Matter

Nash's legacy is entirely contextual — he was in the right place (nWo, Kliq) at the right time to be part of wrestling's defining moments. His individual contributions are solid if not spectacular.
