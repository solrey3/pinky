# Michael Jackson

**Born:** 1958, Gary IN
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer, dancer, songwriter, producer
**Active:** 1969–2009

## Summary

The King of Pop. The most commercially successful recording artist in history. Thriller (1982) is the best-selling album of all time (66+ million copies). His combination of vocal ability, dance innovation (moonwalk), and visual storytelling in music videos transformed how pop music was consumed. His 'Dangerous' world tour is the highest-grossing concert tour in history at the time.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Thriller (1982) — the best-selling album of all time; 66+ million copies
- Off the Wall (1979) — his first fully realized artistic statement; produced by Quincy Jones
- His moonwalk (introduced at Motown 25, 1983) is the most famous dance move in popular music history
- His music videos ('Thriller,' 'Beat It,' 'Black or White') transformed the medium from promotional tool to art form
- His personal legal troubles (1993, 2005) and subsequent reputation rehabilitation are the most complex legacy story in pop music

## Why They Matter

Jackson's commercial dominance is beyond comparison — no other solo artist has sold what Thriller has sold. His dance vocabulary entered the physical lexicon of popular culture globally. His personal legacy is complicated in ways that require careful handling; his musical legacy is not.
