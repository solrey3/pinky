# King Kong Bundy

**Real Name:** Christopher Pallies
**Born:** 1955, Atlantic City NJ
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Active:** 1977–1995
**Personal Rank:** #80 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#80. The quintessential monster heel of the WWF's formative years. Bundy's five-count challenge (demanding a count to five rather than three to acknowledge he'd truly beaten his opponent) was a character detail that made him feel genuinely sadistic. His WM2 main event with Hogan in a steel cage was the first WM main event that used the structure WWE would repeat for 40 years.

## Career Highlights

- WWF Championship contender; WM2 main event
- His WM2 match with Hogan was the first cage match in WrestleMania history
- The five-count gimmick is one of wrestling's best character-specific rule modifications
- His Saturday Night's Main Event appearances helped build WWE's television credibility
- His cameo on Married... with Children brought wrestling into mainstream sitcom culture

## Why They Matter

Bundy's career is defined by his utility as a large, credible threat for babyfaces to overcome. He served the function he was designed to serve, and he served it well. His place on this list reflects personal nostalgia as much as objective assessment.
