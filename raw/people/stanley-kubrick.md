# Stanley Kubrick

**Born:** 1928, Manhattan NY
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Film director, producer, screenwriter
**Active:** 1951–1999

## Summary

The most technically perfectionist and thematically ambitious director in cinema history. His catalog — Spartacus, Lolita, Dr. Strangelove, 2001: A Space Odyssey, A Clockwork Orange, Barry Lyndon, The Shining, Full Metal Jacket, Eyes Wide Shut — spans more genres, decades, and formal innovations than any other director. He had the longest gaps between films (years of research) and the longest shooting schedules (Barry Lyndon took 365 days).

## Key Works / Highlights

- 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968) — the most technically and philosophically ambitious sci-fi film ever made
- Dr. Strangelove (1964) — the greatest political satire film ever made; also in personal film rankings
- Full Metal Jacket (1987) — the most formally precise anti-war film ever made
- A Clockwork Orange (1971) — the most controversial film in the British Film Institute's history
- The Shining (1980) — the most discussed and rewatched horror film in history

## Why They Matter

Kubrick's technique — the one-point perspective shot, the extreme close-up/extreme wide-shot editing pattern, the classical music scoring — influenced every cinematographer trained between 1970 and 2000. His films have never received a definitive interpretation, which is their most permanent quality. He built films that generate new meanings for new viewers in new contexts.
