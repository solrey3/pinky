# George Orwell

**Born:** 1903, Motihari, British India
**Nationality:** British
**Profession:** Novelist, essayist, journalist
**Active:** 1928–1950

## Summary

The most politically essential British writer of the 20th century. 1984 and Animal Farm (1945) between them constituted the most complete fictional critique of totalitarianism ever produced. His journalism (Down and Out in Paris and London, Homage to Catalonia) reflected direct experience rather than theoretical critique. Newspeak, Big Brother, doublethink — his vocabulary has become the vocabulary of political discourse.

## Key Works / Highlights

- 1984 (1949) — the most politically consequential novel in the English language; its terms have entered common political usage
- Animal Farm (1945) — the most economical satire of Soviet communism in literature
- Homage to Catalonia (1938) — the most honest account of a political commitment turning to disillusionment
- His essay 'Politics and the English Language' (1946) is the most useful piece of writing advice in the 20th century
- He died of tuberculosis at 46 — 1984 was completed as he was dying

## Why They Matter

Orwell's legacy is in the vocabulary he created: Big Brother, doublethink, unperson, Room 101, Newspeak. These words exist because he invented them, and they exist because the concepts they describe are real. His journalism added the credential that his fiction was not theoretical.
