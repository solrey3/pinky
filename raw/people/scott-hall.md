# Razor Ramon / Bad Guy

**Real Name:** Scott Hall
**Born:** 1958, Baltimore MD
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Active:** 1984–2010
**Personal Rank:** #44 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#44. The man who invented the ladder match. Scott Hall's Razor Ramon character — modeled on Scarface's Tony Montana — was the cool heel character that prefigured the Attitude Era's moral ambiguity. His IC ladder matches with Shawn Michaels at WM10 and SummerSlam 1995 are the two ladder matches that built the modern stipulation's mythology. His nWo founding membership with Hogan and Nash changed wrestling forever.

## Career Highlights

- WWF Intercontinental Champion (4x); WCW nWo member
- WM10 ladder match with HBK is the match that launched the ladder match as a modern staple
- nWo founding member — his invasion of Nitro with Hogan was the most seismic moment in the Monday Night War
- The Bad Guy character (cool, effortlessly charismatic, morally flexible) was five years ahead of the Attitude Era
- His personal struggles with addiction are documented in an ESPN 30 for 30 ('The Weight') — a genuinely sad story

## Why They Matter

Hall was robbed of a world title run by his personal struggles. On talent and charisma alone, he was as good as anyone in the WWF at his peak. His career is the wrestling tragedy defined by what didn't happen rather than what did.
