# Vince Gilligan

**Born:** 1967, Farmville VA
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Television writer, producer, director
**Active:** 1995–present

## Summary

Creator of Breaking Bad and co-creator of Better Call Saul — the two most tightly plotted television dramas in history. Gilligan's stated premise for Breaking Bad ('turn Mr. Chips into Scarface') is the most precisely articulated character arc premise in television. His attention to foreshadowing (Chekhov's gun applied to every detail) produces the most consistently rewarding rewatches.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Breaking Bad (2008–2013) — #5 personal TV ranking; the most tightly plotted drama in television history
- Better Call Saul (2015–2022) — #14 personal TV ranking; many critics' argument that it surpassed the original
- His 'Breaking Bad' premise ('turn Mr. Chips into Scarface') is the most precisely described character arc in television development history
- His X-Files writing work (1995–2002) prepared him for episodic storytelling with embedded mythology
- His attention to foreshadowing (the pilot's detail paying off in the finale) produces the most rewarding rewatch in television

## Why They Matter

Gilligan's contribution is plotting precision — he created two shows where every detail matters and every planted element pays off. The complaint that Breaking Bad is 'too neat' is the argument that confirms his achievement: he built a five-season drama with the structural integrity of a two-hour film. That has never been done before or since at his level.
