# Aaron McGruder

**Born:** 1974, Chicago IL
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Cartoonist, television writer, producer
**Active:** 1999–2014

## Summary

Creator of The Boondocks — the most politically serious animated show ever produced. McGruder's strip (1999–2006) and series (2005–2014) provided the most furious and precise critique of post-9/11 Black American culture and American politics from within Black cultural production. His 'Return of the King' episode (MLK wakes from a coma) is the most politically complete single episode of animation ever made.

## Key Works / Highlights

- The Boondocks (2005–2014) — #9 personal TV ranking; the most politically serious animated television series
- The Boondocks comic strip (1999–2006) — ran in 350 newspapers; the most politically engaged newspaper comic of its era
- 'Return of the King' (Season 1 episode) — the greatest single episode of political animation in American television
- His critique of BET and the entertainment industry's influence on Black culture was more precise than any media criticism of the era
- His conflict with Cartoon Network over content was the most documented artist-vs-network dispute in animated television

## Why They Matter

McGruder's contribution is courage — he said things about Black American culture and American politics on Cartoon Network that no one else would say in that medium, at that commercial scale, with that audience. The show's conflict with the network is itself the argument about what comedy is permitted to criticize.
