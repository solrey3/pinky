# Walt Frazier

**Born:** 1945, Atlanta GA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PG
**Active:** 1967–1980
**Personal NBA Tier:** Level 2

## Summary

Clyde. The greatest defensive point guard of the pre-Jordan era and the anchor of the most intellectually sophisticated team in NBA history (1970 and 1973 Knicks). His Game 7 performance in the 1970 Finals (36 pts, 19 ast) — the night Willis Reed limped to the court — is the greatest individual PG Finals performance before Magic.

## Career Highlights

- 2x NBA Champion (1970, 1973 New York Knicks); 7x All-Star; 7x All-Defensive First Team
- His 1970 Finals Game 7 (36 pts, 19 ast) is the greatest game 7 performance by a PG in Finals history
- His fashion sense (Clyde Frazier, named after the Bonnie & Clyde film) was the first NBA player to become a style icon
- His color commentary work with the Knicks has produced 30+ years of unique compound words ('releasing and casing,' 'dishing and swishing')
- His defensive IQ is the model for the modern on-ball defender position
