# Prince

**Born:** 1958, Minneapolis MN
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Multi-instrumentalist, singer, songwriter, producer
**Active:** 1978–2016

## Summary

The most musically complete individual in popular music history. Prince played every instrument on most of his albums, produced everything, engineered recording sessions that lasted until dawn, and released music at a rate that would have been productive for a full band. Sign o' the Times (1987) — his #24 personal ranking — is the argument for the double album as a canvas only a complete musician can fill.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Sign o' the Times (1987) — widely considered the greatest pop double album ever made
- Purple Rain (1984) — one of the highest-grossing concert films ever made and the commercial apex of his career
- His guitar solo at the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame tribute for George Harrison ('While My Guitar Gently Weeps') is the most celebrated individual performance at any music industry event
- His protege relationships (Sheila E., Morris Day and The Time, Sinead O'Connor) built a Minneapolis funk scene from one person's vision
- His battles with Warner Bros. over master recording ownership prefigured every subsequent artist-vs-label dispute

## Why They Matter

Prince's career is the most complete argument in pop music that one person can do everything. He is the standard against which all multi-hyphenate musicians are measured. His guitar on the Harrison tribute is the most unexpected revelation in rock — everyone knew he was a pop star; that performance revealed he was one of the three greatest rock guitarists of all time.
