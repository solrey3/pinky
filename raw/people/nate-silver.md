# Nate Silver

**Born:** 1978, East Lansing MI
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Statistician, analyst, author
**Active:** 2003–present

## Summary

The most influential statistical analyst in American journalism. Silver's FiveThirtyEight (founded 2008) pioneered applied probability in political journalism — translating polling data into probability estimates that were more accurate than pundit predictions. His Signal and the Noise (2012) is the best popular treatment of forecasting and Bayesian reasoning in print.

## Key Works / Highlights

- FiveThirtyEight (2008–present) — the foundational political forecasting outlet in American journalism
- The Signal and the Noise (2012) — the best popular book on Bayesian thinking and forecasting
- His 2008 and 2012 presidential election predictions (correct in 49/50 and 50/50 states respectively) validated the probabilistic approach
- PECOTA (his baseball forecasting system) was baseball analytics' most important post-Moneyball tool
- He left FiveThirtyEight in 2023 to launch Silver Bulletin, returning to independent forecasting

## Why They Matter

Silver's contribution is methodological humility — the insistence that predictions should come with probabilities rather than certainties, and that those probabilities should be calibrated against outcomes. His work changed how American journalism talked about elections, sports, and uncertainty.
