# Curtis Mayfield

**Born:** 1942, Chicago IL
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer, songwriter, guitarist, producer
**Active:** 1958–1999

## Summary

The most politically engaged soul musician of his era. Mayfield's Superfly soundtrack (1972) elevated the film soundtrack as a political and musical document. His work with The Impressions and his solo career produced some of the most beautifully orchestrated political soul music ever made. Left paralyzed from the neck down in 1990 from a stage lighting rig accident.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Superfly (1972) — the most politically sophisticated film soundtrack in American music
- 'People Get Ready' (1965, The Impressions) — the civil rights movement's most beautiful song
- His guitar style (open tuning, falsetto combined with guitar tone) is one of the most distinctive in soul music
- His Chicago soul productions (for himself and others) are the most orchestrally advanced in the genre
- He continued recording after being paralyzed in 1990, recording vocals lying flat — his final album is a document of extraordinary determination

## Why They Matter

Mayfield's voice and guitar tone are both immediately identifiable — no one sounds like him. His political commitment was consistent across three decades without ever becoming didactic. His Superfly is the argument that a film soundtrack can be more artistically significant than the film it scores.
