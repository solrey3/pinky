# Bob Dylan

**Born:** 1941, Duluth MN
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer-songwriter, poet
**Active:** 1961–present

## Summary

The most influential songwriter in the history of popular music. Dylan's fusion of folk, rock, and poetry in the early 1960s redefined what a pop song could be. His 'Highway 61 Revisited' and 'Blonde on Blonde' albums (1965–1966) are the peak of rock songwriting. Nobel Prize in Literature (2016) — the first given to a popular musician.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Nobel Prize in Literature (2016) — first popular musician to receive it
- Blood on the Tracks (1975) — #3 personal ranking; one of the most emotionally specific breakup albums ever made
- Blonde on Blonde (1966) and Highway 61 Revisited (1965) are the Twin Peaks of 1960s rock songwriting
- His acoustic-to-electric transition at Newport Folk Festival (1965) is the most consequential genre shift in rock history
- His output across six decades with consistent artistic ambition is the longest sustained creative career in popular music

## Why They Matter

Dylan's contribution is language — he took the lyrical content of popular music from teenage romance to politics, philosophy, and modernist poetry. The electric transition was not just stylistic; it was a statement that art doesn't owe its audience consistency. His Nobel Prize validated the argument.
