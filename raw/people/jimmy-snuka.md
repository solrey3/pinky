# Jimmy Snuka

**Real Name:** James Reiher
**Born:** 1943, Fiji
**Nationality:** Fijian-American
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Active:** 1969–2015
**Personal Rank:** #54 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#54. The man who changed wrestling's aerial dimension. Snuka's Superfly splash from the top rope was the most spectacular move in WWE's formative years, and his dive from the top of the steel cage at Madison Square Garden (1983) onto Don Muraco is the defining image of wrestling's theatrical potential. Also implicated in the death of his girlfriend in 1983 — a case that was re-opened in 2015.

## Career Highlights

- Multiple AWA/WWF champion
- His cage dive onto Muraco (MSG, 1983) is the image that inspired every high-risk wrestler since
- The Superfly splash is wrestling's first truly theatrical finishing move — pure spectacle over technique
- His relationship with the Anoa'i family (the Samoan dynasty) is part of wrestling's most influential family tree
- His legal situation (re-opened 2015, died before trial in 2017) complicates his legacy significantly

## Why They Matter

Snuka's place in history is genuine and complicated in equal measure. His aerial work is the direct ancestor of the modern high-flying style. His personal situation reflects wrestling's long history of ignoring violence outside the ring.
