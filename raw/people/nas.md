# Nas

**Born:** 1973, Brooklyn NY
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Rapper, entrepreneur
**Active:** 1994–present

## Summary

Illmatic (1994) is universally considered the greatest hip-hop album ever made. Nas arrived at 20 with a fully realized poetic voice, technical precision, and a narrative scope that hip-hop had not previously achieved. His subsequent career has been inconsistent but his peak defines the ceiling for what a rapper can do with language.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Illmatic (1994) — the canonical greatest hip-hop album; perfect by every critical metric
- God's Son (2002) — his other major commercial and critical statement
- His feud with Jay-Z ('Ether' vs. 'Takeover') is hip-hop's definitive beef; 'Ether' (his response) is the best diss track in rap history
- His mentorship of the Queensbridge rapper tradition established a New York identity in hip-hop that predated and survived the Bad Boy/Death Row commercial peak
- His literary references (street life filtered through genuine education) distinguish him from contemporaries

## Why They Matter

Illmatic is the argument that a rapper can do everything right on a first album. The production (Pete Rock, DJ Premier, Q-Tip, Large Professor, L.E.S.), the lyrics, the storytelling, the compactness (10 tracks, 39 minutes) — nothing is wasted. His subsequent career could not match it, which is not a failure; it is a testament to how high he set the standard at 20.
