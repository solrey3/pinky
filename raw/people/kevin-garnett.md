# Kevin Garnett

**Born:** 1976, Mauldin SC
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PF
**Active:** 1995–2016
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 4 (#19)

## Summary

The most emotionally intense competitor in the power forward position. KG's championship (2008 Celtics) was preceded by 13 years of excellence in Minnesota without championship support — making the title feel genuinely earned in a way that player movement-era championships cannot replicate. His defensive intensity, his vocal leadership, and his versatility defined the modern stretch-4 position.

## Career Highlights

- 1x NBA Champion (2008 Boston Celtics); 1x Defensive Player of the Year (2008); 1x regular-season MVP (2004)
- His 2004 MVP season in Minnesota (24.2 ppg, 13.9 rpg, 5.0 apg) is the most complete forward season of that decade
- 15x All-Star; 9x All-Defensive First Team — his defensive production rivals his offensive record
- His emotional leadership — the screaming, the pounding the scorer's table — became the NBA's template for intensity
- The 2008 Celtics (Garnett, Pierce, Allen) is the best team constructed through trade in NBA history

## Why They Matter

KG is the reason the modern power forward shoots from outside. He could legitimately guard centers while also stepping out to cover wings. His emotional investment made him different from quieter excellent players — you always knew where he was and what he meant to his team.
