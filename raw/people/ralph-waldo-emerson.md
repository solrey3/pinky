# Ralph Waldo Emerson

**Born:** 1803, Boston MA
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Essayist, poet, philosopher
**Active:** 1820–1882

## Summary

The philosophical architect of American individualism. 'Self-Reliance' (1841) is the most influential essay in American philosophy — its argument that conformity is the enemy of greatness and that the individual conscience is the final authority has shaped every American counterculture from Thoreau to the Beats to Silicon Valley.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Self-Reliance (1841) — the most influential essay in American philosophy; 'Whoso would be a man must be a nonconformist'
- His Transcendentalism school (with Thoreau, Alcott, Fuller) created the first distinctly American philosophical movement
- His Concord Hymn (1837) — 'the shot heard round the world' — is the most frequently quoted American historical poem
- His influence on Nietzsche's individualism, Whitman's expansive self, and William James's pragmatism is direct and documented
- His concept of the Oversoul anticipates both Jungian collective unconscious and New Age spiritual individualism

## Why They Matter

Emerson's influence is the most thoroughly dispersed in American intellectual history — you find him in every American who ever believed their individual judgment outweighed collective opinion. The problem Ralph Ellison identified — that Emerson's individualism was a privilege of the white man who could afford to transcend social constraints — is the honest counterargument that makes Emerson more rather than less important.
