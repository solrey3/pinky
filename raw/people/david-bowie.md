# David Bowie

**Born:** 1947, Brixton, London
**Nationality:** British
**Profession:** Singer, songwriter, actor, artist
**Active:** 1964–2016

## Summary

The most theatrical and chameleonic artist in rock history. Bowie's reinventions — Ziggy Stardust, Aladdin Sane, Thin White Duke, plastic soul, Berlin trilogy, mainstream 80s, drum and bass 90s — are the template for artistic self-invention. His Berlin trilogy (Low, Heroes, Lodger, 1977–1979, with Brian Eno) is the most formally adventurous work in his catalog.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Ziggy Stardust (1972) — the first rock concept album built around a fully realized alter ego
- Heroes (1977, Berlin trilogy) — the peak of his collaboration with Brian Eno; 'Heroes' is the most genuinely inspiring song in rock
- His theatrical influence (androgyny, costume, persona) is the direct ancestor of every theatrical pop artist since Madonna
- Blackstar (2016) — released two days before his death from cancer; a conscious farewell that stands as one of music's most intentional final statements
- His acting career (The Man Who Fell to Earth, Labyrinth) extended his artistic reach into film

## Why They Matter

Bowie's career is the argument for reinvention as a creative principle rather than a marketing strategy. Each transition was driven by artistic curiosity rather than commercial calculation. His final album (Blackstar) — released while he was dying, structured as a farewell — is the most deliberately composed exit in music.
