# Kendrick Lamar

**Born:** 1987, Compton CA
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Rapper, songwriter, producer
**Active:** 2004–present

## Summary

The greatest rapper of his generation and the most artistically ambitious hip-hop artist since Nas. His four albums (good kid, m.A.A.d city; To Pimp a Butterfly; DAMN.; Mr. Morale & The Big Steppers) represent the most sustained artistic sequence in hip-hop. Pulitzer Prize for DAMN. (2018) — the first non-classical/non-jazz Pulitzer for music. His Drake diss campaign (2023-2024) produced 'Not Like Us', the most commercially successful diss track in history.

## Key Works / Highlights

- To Pimp a Butterfly (2015) — the most artistically ambitious rap album of the decade; every track a formal experiment
- DAMN. (2018) — Pulitzer Prize, the first for hip-hop; his most radio-accessible album is also his most thematically compact
- good kid, m.A.A.d city (2012) — the best concept album in hip-hop; a narrative album with beginning, middle, and end
- GNX (2024) — #44 personal ranking; a West Coast victory lap after the Drake feud
- The Drake diss (2024): 'Not Like Us' is the most commercially successful single diss track in music history

## Why They Matter

Lamar is hip-hop's most complete argument that the genre is literature. His Pulitzer is not a marketing claim — it's a recognition that DAMN. has the formal properties of poetry. His Drake diss campaign showed he can operate at the commercial level of pop without sacrificing craft. His Super Bowl halftime show (2025) was the most politically engaged performance in the event's history.
