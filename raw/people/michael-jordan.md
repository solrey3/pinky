# Michael Jordan

**Born:** 1963, Brooklyn NY
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** SG
**Active:** 1984–2003
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 1 (#1)

## Summary

The greatest basketball player of all time by the consensus of this list and most serious analysts. Six NBA championships, six Finals MVPs, five regular-season MVPs — Jordan never lost in the Finals. His Chicago Bulls dynasty (1991–93, 1996–98) is the most dominant six-year run in NBA history. His competitiveness was pathological in the best possible sense.

## Career Highlights

- 6x NBA Champion, 6x Finals MVP, 5x regular-season MVP
- His 1996 season (72-10 regular season record) is the greatest team season in NBA history
- The Shot over Craig Ehlo, The Flu Game, The Shrug — he produced a defining moment in nearly every playoff series
- His scoring title record (10 consecutive, 1988–1998) has never been approached
- Baseball retirement (1993–1995) is the only interruption in an otherwise unbroken dominance

## Why They Matter

Jordan's legacy is beyond debate in terms of achievement. The argument about whether LeBron surpassed him is a testament to how high Jordan set the standard. His competitive drive — the gambling, the relentlessness, the refusal to let anyone beat him — is the defining characteristic of the greatest competitor in team sports history.
