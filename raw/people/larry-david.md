# Larry David

**Born:** 1947, Brooklyn NY
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Television writer, comedian, producer, actor
**Active:** 1977–present

## Summary

Co-creator of Seinfeld and creator of Curb Your Enthusiasm. David's 'no hugging, no learning' rule is the most influential single creative principle in American sitcom history. His philosophy — social contracts are real, most people violate them, and pointing this out politely but insistently is the correct response — is both a comedy principle and a moral one.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Seinfeld (co-creator, 1989–1998) — #25 personal TV ranking; the most influential American sitcom
- Curb Your Enthusiasm (2000–2024) — #33 personal TV ranking; his most personal and most complete artistic statement
- His 'no hugging, no learning' rule changed what American sitcoms were permitted to do after 1990
- The Festivus episode (Seinfeld) and the Palestine Chicken episode (Curb) are his two most perfectly constructed comedy texts
- His Curb Your Enthusiasm finale (2024) closed the show with the appropriate degree of Larry-ness

## Why They Matter

David's contribution is permission — he gave American television comedy permission to be about nothing, to learn nothing, and to let its characters suffer from their own moral failures without rescue. The post-Seinfeld comedy landscape (It's Always Sunny, Arrested Development, Veep) is the direct consequence of his creative principle.
