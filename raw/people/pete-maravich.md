# Pete Maravich

**Born:** 1947, Aliquippa PA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PG/SG
**Active:** 1970–1980
**Personal NBA Tier:** Level 1

## Summary

Pistol Pete. The most creative scorer and ball-handler in basketball before the modern era. His college statistics at LSU (44.2 ppg career over 3 seasons, without a three-point line) are so extreme that they cannot be contextualized by any current standard. Never won an NBA championship; won a scoring title (1977). Died at 40 from an undetected heart defect.

## Career Highlights

- NCAA scoring record (44.2 ppg career — most under any conditions in college basketball history)
- 1x NBA scoring champion (1977)
- His ball-handling moves in pre-YouTube 1970s film are as sophisticated as any current player's
- His passing innovation (behind-the-back, no-look) invented the vocabulary of creative passing
- He died at 40 playing pickup basketball — the cruelest timing for a player who spent his whole life playing
