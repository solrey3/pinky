# Michael Lewis

**Born:** 1960, New Orleans LA
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Journalist, author
**Active:** 1984–present

## Summary

The greatest narrative non-fiction writer in American journalism. Lewis's ability to make complex systems (derivatives, sabermetrics, behavioral economics) comprehensible through character-driven storytelling is unmatched. Moneyball, Liar's Poker, The Big Short, Flash Boys, The Fifth Risk — each book has changed how its subject is publicly understood.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Moneyball (2003) — the most influential sports analytics book ever written; changed how baseball teams evaluate players
- Liar's Poker (1989) — the foundational document of 1980s Wall Street culture
- The Big Short (2010) — the clearest popular explanation of the 2008 financial crisis and the people who bet against it
- The Undoing Project (2016) — the most accessible popular treatment of Kahneman and Tversky's behavioral economics
- Flash Boys (2014) — exposed high-frequency trading to public awareness and sparked regulatory debate

## Why They Matter

Lewis is the rare journalist who creates fields rather than covering them. Moneyball didn't just explain sabermetrics — it accelerated its adoption. The Big Short didn't just explain the financial crisis — it named villains and heroes in a way that shaped regulatory responses. His books become the texts through which their subjects are understood.
