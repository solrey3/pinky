# Paul Thomas Anderson

**Born:** 1970, Studio City CA
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Film director, writer
**Active:** 1993–present

## Summary

The greatest American director of his generation. Hard Eight, Boogie Nights, Magnolia, Punch-Drunk Love, There Will Be Blood, The Master, Inherent Vice, Phantom Thread, Licorice Pizza — each film is formally distinct from the others. His ability to direct actors to career-best performances (Burt Reynolds, Tom Cruise, Daniel Day-Lewis, Joaquin Phoenix, Philip Seymour Hoffman) is unparalleled in his era.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Boogie Nights (1997) — in personal film rankings; his commercial breakthrough; the best ensemble American film of the 1990s
- Magnolia (1999) — in personal film rankings; his most ambitious and polarizing film
- There Will Be Blood (2007) — Daniel Day-Lewis's best performance; one of the greatest American films of the 21st century
- The Master (2012) — Joaquin Phoenix and Philip Seymour Hoffman in the most mutually committed two-character performance in recent cinema
- His opening tracking shots (Boogie Nights, Magnolia) are the most technically accomplished in modern American cinema

## Why They Matter

PTA's directorial range — from the pornography industry (Boogie Nights) to American empire (There Will Be Blood) to religious cult dynamics (The Master) — is wider than any other contemporary director. His actor collaborations are the most consistently productive in cinema: he gets career-best work from almost everyone.
