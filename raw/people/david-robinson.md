# David Robinson

**Born:** 1965, Key West FL
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** C
**Active:** 1989–2003
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 5 (#30)

## Summary

The Admiral. Two championships (1999, 2003 San Antonio Spurs), one regular-season MVP (1995), one scoring champion (1994), one Defensive Player of the Year. Robinson played the first five years of his career without a championship partner; once Tim Duncan arrived (1997), he transitioned from primary star to complementary giant and won twice.

## Career Highlights

- 2x NBA Champion (1999, 2003 San Antonio Spurs); 1x MVP (1995); 1x DPOY (1992)
- His 71-point Game 82 (1994) to secure the scoring title over Shaquille O'Neal is the most calculated individual performance in NBA history
- 4x All-Defensive; 10x All-Star
- His Naval commitment (two-year service after being drafted) delayed his NBA career until 26 — he was still excellent into his late 30s
- His partnership with Tim Duncan (their twin towers) is the most effective two-center lineup in modern NBA history

## Why They Matter

Robinson's career is the Navy's greatest contribution to professional basketball. His willingness to subordinate his individual role when Duncan arrived — going from team star to complementary giant — enabled two championships that would not have been possible with Robinson as the primary option. That sacrifice is the story.
