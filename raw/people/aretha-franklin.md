# Aretha Franklin

**Born:** 1942, Memphis TN
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer, pianist
**Active:** 1960–2017

## Summary

The Queen of Soul. The greatest female vocalist in the history of American music. Franklin's combination of gospel training, jazz piano, and raw emotional power produced a voice that has never been replicated. 'Respect' (1967) is the anthem that named a generation's demand for dignity.

## Key Works / Highlights

- 'Respect' (1967) — the defining civil rights and women's rights anthem in popular music
- I Never Loved a Man the Way I Love You (1967) — #67 on Rolling Stone's 500 Greatest Albums; her debut Atlantic Records album was made in one session
- 18 Grammy Awards; Presidential Medal of Freedom; first female Rock and Roll Hall of Fame inductee
- Her combination of gospel, R&B, jazz, and pop was more stylistically complete than any other vocalist of her era
- Her performance of 'Amazing Grace' (live gospel album, 1972) is the best gospel recording ever made

## Why They Matter

Franklin's voice is the most complete instrument in American music — it can carry the emotional weight of the entire civil rights movement, the entire women's rights movement, and the entire gospel tradition simultaneously. She was not just a great singer; she was a force of nature wearing a crown.
