# David Sklansky

**Born:** 1947, Teaneck NJ
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Poker theorist, author
**Active:** 1974–present

## Summary

The most influential poker theorist in the history of the game. The Theory of Poker (1987) is the foundational text of modern poker strategy — his mathematical analysis of pot odds, implied odds, and hand ranges transformed poker from a game of intuition into a game with a theoretical framework. Every modern poker solver can trace its conceptual lineage to Sklansky.

## Key Works / Highlights

- The Theory of Poker (1987) — the foundational text of modern poker strategy
- Poker, Gambling and Life (multiple editions) — extended his framework to all forms of gambling and decision theory
- His Sklansky Theorem of Poker ('Every time you play a hand differently from how you would have played it if you could see all your opponents' cards, they gain; and every time you play your hand the same way you would have played it if you could see all their cards, you gain') is the most precise statement of game-theoretic poker
- WSOP bracelet winner and multiple high-stakes poker career
- His work with Mason Malmuth (2+2 Publishing) built the internet poker education infrastructure

## Why They Matter

Sklansky is to poker what Newton is to physics — the person who provided the mathematical framework that made the game theoretically tractable. Every GTO solver, every poker training program, and every professional player's reasoning process uses language and concepts Sklansky formalized.
