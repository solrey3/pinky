# The King

**Real Name:** Harley Race
**Born:** 1943, Quitman MO
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Active:** 1960–2004
**Personal Rank:** #40 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#40. The greatest NWA World Heavyweight Champion in history — an eight-time champion who held the belt during the territory era when the NWA title meant something globally. Race was the legitimate hardest man in wrestling in an era when wrestling was still semi-legitimized. His in-ring style was punishing and credible. He trained and mentored dozens of wrestlers including Goldberg, Brock Lesnar, and Batista.

## Career Highlights

- 8x NWA World Heavyweight Champion
- His reign as NWA champion (1977–1983, eight total reigns) is the most credible world title run in territory era wrestling
- He legitimately had his nose broken dozens of times and kept working — his toughness set a standard
- His feud with Dusty Rhodes across the NWA territory system built both men's legends
- His training school (in Missouri) produced some of the most successful wrestlers of the 2000s

## Why They Matter

Race is a pre-Hogan wrestling star whose legacy requires historical context to appreciate — he was the definitive world champion in an era when a world champion traveled 300 days a year defending the belt across multiple territories.
