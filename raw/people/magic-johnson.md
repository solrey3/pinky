# Earvin Johnson

**Born:** 1959, Lansing MI
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PG
**Active:** 1979–1996
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 3 (#7)

## Summary

The greatest playmaking point guard in NBA history and the anchor of the Showtime Lakers dynasty. Five championships, three Finals MVPs, three regular-season MVPs. Magic's ability to play all five positions — he started at center in the 1980 Finals as a rookie and scored 42 points with 15 rebounds and 7 assists — was unprecedented.

## Career Highlights

- 5x NBA Champion; 3x Finals MVP; 3x regular-season MVP
- His 1980 Finals Game 6 (42 pts, 15 reb, 7 ast as a rookie center) is the greatest individual Finals performance of the post-Russell era
- 12x All-Star; point guard who led the league in assists nine times
- His Magic/Bird rivalry elevated the NBA from a secondary sports property to a national television event
- His 1991 HIV announcement and subsequent return (1992 Olympics, 1992–93 season, 1996) handled a public health crisis with dignity that was transformative

## Why They Matter

Magic Johnson and Larry Bird jointly rescued the NBA from declining television ratings and attendance in the late 1970s. Their college rivalry (1979 NCAA title game) turned into the defining professional rivalry of the 1980s. Magic's physical gifts — 6'9" with point guard vision — were never seen before him and have never been fully replicated.
