# Jerry West

**Born:** 1938, Chelyan WV
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PG
**Active:** 1960–1974
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 4 (#14)

## Summary

The logo. Jerry West's silhouette is literally the NBA's brand identity. One championship (1972 Lakers), 14x All-Star, the 1969 Finals MVP — despite his team losing to the Celtics (the only Finals MVP ever given to a player on the losing team). His clutch performance gave rise to the Mr. Clutch nickname before 'clutch' was a standard basketball descriptor.

## Career Highlights

- 1x NBA Champion (1972); 14x All-Star; 1969 Finals MVP (losing team — only time in history)
- His 46.3 ppg average in the 1965 NBA Playoffs is the highest scoring playoff average in history
- His shooting mechanics and off-the-dribble game influenced every guard who followed him
- General Manager career: built the Showtime Lakers and the 2010s Golden State Warriors dynasty from the front office
- The NBA logo silhouette is based on a photo of West — he objected to the honor but the NBA kept it

## Why They Matter

West played his entire career losing to Bill Russell's Celtics, then built two dynasties as a front office executive. His career is the example of basketball excellence without proportionate championship reward — which makes his single title feel like a validation rather than a ceiling.
