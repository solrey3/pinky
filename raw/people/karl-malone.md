# Karl Malone

**Born:** 1963, Summerfield LA
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** PF
**Active:** 1985–2004
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 4 (#20)

## Summary

The second-leading scorer in NBA history at the time of his retirement (36,928 points). The Mailman's career is the definitive example of excellence without a championship — two trips to the Finals (1997, 1998) with Stockton's Jazz, both losses to Jordan's Bulls. His partnership with John Stockton is the most productive pick-and-roll relationship in NBA history.

## Career Highlights

- 2x regular-season MVP (1997, 1999); 14x All-Star; 11x All-NBA First Team
- Second all-time scorer when he retired (36,928 career points)
- His pick-and-roll with John Stockton is the most statistically productive partnership in NBA history
- His 1997 and 1998 playoff performances against the Bulls were the closest anyone came to beating Jordan in the Finals
- The Mailman always delivered — except in the two most important games of his career, in Game 6 of back-to-back Finals

## Why They Matter

Malone is wrestling with an unfair historical problem: he was excellent for 19 years and lost to Jordan twice. That defines him. His legacy deserves to be discussed on its own terms — a power forward who scored more points than anyone in NBA history except Jordan and LeBron — but the Jordan comparison is unavoidable.
