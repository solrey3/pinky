# Bruce Springsteen

**Born:** 1949, Long Branch NJ
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer, songwriter, guitarist
**Active:** 1972–present

## Summary

The Boss. The greatest live rock performer in history and the artist who most completely embodied blue-collar American music. Born to Run (1975) is the album that established his mythology. His E Street Band is the most democratic rock ensemble in history — every member is a character, every character has a story. Nebraska (1982) is his most essential artistic statement.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Born to Run (1975) is the greatest single statement of rock 'n' roll mythology as aspiration
- Nebraska (1982) — a solo acoustic album recorded on a 4-track cassette machine; his most musically stripped and emotionally complete work
- His E Street Band is the template for the rock 'big band' format — horn section, keyboards, multiple guitars functioning as a unit
- His live shows (3-4 hours, no setlist repeat) are the most committed performances in rock history
- 20 Grammy Awards, Academy Award (Philadelphia), Presidential Medal of Freedom

## Why They Matter

Springsteen's body of work is the American working class rendered in rock mythology. His live shows are acts of community rather than performance — the audience is not a crowd but a congregation. His longevity (50+ years of consistent artistic engagement) is the other argument his career makes.
