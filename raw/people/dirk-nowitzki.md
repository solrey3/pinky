# Dirk Nowitzki

**Born:** 1978, Würzburg, Germany
**Nationality:** German
**Position:** PF
**Active:** 1998–2019
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 4 (#18)

## Summary

The greatest international player in NBA history and the inventor of the one-legged fadeaway as a primary offensive weapon. One championship (2011 Dallas Mavericks, defeating LeBron's Heat), one Finals MVP, one regular-season MVP. His 21 seasons with one franchise (Dallas) and his career arc — from German kid to greatest power forward outside of Duncan — is one of the NBA's most complete stories.

## Career Highlights

- 1x NBA Champion (2011); 1x Finals MVP; 1x regular-season MVP (2007)
- 21 seasons with one franchise — the most loyal franchise player in NBA history for a superstar
- 6th all-time NBA scorer (31,560 career points)
- His one-legged fadeaway is the most difficult shot to guard in the modern era
- His 2011 Finals performance (26.0 ppg on 47% shooting including the elimination games) is the best Finals performance of the 2010s

## Why They Matter

Dirk proved that the NBA could accommodate a 7-foot power forward who played the game from the outside in rather than the inside out. His championship in 2011 — particularly his performance against the Heat, who were widely expected to win easily — is one of the greatest individual championship story arcs in any sport.
