# Miles Davis

**Born:** 1926, Alton IL
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Trumpeter, bandleader
**Active:** 1944–1991

## Summary

The most influential figure in jazz history in terms of genre redefinition. Davis pioneered or co-pioneered cool jazz (Birth of the Cool, 1949–1950), hard bop (Cookin', 1956), modal jazz (Kind of Blue, 1959), jazz fusion (Bitches Brew, 1970), and electric jazz. Each transition arrived at the exact moment when the previous style had reached its creative ceiling.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Kind of Blue (1959) — the best-selling and most critically acclaimed jazz album in history
- Bitches Brew (1970) — launched jazz fusion and influenced rock as much as jazz
- Birth of the Cool (1949–1950) — invented the West Coast cool jazz aesthetic
- His First Great Quintet (Coltrane, Evans, Chambers, Cobb) and Second Great Quintet (Shorter, Hancock, Carter, Williams) are the two most important small group configurations in jazz history
- His silence (1975–1981) and return showed that artistic hiatuses can be as defining as output

## Why They Matter

Davis's career is the argument that genius is not the ability to do one thing perfectly but the ability to know when a form is exhausted and move to the next one. His five genre redefinitions across 45 years is the most varied creative arc in any musician's history.
