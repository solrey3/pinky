# Ben Mezrich

**Born:** 1969, Philadelphia PA
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Author, journalist
**Active:** 1995–present

## Summary

The author of Bringing Down the House (2003) — the true story of the MIT blackjack card counting team — and The Accidental Billionaires (2009, adapted as The Social Network). Mezrich's specialty is narrative non-fiction that dramatizes real events with such novelistic technique that the distinction blurs deliberately.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Bringing Down the House (2003) — the most commercially successful gambling narrative in book history; adapted as 21 (2008)
- The Accidental Billionaires (2009) — adapted as The Social Network; the most commercially successful technology biography
- Bitcoin Billionaires (2019) — the most accessible narrative of cryptocurrency's early history
- His technique: 'narrative non-fiction' with composite characters and reconstructed dialogue that has generated ongoing debate about journalism ethics
- His books are the primary popular documentation of several of the most significant technology and finance stories of the last 25 years

## Why They Matter

Mezrich's contribution is accessibility — he makes complex financial and technical stories legible to general audiences through narrative technique. The ethical debate about his methods (composite characters, scenes he wasn't present for) is the honest companion to his commercial success.
