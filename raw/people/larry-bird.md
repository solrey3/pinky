# Larry Bird

**Born:** 1956, French Lick IN
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** SF
**Active:** 1979–1992
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 3 (#8)

## Summary

The greatest fundamentally grounded scorer in NBA history. Bird's court vision, passing ability, and competitive trash talk combined with shooting precision that made him uniquely threatening. Three championships, three Finals MVPs, three consecutive regular-season MVPs (1984–1986). The rivalry with Magic Johnson is the foundational narrative of the NBA's modern era.

## Career Highlights

- 3x NBA Champion; 3x Finals MVP; 3x regular-season MVP (consecutive, 1984–1986)
- 12x All-Star; 10x All-NBA First Team
- His 3-point contest wins (1986–1988) are the definitive performance moments in NBA All-Star history
- His passing ability from the forward position — seeing plays before they developed — influenced offensive philosophy
- His back injuries (1988–1992) limited his final years and ended a career that was on pace for deeper GOAT conversation

## Why They Matter

Bird's legacy is built on doing difficult things simply — making the right pass, taking the right shot, saying the right thing to make his opponent miserable. His trash talk is the stuff of legend: he reportedly told opponents which hand he would score on, then did it. His career is the best argument for basketball IQ as the most decisive skill.
