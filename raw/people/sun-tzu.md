# Sun Tzu

**Born:** c.544–496 BC, Qi (China)
**Nationality:** Chinese
**Profession:** Military strategist, philosopher
**Active:** c.500 BC

## Summary

The Art of War is the most widely read strategy text in history, applied to business, sports, politics, and military conflict for 2,500 years. Sun Tzu's core insight — that superior intelligence about terrain, enemy, and self is more decisive than superior force — is the foundational principle of asymmetric warfare and modern competitive strategy.

## Key Works / Highlights

- The Art of War (c.500 BC) — the most widely translated and applied strategy text in human history
- His theory of intelligence-gathering (knowing the enemy and knowing yourself) predates modern strategic analysis by 2,500 years
- His principle of winning without fighting (supreme excellence) anticipates economic warfare, deterrence theory, and business strategy
- Applied by every major military strategist from Napoleon to Mao
- Applied in business strategy, sports coaching, and negotiation in the contemporary world with documented results

## Why They Matter

Sun Tzu's continued relevance is the argument that the principles of strategy are structural rather than historical — the same psychological and informational principles apply whether the battlefield is Qi province in 500 BC or a modern corporate negotiation. The Art of War is the most durable how-to book in history.
