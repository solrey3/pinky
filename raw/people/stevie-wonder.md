# Stevie Wonder

**Born:** 1950, Saginaw MI
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer, multi-instrumentalist, producer
**Active:** 1961–present

## Summary

The most musically complete artist in popular music — he played every instrument, wrote every song, and produced every album across his classical period. His five-album run (Music of My Mind, Talking Book, Innervisions, Fulfillingness' First Finale, Songs in the Key of Life, 1972–1976) is the most sustained period of creative excellence in pop music history.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Songs in the Key of Life (1976) — the most ambitious double album in pop music, Grammy for Album of the Year
- Innervisions (1973) — #16 personal ranking; the peak of his political and musical synthesis
- His five-album run (1972–1976) produced 50+ Grammy nominations and five consecutive Album of the Year wins
- 25 Grammy Awards — the most by any solo artist in his era
- Born blind, became a recording prodigy at 12 — his Motown 'Little Stevie Wonder' run is pop's most precocious debut

## Why They Matter

Wonder's five-album peak is the argument for what happens when a musical genius is given complete creative control. Every instrument, every arrangement, every lyric across five albums — no collaborator filter. The result is the most concentrated genius output in popular music history.
