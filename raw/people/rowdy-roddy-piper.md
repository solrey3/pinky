# Rowdy Roddy Piper

**Real Name:** Roderick Toombs
**Born:** 1954, Saskatoon SK
**Nationality:** Canadian
**Profession:** Professional wrestler / actor
**Active:** 1973–2015
**Personal Rank:** #43 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#43. The greatest talker in wrestling history. Piper's Pit was the most important interview segment in wrestling — it gave the WWF a character-driven talk show that advanced every major storyline. His feud with Hulk Hogan drove the first WrestleMania. His match with Adrian Adonis at WM3 and his fight with Mr. T at WM1 are defining moments of wrestling's mainstream arrival.

## Career Highlights

- WWF IC Champion; WCW World Champion (1990s)
- Piper's Pit (1984–1987) is the most influential interview segment in wrestling history
- His feud with Hulk Hogan built the first WrestleMania into a mainstream event
- His babyface turn (1986) and feud with Bob Orton is one of the best face turns of the era
- They Live (1988) — 'I have come here to chew bubble gum and kick ass' — is his best acting work

## Why They Matter

Piper could talk anyone into a match. His ability to be funny, threatening, and sympathetic in the same promo was unique. His legacy is in the language of wrestling — the celebrity involvement, the mainstream spectacle — that his WWF rivalry with Hogan created.
