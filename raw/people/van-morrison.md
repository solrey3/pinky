# Van Morrison

**Born:** 1945, Belfast, N. Ireland
**Nationality:** Northern Irish
**Profession:** Singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist
**Active:** 1960–present

## Summary

Astral Weeks (1968) is one of the three greatest albums in rock music history — a stream-of-consciousness folk jazz record that has no precedent and no successor. Morrison's voice — searching, mystical, demanding — is the most distinctive in rock. His Celtic soul synthesis (Moondance, 1970) defined a genre. His prolific output has been inconsistent but the peaks are extraordinary.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Astral Weeks (1968) — the most formally experimental album in the rock canon; a 47-minute sustained improvisation around myth and memory
- Moondance (1970) — the most fully realized Celtic soul record; still the entry point for new listeners
- His voice ('Them' era through the 1970s) is the most recognizable in white rock music
- 'Brown Eyed Girl' (1967) is the most universally known 2:45 single in rock music
- His live performances in the early 1970s (Fillmore shows) are the most sustained concert performances in the genre

## Why They Matter

Morrison's contribution is formal rather than commercial — Astral Weeks is the album that most completely expanded what rock music was allowed to be in 1968. His later career is a genuine mixed bag, but his peak (1968–1974) is unassailable.
