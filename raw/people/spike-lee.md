# Spike Lee

**Born:** 1957, Brooklyn NY
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Film director, writer, producer, actor
**Active:** 1982–present

## Summary

The most politically essential American director of his generation. Do the Right Thing (1989) is the most important American film of the 1980s — a film that predicted the Los Angeles riots that would occur three years later and still generates debate about whether Mookie did the right thing. His filmography is the most consistent engagement with race in American cinema.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Do the Right Thing (1989) — the most politically essential American film of the decade; in personal film rankings
- Malcolm X (1992) — the most complete biographical film about an American political figure
- 25th Hour (2002) — the most emotionally precise response to 9/11 in American film
- BlacKkKlansman (2018) — the most commercially successful film of his later career; Oscar for Adapted Screenplay
- His relationship with Nike (Jordan commercials) made him the first director to be publicly identified with a brand

## Why They Matter

Lee's contribution is subject matter: he put Black American experience at the center of mainstream cinema at a time when Hollywood's commercial calculus pointed in the opposite direction. Do the Right Thing's Academy Award snub (nominated for nothing significant in 1989, the year Driving Miss Daisy won) is its own historical document.
