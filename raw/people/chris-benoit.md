# The Crippler

**Real Name:** Chris Benoit
**Born:** 1967, Montreal QC
**Nationality:** Canadian
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Active:** 1985–2007
**Personal Rank:** #24 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#24. A deeply complicated entry. On ring work alone, Benoit was the best pure technical performer in WWE history. His match with Angle at Royal Rumble 2003 is a 20-minute clinic. His WrestleMania XX win against Triple H and HBK was a legitimate career-defining moment. He was murdered his wife and son in 2007 and took his own life. WWE has effectively erased him from its history.

## Career Highlights

- WWE World Heavyweight Champion (WM XX)
- The Benoit/Guerrero WM XX celebration is one of wrestling's most joyful moments — made unwatchable by subsequent events
- His technical match with Angle at RR 2003 is the best short technical match in WWE history
- The Crippler Crossface is technically flawless — the most anatomically correct submission hold in WWE
- His brain showed the worst CTE damage ever recorded in a person of his age at autopsy

## Why They Matter

Impossible to rank without complication. His place in this list reflects the ring work only, with full acknowledgment that the human being failed catastrophically. His case is the most visible example of CTE damage in wrestling history.
