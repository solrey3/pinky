# Laozi

**Born:** c.601–531 BC, Chu (China)
**Nationality:** Chinese
**Profession:** Philosopher, poet
**Active:** c.500 BC

## Summary

The author (attributed) of the Tao Te Ching — the foundational text of Taoism and the second most-translated book in world history after the Bible. Laozi's philosophy of wu wei (non-action), the Tao (the Way), and the virtue of yielding over force is the most complete counter-argument to every ambitious, striving tradition in human history.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Tao Te Ching (c.500 BC) — the second most translated book in history; 81 short chapters that constitute the most economical philosophical system ever written
- Wu wei (non-action/non-interference) is the most radical political philosophy in human history — governance through restraint
- His influence on Chinese Buddhism, Confucianism, and Japanese Zen is total
- The Tao Te Ching has been translated into English more than 250 times — more than any other work
- His influence on American counterculture (1960s) and on business philosophy (effortless action) continues

## Why They Matter

The Tao Te Ching is 5,000 Chinese characters. Those 5,000 characters have generated more commentary, translation, and debate than any comparable work. Laozi's argument — that the universe has a natural order that works best when human intervention is minimal — is the permanent counter-narrative to every ideology of progress and control.
