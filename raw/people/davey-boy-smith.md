# Davey Boy Smith

**Real Name:** David Smith
**Born:** 1962, Golborne, England
**Nationality:** British
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Active:** 1978–2002
**Personal Rank:** #78 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#78. The greatest British wrestler in American wrestling history. The British Bulldog's physique and power were genuine — he was one of the most legitimately strong men in wrestling. His SummerSlam 1992 match with Bret Hart at Wembley Stadium (80,000 fans) is the largest wrestling crowd in UK history and one of the best pure matches in WWE history.

## Career Highlights

- Multiple WWF/WCW Tag and IC/US Champion
- His SummerSlam 1992 match with Bret Hart at Wembley (80,000) is the best WWE European match ever produced
- His British Bulldogs tag team with Dynamite Kid is the greatest pure tag team match workers in wrestling history
- His physicality — the hanging powerslam — is the most powerful-looking finish in wrestling
- His death in 2002 at 39 from heart failure reflects the physical cost of wrestling's pharmaceutical culture

## Why They Matter

Smith's career is inseparable from the British wrestling tradition he represented — and from the physical damage he sustained in representing it.
