# Billy Joel

**Born:** 1949, The Bronx NY
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer, songwriter, pianist
**Active:** 1971–present

## Summary

The greatest commercial-popular singer-songwriter in American music. The Stranger (1977) is his masterpiece — a perfectly balanced collection of character studies, narrative songs, and melodic invention that sold 10 million copies and produced four Top 10 singles. His piano-based songwriting connects Broadway tradition to rock 'n' roll.

## Key Works / Highlights

- The Stranger (1977) — #42 personal ranking; his most complete album and one of the best-selling albums in American music history
- Piano Man (1973) — his signature and the most recognizable piano song in American popular music
- 52nd Street (1978) — first Album of the Year Grammy for a rock/pop album
- His storytelling approach — character studies and narrative songs in rock — influenced every singer-songwriter after him
- His Piano Man residency at Madison Square Garden (2014–2024) is the most successful single-venue residency in music history

## Why They Matter

Joel's commercial success obscures his craft — he is one of the best melody writers in American music, and The Stranger's balance of rock, pop, jazz, and theatrical storytelling has rarely been equaled. His MSG residency is the most economical demonstration that his audience never left.
