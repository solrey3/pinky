# Frank Sinatra

**Born:** 1915, Hoboken NJ
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer, actor
**Active:** 1935–1995

## Summary

The Voice. The defining male vocal stylist in American popular music. Sinatra's Capitol Records period (1953–1961) with arranger Nelson Riddle produced the album format as an art form — cohesive collections of songs organized around a mood rather than a hit single. Chairman of the Board of the Rat Pack. His phrasing influenced every singer who followed him.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Capitol Records era (1953–1961) with Nelson Riddle defined the concept album for popular music
- 'Songs for Swingin' Lovers!' (1956) is the foundational document of the swing vocal album
- His Nelson Riddle arrangements produced the template for orchestral pop that dominated the 1950s–1960s
- His film career (From Here to Eternity, Oscar win 1954) demonstrated the crossover potential of a pop singer
- The Rat Pack (Sinatra, Dean Martin, Sammy Davis Jr.) is popular music's most commercially powerful social formation

## Why They Matter

Sinatra's influence operates at two levels: vocal technique (phrasing, breathing, pitch control) and format innovation (the album as a unified mood statement). Every male pop and jazz vocalist from 1945 to the present has been measured against him. His cultural reach — mob connections, Rat Pack, Democratic politics — extended far beyond music.
