# Niccolò Machiavelli

**Born:** 1469, Florence, Italy
**Nationality:** Italian
**Profession:** Political philosopher, historian, playwright
**Active:** 1498–1527

## Summary

The first modern political scientist. Machiavelli's The Prince (1513, published 1532) is the founding document of realpolitik — the analysis of power as it actually operates rather than as moral theory prescribes. His observation that the ends justify the means is the most misquoted and most precisely accurate statement about political power ever written.

## Key Works / Highlights

- The Prince (1513) — the most influential political philosophy work in history, read by every political leader since its publication
- Discourses on Livy — his more complete work; shows The Prince as tactical rather than his full political philosophy
- Secretary of the Florentine Republic (1498–1512) — his political experience preceded his theory
- His historical plays (Mandragola) show that his literary ambitions extended beyond political writing
- The term 'Machiavellian' entered every European language as a synonym for politically ruthless pragmatism

## Why They Matter

Machiavelli's contribution is the separation of political ethics from personal ethics — the acknowledgment that governing requires different moral calculations than personal conduct. Whether this is a liberation from hypocrisy or a legitimization of brutality is the argument his work has generated for 500 years.
