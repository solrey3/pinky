# Scottie Pippen

**Born:** 1965, Hamburg AR
**Nationality:** American
**Position:** SF
**Active:** 1987–2004
**Personal NBA Tier:** Tier 5 (#27)

## Summary

The greatest second-option player in NBA history and the best defensive forward of his era. Six championships with Jordan's Bulls, three times All-Defensive First Team, an Olympic Dream Team cornerstone. His 1994 playoff series (when Jordan was retired) shows he could carry a team to the ECF without the greatest player ever as his partner.

## Career Highlights

- 6x NBA Champion; 7x All-Defensive First Team; 8x All-Star
- His 1994 playoff run without Jordan (Jordan was retired) shows he was a legitimate second-tier MVP candidate
- His defense on Magic Johnson, Larry Bird, and Clyde Drexler was the most versatile defensive assignments of any wing
- Olympic Dream Team (1992) — arguably the most dominant team in any sport ever assembled
- His career earnings relative to his contribution is the NBA's most significant underpayment story

## Why They Matter

Pippen's legacy is complicated by his relationship to Jordan — 'I played with Michael Jordan, not for Michael Jordan' is his most important sentence. His individual contributions to the Bulls dynasties are underrated precisely because Jordan overshadows everything. His 1994 run shows what he was capable of independently.
