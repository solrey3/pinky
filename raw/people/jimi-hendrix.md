# Jimi Hendrix

**Born:** 1942, Seattle WA
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Guitarist, singer, songwriter
**Active:** 1963–1970

## Summary

The greatest rock guitarist in history. Hendrix's combination of technical innovation (feedback, whammy bar, left-hand tuning) and emotional expressiveness on the guitar has never been equaled. His three studio albums (Are You Experienced, Axis: Bold As Love, Electric Ladyland) across three years represent the most concentrated creative burst in rock guitar history. Died at 27.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Are You Experienced (1967) — #21 personal ranking; the debut that established him as rock's supreme guitarist
- Electric Ladyland (1968) — his most expansive and experimental album
- His Woodstock performance (1969, 'The Star-Spangled Banner') is the most politically resonant guitar performance in American music history
- He played right-handed guitars restrung for left-hand play — creating a unique physical relationship with the instrument that affected his tone and technique
- Died at 27 (1970) — his death marks the moment at which rock guitar's most advanced practitioner was removed from the timeline

## Why They Matter

Hendrix is the hardest argument to make for historical context mattering — his recordings still sound more advanced than almost any rock guitar playing produced since his death. The technology has improved; the imagination has not kept pace. He invented sound effects that took decades to identify, let alone replicate.
