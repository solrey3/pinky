# Miguel de Cervantes

**Born:** 1547, Alcalá de Henares, Spain
**Nationality:** Spanish
**Profession:** Novelist, poet, playwright
**Active:** 1570–1616

## Summary

Author of Don Quixote (1605, 1615) — the first modern novel and the most influential work in Western literature outside Shakespeare. Cervantes's invention of the self-aware fictional character (Quixote knows he is a character in previous stories), the unreliable narrator, and the meta-fictional novel predates every 20th-century literary innovation.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Don Quixote (1605, 1615) — the first modern novel; the most widely read novel in the Spanish language
- The Quixotic archetype — idealism meeting reality and choosing to persist — entered every European language as a concept
- His invention of meta-fiction (characters aware of being fictional) preceded postmodernism by 350 years
- He wrote Don Quixote while imprisoned for debt — the physical circumstances of its composition are part of its biographical mythology
- The word 'quixotic' entered English as the most complete single-word description of romantic idealism meeting practical reality

## Why They Matter

Cervantes wrote the book that invented novels — in the sense that everything the novel form does, it does because Cervantes showed it was possible. The unreliable narrator, the character aware of his fictionality, the parodic relationship to earlier genres — these are the techniques of 21st century fiction, first deployed in 1605.
