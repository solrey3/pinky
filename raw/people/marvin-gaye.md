# Marvin Gaye

**Born:** 1939, Washington DC
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Singer, songwriter, producer
**Active:** 1960–1984

## Summary

The artist who proved that R&B could be a vehicle for social commentary. 'What's Going On' (1971) is the most politically significant album in soul music. His voice — capable of simultaneously being tender, urgent, and heartbroken — is one of the most emotionally complete instruments in popular music. Murdered by his father in 1984.

## Key Works / Highlights

- What's Going On (1971) — the most politically engaged soul album ever made; his insistence on releasing it over Motown's objections is the greatest act of artistic courage in the label's history
- Let's Get It On (1973) — the most sonically sensual album in popular music
- His Motown period (1961–1971) produced some of the greatest singles in R&B history
- His 'Sexual Healing' (1982) post-comeback single is the greatest late-career return in R&B history
- He was shot and killed by his father on April 1, 1984 — one day before his 45th birthday

## Why They Matter

Gaye's death is music's most terrible loss because it came at the moment of what appeared to be his final creative renaissance. His influence — on Prince, D'Angelo, Maxwell, Frank Ocean — is total. What's Going On remains the argument that popular music can carry political weight without losing beauty.
