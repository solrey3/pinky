# Kevin Von Erich

**Real Name:** Kevin Von Erich
**Born:** 1957, Muenster TX
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Professional wrestler
**Active:** 1976–1995
**Personal Rank:** #67 (Pro Wrestling All-Time)

## Summary

#67. The last standing Von Erich, the family that defined Texas wrestling in the 1980s. The Von Erich family — Fritz, David, Kerry, Mike, Chris, and Kevin — is wrestling's most tragic family story. Their World Class Championship Wrestling (WCCW) is the origin of modern regional wrestling as a legitimate alternative to the national WWF model. Kevin is the only brother who survived.

## Career Highlights

- WCCW World Champion; AWA Tag Team Champion
- The Von Erich Iron Claw is wrestling's most famous family-specific finishing hold — every Von Erich used it
- His family's WCCW territory (1982–1988) is the most emotionally intense wrestling product ever produced
- His matches with Ric Flair in WCCW are the best Texas territory wrestling of the 1980s
- His documentary/'The Iron Claw' film (2023) brought the family's story to a new generation

## Why They Matter

The Von Erich family story is wrestling's most profound tragedy. Kevin's survival and his continued connection to wrestling is both the hope and the grief of the story simultaneously.
