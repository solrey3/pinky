# Mel Brooks

**Born:** 1926, Brooklyn NY
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Director, writer, actor, producer
**Active:** 1949–present

## Summary

The greatest satirical comedy director in film history. Blazing Saddles and Spaceballs represent two of the most precisely constructed genre parodies ever made — films that demolish their targets while understanding and loving them. His ability to make audiences laugh at things they shouldn't laugh at, then examine why they're laughing, is the most ethical deployment of comedy as a tool.

## Key Works / Highlights

- Blazing Saddles (1974) — the most politically courageous American comedy film ever made; in personal film rankings
- The Producers (1967) — the original satirical takedown of Nazi kitsch; launched his directorial career
- Spaceballs (1987) — #1 in personal film rankings; the most precisely targeted Star Wars parody
- Young Frankenstein (1974) — the most affectionate genre parody in film history
- History of the World, Part I (1981) — his most irreverent film; 2000 Years is the best closing number in comedy history

## Why They Matter

Brooks's contribution is the proof that satire requires genuine understanding of what it satirizes — Blazing Saddles works because Brooks loved the Western genre before he dismantled it. His courage in keeping the N-word in Blazing Saddles (against studio pressure) is the canonical example of artistic integrity defeating corporate risk-aversion.
